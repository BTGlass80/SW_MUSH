# SW_MUSH — Secrets of Tatooine Extraction
## Version 1.0 — June 3, 2026
## Source: WTC11839 — *Star Wars Roleplaying Game: Secrets of Tatooine*, Wizards of the Coast (2001, d20 System, 96 pages, scanned)
## Purpose: Capture the era-correct Tatooine **setting/lore** that the WEG sources never wrote — geography, environmental hazards, settlement history, Mos Espa, and the Hutt-era economy — to complement the GG7 Mos Eisley geography. Lore-only; all d20 stats discarded.

---

## 0. What this is, and the three mandates that govern it

This is a **design reference**, not a transcription. Everything below is paraphrased and reorganized
for the SW_MUSH Tatooine build (the painted Dune Sea / wilderness substrate, the world data, the
economy and security layers, and a possible second city). Three project rules govern it:

- **WotC d20 → setting/lore ONLY.** Per the extraction roadmap: WEG (D6) books share our ruleset and
  yield content *and* stats; **WotC (d20) books are mechanically incompatible — extract
  setting/lore/locations/NPC concepts only, and re-stat to D6 (R&E) from scratch or not at all.**
  Every stat block, DC, Defense, Challenge Code, VP/WP, Fortitude/Reflex/Will save, feat, and
  d20 skill modifier in this book is **discarded**. SoT's value is purely that it holds the
  pre-Imperial / Clone-Wars-adjacent Tatooine setting WEG never published.
- **Era translation (Clone Wars, ~20 BBY).** The book is organized into three eras: **Rise of the
  Empire** (the prequel / late-Old-Republic period — *our* era, the keepable slice), **Rebellion**
  (Empire, stormtroopers, prefects, TIEs), and **New Jedi Order** (Yuuzhan Vong, post-ROTJ). Only
  the Old Republic / pre-Imperial / **Hutt-dominated** slice survives. Everything Rebellion-era and
  NJO-era *setting* is off-era and dropped wholesale (B3 era-cleanness: no
  Empire/Imperial/Rebel/TIE/X-wing/stormtrooper reference reaches a seeded string).
- **Q1 canon-character policy.** SoT names Jabba, Gardulla, Watto, Shmi, and Anakin (plus the entire
  Rebellion/NJO cast). **None appears as a named NPC.** Marquee *locations* are pinned the GG7 way
  (`JABBA'S PALACE` is already a pinned location); the figures are reduced to archetypes or referenced
  off-screen. **Shmi and Anakin Skywalker are simply gone from Tatooine by ~20 BBY** — pure
  absence-framing, not seeded at all.

**Omitted on purpose:**
- The **d20 Podracing subsystem** (pp. 63–74) — a ruleset, discarded. Podracing survives only as
  *lore/landmark* (§5).
- The adventure **"Between Sand and Sky"** (pp. 75–95) — a d20 module. Flagged as a future
  **quest-seed for the director library** (§8C), not extracted here.
- All **Rebellion-era and NJO-era** city/NPC sections (pp. ~39–62).
- The d20 tactical **floorplans** (Docking Bay 94, Moisture Farm, Mos Eisley Cantina, Jabba's Throne
  Room) — tactical maps, not setting.

**De-dup (do not redo):**
- **Mos Eisley** is owned by the **GG7 extraction** — geography, docking, customs, fees, the
  underground, the cantina, the establishments gazetteer. SoT's Mos Eisley material is also
  Rebellion-era-framed. Not re-extracted.
- **Marquee Tatooine fauna** — **bantha, dewback, krayt dragon, ronto, eopie, rancor** — already have
  D6 stats in the **R&E core appendix (WEG40120)** per the CotG dedup. The **worrt** plus desert
  drop-ins (glim worm, hitcher crab, magus, stalker lizard, wrix, voroos, shredder bat) are in the
  **Creatures of the Galaxy extraction**. SoT adds ecology flavor + a short D6-stat gap list (§10).

## 1. Planetary frame — additive detail (beyond the GG7 stat line / world data)

The GG7 doc already carries the Tatooine stat line (terrestrial, hot, dry, desert, 23-hour day,
304-day year, ~80,000 pop, Humans + Jawas + Tusken Raiders). SoT adds usable detail on top:

- **The Tatoo system.** A yellow binary, **Tatoo I** and **Tatoo II**. Tatooine shares the system with
  two gas giants, **Ohann** and **Adriana**; **Adriana's ice rings are the source of most of
  Tatooine's imported water** (a direct hook for the water-import economy — §6). The system's many
  moons (Ohann has three, Adriana four) host a few mining bases and serve mainly as **smuggler /
  pirate hideouts**.
- **Day/night vocabulary (a ready MUSH-clock flavor layer).** Two sunrises and two sunsets give
  Tatooine its own time idiom: **First Dawn** (very early) and **Second Dawn** (a reasonable hour);
  **First Twilight** (law-abiding folk safely home) and **Second Twilight** (after which the streets
  turn dangerous); **High Noon** (both suns overhead — a stifling midday lull when sensible beings
  shelter underground). Drop straight into the in-game clock's period strings.
- **"Largely unmapped."** Only the temperate-zone cluster of spaceports + moisture farms is mapped;
  storms erase landmarks everywhere else, and "no one really cares" what's past the cluster. This is
  the lore justification for our design: a small **navigable cluster** ringed by **trackless waste**.

## 2. Geography & terrain regions (the core unique contribution → wilderness / painted maps)

The "Civilized Areas" planetary map (p.6) anchors the cluster. Era-clean landmarks worth seeding as
world-map features: **Fort Tusken** (ruin, far north), **Mos Espa** + the **Mos Espa Grand Arena** +
**Xelric Draw** + **Mospic High Range**, **Mos Taike**, a **Jawa mountain fortress**, then south
across the **Jundland Wastes** to **Bestine, Arnthout, Motesta, Mos Eisley**, and (far south)
**Anchorhead, Tosche Station, Wayfar**, the **B'omarr Monastery (Jabba's Palace)**, and a **krayt
dragon skeleton**. *(Drop "Ben Kenobi's home" — off-era / Q1.)*

Regions, with their build use:

- **Jundland Wastes** — rocky badlands between the Western and Northern Dune Seas, incorporating the
  western end of the Great Mesra Plateau. No settlements; **Tusken + Jawa territory**; home to
  dewbacks, krayt dragons, and womp-rat packs. Lore beat worth keeping: *if news stops coming from the
  outlying moisture farms, Tuskens are pushing in.* → a wilderness-region template (rocky/canyon,
  hostile fauna, Tusken-gated encounters).
- **Dune Sea — Western & Northern** — a dried ancient sea: trackless rolling dunes, regularly
  reconfigured by storms, **dotted with wrecked starships** (the Jawa salvage supply). Banthas, Sand
  People, and water prospectors wander it. → the existing Dune Sea substrate; "wrecked ships under the
  sand" is a turnkey salvage/POI hook.
- **Great Mesra Plateau** (spelled **Mesra** in text, **Mesa** on the map — note the variance) — the
  eastern highland; Bestine and Arnthout sit at its edge; survival is somewhat easier here than in the
  open Dune Sea.
- **Beggar's Canyon** (near Anchorhead) — winding gorges that make a swoop/skyhopper **race course**.
  Named features to seed as POIs: **Dead Man's Turn, the Bottleneck, Diablo Cut**, and the **Stone
  Needle** (a tall stone column with a prominent "eye"; *"threading the Needle" is the only fair
  shortcut*). Also the venue for **"womp runs"** — skyhopper hunts that pick off womp rats for points.
  → a ready racing/landmark sub-zone.
- **Great Pit of Carkoon** (Western Dune Sea) — the nesting pit of the **sarlacc**. Treat the sarlacc
  as a **fixed terrain hazard / landmark**, not a roving encounter: a near-buried colossal pit-predator
  that drags in passers-by and is used by criminals for disposal. → a unique fixed-hazard POI (D6 stats
  from scratch if ever made interactive; the d20 block is discarded).
- **Funnel-flower cliffs & hidden condensation pools** — the water-bearing plant the Jawas and Tuskens
  tap, and the rare natural pools (usually jealously guarded by Sand People). → survival-resource flavor
  feeding §3 and the water economy.

## 3. Environmental hazards (→ Guide 24 hazards / wilderness weather)

Phenomenology only — the d20 DCs and damage are discarded; re-tune to our hazard/weather system.

- **Heat & thirst.** Lethal at High Noon (both suns at zenith); relief from shade, proper clothing, or
  nightfall. Water is the planet's scarcest resource and the spine of its economy.
- **Sandstorms.** Block vision; cripple ranged energy and missile fire; punish atmospheric flight;
  choke the unprotected (breath masks immune, a scarf buys time); bury everything in dust afterward.
- **Gravel storms.** Worse sandstorms — stones in the wind. Even shorter weapon ranges, heavier flight
  penalties, deeper burial.
- **Sandwhirls (the standout).** Sudden tornado-like sand funnels with **no warning**, internal winds
  *ten times* fiercer than a sandstorm's, short-lived but violent: they can **drag a light starship
  half a kilometer**, smash buildings, and fling banthas. Pull radius ~200 m, and you can't simply walk
  out — each round the funnel wanders randomly. → a dramatic, era-clean wilderness *and* space weather
  set-piece. (The adventure itself uses a sandwhirl to destroy pursuing starfighters — a ready
  "use the terrain" beat for the director library.)
- **Finding water.** Funnel flowers (cliff faces), hidden condensation pools (Sand-People-guarded),
  and vaporators. Difficulty scales by region (open Dune Sea hardest; Plateau/Wastes easier). → ties
  moisture-farming to the water-as-currency economy (§6).

## 4. Settlement history & lore (era-appropriate slice → `world_lore` / background)

Trimmed to the timeline **up to ~20 BBY**; everything at/after the Imperial presence (-11) and the
NJO is dropped.

- **Deep history.** Ancient orbital battles seeded the sands with wreckage (the origin of Jawa
  salvage); early miners left when the ore proved substandard; a Republic Survey rediscovered the world
  and declared it "nominally habitable," opening it to colonization.
- **The B'omarr monks** — the first true settlers: a contemplative order that walled off an old
  monastery on the edge of the Western Dune Sea (later seized by a succession of crime lords; this is
  canonically the structure that becomes Jabba's Palace). Their **"enlightenment"** rite (a living
  brain transplanted into a nutrient jar to contemplate forever) is excellent eerie flavor for the
  monastery POI. → `B'OMARR MONASTERY` / `JABBA'S PALACE` location lore (figure off-screen).
- **The *Dowager Queen*** — the crash-landed colony ship (downed in a sandstorm) whose survivors
  founded **Bestine**; its stripped hull later becomes the centerpiece of "old" Mos Eisley. → a
  settlement-origin story + the *Dowager-Queen-wreck* landmark (GG7 already references the old core;
  this supplies the origin).
- **First contacts.** With the **Jawas** (a search for a lost droid → a mutual nonaggression / water /
  salvage pact) and with the **Sand People** (the walled settlement at **Fort Tusken** overrun and
  razed — the slaughter that *named* the Tusken Raiders in the settlers' tongue). Fort Tusken stays a
  ruin. → the settler-vs-Tusken backstory that underpins the whole frontier-danger framing.
- **Founding sequence.** Bestine → Fort Tusken (razed) → **Anchorhead** (reinhabited around an old deep
  well; first to build a spaceport, prospered on ore export) → **Mos Espa** and **Mos Eisley**
  (Rodian-refugee-founded spaceports). The **mining boom → magnetic-ore collapse → economic
  depression** is the pivot that draws the Hutts in.
- **The Hutts arrive.** They restored utilities, resumed water shipments from Adriana, and bought up
  the moisture-farmers' debts — then converted defaulters into **slaves** ("indentured servitude,"
  implanted tracking devices, the Mos Espa Slave Quarter). This sets the era-defining status quo:
  **the Hutt cartel runs Tatooine; slavery is legal under Hutt law; crime is "just another business."**
  → the canonical ~20 BBY power structure (fully consistent with the GG7 authority model).
- **Return of the sandcrawlers.** When the miners left, the Jawas repurposed the abandoned mining
  sandcrawlers; their once-a-year **swap-meet** in a Dune Sea basin trades scrap, shares navigation
  data, and arranges marriages. → Jawa-culture lore (complements GG7's Jawa Traders and the CotG worrt
  flavor).

## 5. Mos Espa — the era-correct capital (the biggest unique build target)

Mos Eisley is GG7's. **Mos Espa is new** and is the era-appropriate capital — a strong candidate for a
future **second painted city** (the OR/Hutt-era counterpart to the seedy Mos Eisley).

- **Character.** Tatooine's largest Old-Republic-era city, "winding through the **Xelric Draw** like a
  dusty serpent." The main thoroughfare, **Mos Espa Way**, runs the city's full length and carries the
  tourist/commerce traffic. Districts: an **entertainment district** (mundane + illicit) and a
  **merchant district** (endless stalls; starship fuel) front the Way; **residential** sits behind;
  the **Slave Quarter** is on the outskirts; **docking bays** are sprinkled throughout near parts-shops
  and cantinas. *(Map p.32 legend: docking bays "D", Mos Espa Way (1), Watto's junk shop (2), slave
  quarters (3), Jabba's city house (4), Gardulla's palace (5).)*
- **Power structure (OR era).** Five Hutt clans share Tatooine — **Besadii, Nasirii, Faljozic,
  Jahibakti, Desilijic** — each jockeying for position; the cartel runs licensed police and courts
  "with a Huttese flavor." → a multi-clan cartel layer that feeds **Orgs/Factions (G10)** and
  **Territory (G11)**: rival Hutt kajidics as factions contesting Mos Espa.
- **Marquee POIs** (locations pinned the GG7 way; figures → archetype, off-screen):
  - **Mos Espa Grand Arena** — the 100,000-seat podracing arena several km out, where the Xelric Draw
    meets the Northern Dune Sea; home of the annual **Boonta Eve Classic** (>50% pilot mortality).
    Podracing is **past its zenith by ~20 BBY** but still runs. → a landmark + spectacle/gambling hook.
    (The d20 podracing *rules* are discarded; podracing-as-a-feature would be a from-scratch D6 build.)
  - **Watto's Junk Shop** — a **Toydarian-run** junkyard and parts shop: abrasive salesmanship, "what's
    not on the shelf is somewhere in the yard," chance-cube wagers on the price. → a vehicle/parts
    vendor archetype (Toydarian junk-dealer; figure anonymized/off-screen).
  - **Gardulla's Palace** — a fortress-like Hutt estate off Mos Espa Way (the Besadii seat), heavy with
    bodyguards and slaves, rumored to mount roof shield-generators. → a rival-Hutt power center +
    high-security estate POI (matriarch → "a rival Hutt matriarch / the Besadii presence").
  - **Jabba's City House** — the Desilijic city residence (smaller than Gardulla's palace but
    ostentatious). → a cartel city-house POI (the desert estate `JABBA'S PALACE` is already pinned via
    GG7).
  - **The Slave Quarter** — an adobe sprawl on the Way's outskirts (cooling units, a daily water ration,
    the implanted-tracker horror). → a grim-flavor district; slavery is the era's defining injustice,
    handled with the project's existing tone discipline.

## 6. The Hutt economy & law (→ Economy G06, Security G04 — era-correct)

- **Currency.** In the OR/Hutt era Tatooine runs on Hutt **truguts** (plus peggats and wupiupi), not
  Republic dataries. Republic credits are converted at the customs office with a punitive **25% Hutt
  exchange fee**, and most businesses accept only Hutt money. This holds *"until the arrival of the
  Empire,"* so it's era-correct for us. Exchange ratios for a money-changer mechanic:

  | | Datary | Peggat | Trugut | Wupiupi |
  |---|---|---|---|---|
  | **Datary** | 1.0 | 0.025 | 0.1 | 1.6 |
  | **Peggat** | 40.0 | 1.0 | 4.0 | 64.0 |
  | **Trugut** | 10.0 | 0.25 | 1.0 | 16.0 |
  | **Wupiupi** | 0.625 | 0.0156 | 0.0625 | 1.0 |

  → a Hutt-currency / exchange-fee layer GG7 doesn't have; pairs with the existing credit log.
- **Docking / fees / customs / licenses.** The Mos Eisley fee model is GG7's; SoT confirms the same
  picture (bribeable customs, smuggling-permissive, fees aimed at milking offworlders) and adds the
  **merchant's-license / temporary-permit** sink (license sold by customs; temp permit renews monthly).
  → defer the fee model to GG7; lift the license sink + trugut framing if useful.
- **Law & order (OR/Hutt era — era-clean).** Police are Hutt-employed and corrupt: their job is to
  *minimize damage to commerce*, they run on bribes, and they respond three-at-a-time (two to arrest,
  one to watch their backs). **No city walls or gates**, open carry is normal, and crime is "just
  another business." Crime classes are **Huttese**: Class One (conspiracy against the Hutts) →
  execution / asset seizure / enslavement of relatives, scaling down to fines. → confirms and enriches
  the GG7 cartel-authority model (Mos Espa as a cartel-run mixed/low-security zone); the Huttese
  infraction ladder is ready law-board flavor.
- **Goods pricing.** Legal goods run ~5–20% cheaper than the Core; illegal goods 50–100% dearer but
  sold openly — and openly-sold means *low risk*, which is the whole appeal. → a contraband-economy
  pricing note.

## 7. Cultures & vehicles (era-clean; complements GG7 / CotG)

- **Tusken Raiders.** ~2-meter desert warriors wrapped head-to-toe in bandages, breath-masked,
  speaking a tongue only other Tuskens fully grasp; most of their "tech" is stolen from settlers
  (slugthrowers especially prized). Fierce hatred of offworlders; the Fort-Tusken slaughter is their
  founding legend. Primarily desert-survival specialists. → the wilderness antagonist culture
  (Jundland / Dune Sea Tusken-gated encounters; **D6 stats from scratch** — the d20 block is discarded).
- **Jawas.** Short hooded scavengers with glowing eyes; sandcrawler clans; a "scrounge-and-trade"
  economy and a deserved reputation for refurbished-junk that fails after the crawler leaves; a complex
  scent-laced language with a simplified **Jawa Trade** pidgin for outsiders; the annual Dune Sea
  swap-meet. → the desert trader culture (vendor/quest hooks; complements GG7's Jawa Traders).
- **Transportation (vehicle *types* only; d20 stats discarded).** **Sail barges** (slow luxury repulsor
  hulks that cross sand without sinking), **cargo skiffs** (labor-droid-run low transports),
  **skyhoppers** (T-16; the Beggar's-Canyon womp-run craft, popular with racers and smugglers),
  **landspeeders** (the SoroSuub V-35 family), **swoops**, and **podracers**. → vehicle archetypes for
  the vehicle/vendor lane (re-stat to D6 from the WEG gear catalog where stats are wanted).

## 8. Decision points / forks (GG7-consistent defaults — adjust if any are wrong)

I made the following calls so this pass didn't stall on them; each is easy to change:

- **(A) Mos Espa scope.** Written as a **future second painted-city build target** (its unique value vs.
  GG7). If you only want Mos Espa as world-data lore for now, the §5 build-hook detail trims down to a
  short gazetteer.
- **(B) Q1 marquee locations.** Defaulted to the **GG7 precedent** — pin `MOS ESPA GRAND ARENA`,
  `WATTO'S JUNK SHOP`, `GARDULLA'S PALACE`, `JABBA'S CITY HOUSE` as *locations*, figures
  off-screen/archetype. Alternative: fully genericize ("the Grand Arena," "a Toydarian's junkyard,"
  "the Besadii estate").
- **(C) "Between Sand and Sky" adventure.** Flagged as a **quest-seed for the director pass** (roadmap
  step 6), **not** extracted here. It era-translates cleanly — the book's own "Rise of the Empire"
  note recasts the antagonist (Ranon Djelkh) as a **Gardulla underling** with **Act I in Mos Espa** and
  no Empire; the spine (mistaken-identity smuggling deal → stranded in the Dune Sea → Jawa salvage
  haggling → desperate Tuskens → a buried alien predator / the cologne-as-lure trick → the crime lord's
  ambush) is a self-contained Tatooine quest. **Say the word and I'll do the structure extraction now.**
- **(D) Podracing.** Captured as **lore/landmark** only; the d20 podracing subsystem is discarded. A
  podracing *feature* would be a separate from-scratch D6 design.

## 11. Prune note

With this extraction in hand, the **Secrets of Tatooine scan can be removed from the project**. The
era-clean setting — geography, hazards (incl. sandwhirls), settlement history, **Mos Espa**, the Hutt
**trugut** economy, Jawa/Tusken culture, vehicle types, and the day/night vocabulary — is preserved
here. The discarded material (all d20 mechanics, the Rebellion and NJO eras, the floorplans, the
podracing subsystem, and the d20 adventure) is intentionally not carried. If you later want an art
plate, or the **"Between Sand and Sky"** structure for the director quest library, the book is
re-obtainable and the adventure is flagged in §8C.

**Roadmap position:** sequencing step 5 (CW-era WotC, lore-only). **Next in this lane:** *Geonosis and
the Outer Rim Worlds* (WotC, setting-only) — the CW-era Outer Rim gazetteer for expansion beyond
Tatooine.
