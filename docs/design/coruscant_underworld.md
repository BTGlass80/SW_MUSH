## Layered structure (top-to-bottom)

Coruscant is an ecumenopolis built up over 100,000+ years. The lowest fifty levels were already buried by 100,000 BBY; total levels number in the thousands. For SW_MUSH zone-design purposes, the underworld is best modeled as roughly five tiers:

| Tier | Approx. Level Range | Character |
|---|---|---|
| **Surface / Skyscraper Tier** | 5000+ | Wealthy residences, spires, Senate District, Jedi Temple. Out of underworld scope. |
| **Upper Underworld** | ~500–2000 | Refined seediness — high-end gambling clubs, opera houses, restaurants where senators slum. **Uscru District (upper)** lives here; Galaxies Opera House. Crime exists but is sanitized. |
| **Mid Underworld** | ~100–500 | The classic Clone Wars underworld backdrop. Cantinas, brothels, nightclubs, gambling dens. **Uscru Entertainment District (lower)** lives here; the Outlander Club, the Snapping Septoid, the POTU. Black Sun and other crime syndicates operate. Coruscant Security Force underworld police patrol but cannot fully control. |
| **Deep Underworld** | ~5–100 | Last inhabitable levels for normal beings. Crime rate beyond government control; some neighborhoods sealed off behind blast doors. **Level 1313** lives here — a dungeon level filled with shattered conduits, fungus, and gangs. Devolved or feral humanoid populations begin appearing. |
| **Sub-Habitable / Surface** | 0–5 | Original ground level. Mostly toxic, low-oxygen, irradiated. Mutant species (Cthon, Coruscani ogre, duracrete slugs, hive rats, shadow barnacles, conduit worms, taozin, granite slugs). Pre-Republic ruins and ancient infrastructure. |

For SW_MUSH zone abstraction, three named tiers are probably enough: **Upper Underworld** (Uscru-style), **Lower Underworld** (1313-style), **Surface / Ruins** (mutant fauna, Force-resonant ancient architecture).

## Named locations (Clone Wars era)

### Uscru Entertainment District
- The lower-levels portion of the larger Uscru District. Considered the outer layer between the surface jewel-city and the true underworld.
- High traffic, holographic advertising, large crowds — perfect cover for fugitives and illicit deals.
- The intersection of **Daring Way and Vos Gesal Street** is one of the highest-crime-rate locations on Coruscant.
- Site of Obi-Wan and Anakin's pursuit of bounty hunter Zam Wesell just before the Clone Wars.
- Ruled politically by **Mayor Acros-Krik** (Ongree, corrupt, Black Sun puppet — see NPCs below).

### Outlander Club
- Popular gambling/nightclub in the Uscru Entertainment District.
- Central bar plus betting cutouts for sports across the galaxy (podracing, grav-ball).
- Originally upmarket; under the **Baath brothers** it expanded into a hub for criminal networks.
- Death-stick dealers operate openly in the surrounding alleys.
- Attack of the Clones canon: Zam Wesell flees here after her failed assassination attempt; Jango Fett kills her in the alley behind the club to prevent her testifying.

### Galaxies Opera House (upper-tier reference, near underworld border)
- Elegant opera theater in the *upper* levels of Uscru District. Two large arching speeder entrances.
- Owned by **Romeo Treblanc**.
- Site of Palpatine's "Tragedy of Darth Plagueis the Wise" speech to Anakin during a performance of the Mon Calamari opera *Squid Lake*.
- Useful as an "elite tier" location bordering the seedier zones below — perfect for Jedi-investigation hooks where a Padawan tracks a target from the opera box down into the entertainment district.

### The POTU ("Periphery of the Uscru" / "Peril of the Uscru")
- Low-tier district on the edge of the Uscru Entertainment District.
- Turf gangs, serial killers, thieves. Coruscant Security Force has minimal presence — security cameras are stolen for parts.
- Accessible via the Deep Core Mag-Lev Line.
- Senators or senatorial aides are sometimes found here for thrill-seeking, illegal deals, or proscribed substances. The **Shimmersilk** restaurant operated here in 52 BBY.

### Level 1313
- Coruscant's most infamous criminal level. A dungeon-deep zone the surface-world has forgotten about.
- Shattered conduits, fungus on walls, criminal gangs.
- **Trace and Rafa Martez** lived here until they fell into debt with the loan shark **Pintu Son-El** and fled the planet with Ahsoka Tano (canon).
- Originally developed by George Lucas for a never-produced live-action TV series; introduced in *The Clone Wars* and the canceled *Star Wars: 1313* video game.
- A **massive ventilation shaft** offers access from upper levels to the heart of the underworld here.

### Sealed Communities (Deep Underworld)
- The lowest levels have crime rates so high that some communities have been sealed behind enormous, impenetrable blast doors by government order.
- These doors require passcode access; in canon, security captain **Quarsh Panaka** once forced a government door operator at gunpoint to access one such community while rescuing Queen Padmé Amidala from kidnappers — passcode phrase: "*Coruscant has lovely sunsets.*"

## NPCs / Factions

### Coruscant Underworld Police (CUP)
- Special-forces division of the Coruscant Security Force.
- Patrol the lower levels of Galactic City; distinct from the sleek GU-series Guardian police droids that operate above.
- **Uniform:** heavy leather and metal armor, mechanical eye coverings, fully covered heads, "PD" in yellow Aurebesh on helmets and backs. Anonymity-by-uniform is intentional — locals are unsure if they're humanoid, droid, or other.
- **Reputation:** courageous, loyal, relentless, hardened, "burly." Memorable toolkit.
- **Ranks:** Police Sergeant, Police Lieutenant, Police Captain.
- Patrol on foot, in police speeders; during the Clone Wars they also use patrol transports.
- Notably appear in *The Clone Wars* episode "To Catch a Jedi" pursuing the framed Ahsoka Tano through 1312/1313.

### Black Sun
- Galaxy-wide crime syndicate; deep operations in the Coruscant underworld.
- Installed Acros-Krik as Uscru Entertainment District mayor via rigged election.
- Operates through allies and informants throughout the underworld; controls corrupt officials.

### Acros-Krik (Ongree male, Mayor of Uscru Entertainment District)
- Corrupt mayor; orange skin, yellow eyes.
- Installed by Black Sun as a political asset due to Uscru's proximity to the Senate District — gives Black Sun intelligence on the political cadre.
- Plays the role of kindly father-figure mayor in public; private associations with the Baath brothers and other criminals.
- Employs the **Nalle triplet** spies for monitoring duties.
- Patron of **Dex's Diner** in CoCo Town (frequents nerfsteak sandwiches by airbus).
- Survived the Wesell airspeeder crash in 22 BBY in his district.
- A useful "corrupt-official" NPC archetype — friendly face, deeply compromised.

### Devolved species in deep underworld
- **Cthon** — humanoid, decaying-corpse-like with loose flaps of flesh. Levels 1–5.
- **Coruscani ogre** — large mutant predator.
- **Corridor ghouls** — feral humanoids.
- **Granite slug, duracrete slug, hive rats, shadow barnacles, conduit worms, taozin** — vermin and mutant fauna; threats to sentient beings.

## Atmospheric notes for SW_MUSH writing

- **Light:** the underworld is in perpetual artificial illumination; no sunlight reaches the lower levels. Holograms and ad-boards provide most illumination in inhabited zones; the deeper you go, the dimmer.
- **Sound:** "constant sense of motion and unrest" — machinery serving the upper city runs through the lower levels.
- **Population:** war refugees, poor immigrants, criminals, fugitives, sealed-off communities. Many alien communities live below because they were displaced from the surface by Imperial human-supremacy policies (Empire era — useful as foreshadowing in late Clone Wars content).
- **Tone reference:** The Coruscant Nights novels (Reaves) are the canonical noir-tone reference. Atmospheric beats: the Outlander Club's neon ad-boards and gambling bustle masking underworld deals; sealed blast doors with mythical passcodes; police in featureless armor that locals can't read; Senators slumming in The POTU; ancient ruins beneath modern levels; mutant fauna scratching at the edges of habitable zones.
