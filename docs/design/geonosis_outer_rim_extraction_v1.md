## 0. What this is, and the mandates that govern it

This is a **design reference**, not a transcription. Everything below is paraphrased and reorganized for the SW_MUSH Geonosis/Kamino builds (world data, interior/wilderness tiers, the encounter lane, Force-resonant landmarks, the crime/economy and Director-quest libraries). Four project rules govern it:

- **WotC = d20 → setting only.** Every d20 stat in the source (DCs, feats, levels, Defense, VP/WP, Hardness, Break DC) is **discarded**. Lore, locations, factions, and NPC *concepts* are kept; gear and creatures are **re-statted to D6 from scratch** below and marked *(D6 stub — calibrate on the dev box against the existing weapon/creature tables).*
- **Era translation (Clone Wars, ~20 BBY).** This book is **already CW-era** — it is *the* gazetteer companion to the AOTC period — so translation is light. Only a few stray off-era/post-CW asides are stripped (see the cheat-sheet, §5). Dates given as "X years before the Battle of Yavin/Naboo" are recast to BBY framing. Geonosis is the planet where the Clone Wars *begin* (~22 BBY); Kamino is where the clone army is *grown*. For our timeline both are squarely in-era.
- **Q1 canon-character policy.** Canonical figures become **archetypes**, never named open-world NPCs. Geonosis canon → *Poggle the Lesser, Hadiss the Vaulted, Sun Fac.* Kamino canon → *Lama Su, Taun We, Ko Sai, Kina Ha, Spar / the ARC prototype;* plus *Jango Fett, Dooku/Tyranus, Sifo-Dyas, Obi-Wan, Quinlan Vos, Aayla Secura* as **historical backdrop only.** The book's **original** NPCs (*Typtus of the 33rd Egg, Acklay Chopper, Marmio Mio, Yubookoo, the Jedi Master Qalsneek*) are not canon film figures and **may** be used as named NPCs.
- **No-duplication discipline.** `creatures_of_the_galaxy_extraction_v1.md` already statted a separate Geonosis cave/rock set (Arqet, Draagax, Keejin, Preying Makthier, Thanu) and Kamino ocean set (Selligore, Onahk, Chiilak, Two-Headed Tortuce, **Svaper**, Zuxu, Tresher, Andoan Mineral-Fish). The 8 creatures below are **net-new** to those; *Svaper* (mentioned here as a cloned Kamino "variant") is cross-referenced, not re-statted.

# 1. GEONOSIS

## 1.1 Planet stat line

Terrestrial · Temperate · Type I (breathable) · **Light gravity (~0.9)** · Rock & desert terrain · 30-hour day · 256-day local year · Sapients: **Geonosians** (insectoid hive species) · Population ~100 billion (overwhelmingly drone) · Government: **ruling archduke + hive councils** · Major exports: **droids, high tech** · Major imports: raw materials · Function: bulk droid manufacture.

System (star **Ea**): Geriss (airless rock), **Geonosis** (rocky terrestrial, 4 moons), Corus (volcanic), Mabonte (toxic), Abyssissa (frozen). The planet sits **off the main hyperlanes, ringed by a perilous asteroid field** (debris from an ancient comet strike on its largest moon — a cataclysm that drove >99% of native species extinct).

**Setting framing (era-clean).** A hellish, radiation-scoured world where the rocks glow red; gloomy multi-week fogs ("longnights") blot the sun; water is scarce and arrives as an annual flood that drowns and destroys. The surface is owned by vicious carnivores; the natives can barely contain their contempt for off-worlders. The one thing that draws the galaxy here is **precision droid manufacturing in bulk**, run for centuries through a distribution alliance with **Baktoid Armor Workshop / the Techno Union** (conveniently beyond the reach of Republic legislation). Local super-battle-droid and droideka (Colicoid-designed) production feed the Separatist war machine; the **Battle of Geonosis (~22 BBY) opens the Clone Wars.**

## 1.2 The Geonosians — caste society (NPC behavior model)

A hive species; every individual serves a rigid function fixed at birth. Three castes:

- **Queen** — gigantic, immobile, hidden in the deepest catacomb; her sole role is laying egg-clusters. A hive can die with its queen; backup larvae take *years* to mature, and the lost drone output leaves a hive open to conquest.
- **Aristocrat** — only ~5% of the population but rules everything (including the queen). Winged; older ones grow drooping facial wattles. Arrogant, disdainful of drones, derisive of other species; fiercely competitive yet they almost never do their own violence — they have soldier drones and battle droids for that. **Off-worlders deal almost exclusively with aristocrats.**
- **Drone** — the nameless masses (aristocrats consider workers interchangeable). Biologically distinct **subcastes**: *service, labor, farmer, maker, overseer, pilot, soldier* (pilots are smarter than farmers; soldiers fly, labor drones are wingless). Drones honor the aristocracy on the calendar holiday **Meckgin** ("the virtues of industry"). Any drone may try to rise through **arena combat** — the most famous example seized a throne.

**Culture hooks.** *Petranaki* = the ancient art of gladiatorial combat (mythologized as the hive-overlord giants battling on the surface). *Phidnas* = farmed parasitic insects whose paste the maker drones use to grow the bone-like **spire-hives** (airy aristocrat halls above, kilometers of drone catacombs below, the queen at the bottom). **Rising drone discontent** — droids now do the work drones once did — is the live tension: it boiled over at the Golbah hive, and the aristocrats answered a drone uprising by **proton-bombing the entire hive**.
→ *Use:* drop-in Geonosian dialogue/behavior model; "drone unrest vs. aristocrat rule" is ready-made internal-faction quest fuel.

## 1.3 Hives & factions (the local politics = inter-hive war)

- **Stalgasin Hive** — the dominant hive; seat of the archduke; owns the most advanced droidworks and controls the arena.
- **Gehenbar** — Stalgasin's primary rival, led by **Typtus of the 33rd Egg**, openly plotting Stalgasin's destruction.
- **Golbah** — destroyed rebel hive; now **Golbah's Pit** (see below).
Rival hives wage **vast underground tunnel wars** (soldier drones, sonic cannons). This rivalry is the engine for Geonosis faction content.

## 1.4 Locations (mappable POIs)

- **Stalgasin Hive & Droid Factory** — the foundry fills a rectangular cavern **nearly a kilometer deep**; an assault on the senses (sparks, steam, grease-and-metal air), with an **ore-smelting superlaser** at the far end and **zero safety** (drones die daily). Off-worlders see only the tower conference rooms and maybe a glimpse of the floor; catacombs and the queen's chamber are Geonosian-only.
- **E'Y-Akh Desert** — low, featureless dunes that make up most of Stalgasin's territory, near the arena, ending at the **N'G'Zi badlands**. Subterranean aquifers overflow in the **annual flood**; before it, Geonosians herd **merdeths** into the desert to drown them (the receding water leaves glittering merdeth shells).
- **Marmio Mio's Freighter** — a wrecked Corellian Action IV transport half-buried in the E'Y-Akh, ringed by six colossal merdeth shells; home to a survivalist hermit (see NPCs). On ballast tanks during the flood.
- **Golbah's Pit** — a poisoned, km-wide crater bubbling with black muck: the former Golbah spire-hive (it built Hailfire droids for the InterGalactic Banking Clan) after the aristocrats proton-bombed its drone revolt. A chemical-payload spill leached into the Ebon Sea → **mutant acklays**.
- **The Ebon Sea** — a rare permanent (stagnant, scum-covered) body of water; territory of escaped **acklays** and **mutant acklays** that lurk beneath the surface film; gigantic **hydra worms** in the mud.
- **Execution / Petranaki Arena** — Stalgasin-controlled open-air amphitheater; generations of gladiatorial *petranaki* (drones vs. gladiators, battle droids, or Geonosian massiffs) **and** executions (the condemned eaten by arena monsters — a tradition merged into the games).
- **Gladiator Barracks** *(beneath the arena — INTERIOR MAP, 1 square = 2 m):* **Slave Barracks · Gladiator Barracks · Sparring Pit (center) · Beast Cage ×2 · Picadors' Chamber · Beastwardens' Chamber · Acklay Chopper's Chamber · Med Center · Armory · exit south "To Orray Stables."** Houses arena staff, gladiator-slaves, hopefuls; the petranaki armory; creature cages for special training.
→ *Use:* the Barracks is a turnkey **interior-tier room layout**; the arena is a combat/quest hub; E'Y-Akh + Ebon Sea + Golbah's Pit are wilderness POIs with a built-in **flood mechanic** and an apex-predator zone.

## 1.5 NPC archetypes & named NPCs

- **The Ruling Archduke** *(canon: Poggle the Lesser → archetype).* An ambitious ex-low-caste drone who seized the throne with off-world backing, rode an acklay into the audience chamber to kill his predecessor, and executes all rivals in the arena. Keep off-screen / location-only.
- **The Chief Negotiator** *(canon: Sun Fac → archetype).* The archduke's lieutenant and hive's face to outsiders — brokers corporate deals, **hires freelancers for hive-security jobs**, gates access to the archduke. **This is your Geonosis quest-giver archetype.** (Rival hives resent that he also speaks for the lesser hives — a built-in grudge.)
- **Typtus of the 33rd Egg** *(original — named NPC OK).* Young Gehenbar aristocrat reviving the cult of "Typtus the First," **mustering a strike against Stalgasin**. Aids anyone who helps him, crushes anyone in the way → a faction-conflict patron.
- **Acklay Chopper** *(original — named NPC OK).* Aged, scarred, **limping** drone who earned a name by beheading the acklay that crippled him; now the arena's **fight-manager / slavemaster** — runs the barracks, trains slaves, and personally leads prisoner work-parties to the Ebon Sea to **capture live acklays**. Antagonist / handler.
- **Marmio Mio** *(original — named NPC OK).* A Human "stranded" trader, apparently driven mad, living in her wrecked freighter; **actually a multi-hive spy** with a hidden speeder bike and a half-dozen probe droids. Knows the desert (merdeth movements, groundwater). Information-broker with divided loyalties.
- **Yubookoo** *(original — named NPC OK).* A Wookiee **gladiator-slave** (16 arena kills with a butcher's scimitar) who fights to survive and would join a **breakout** if he found trustworthy allies. PC-rescue / ally hook.

## 1.6 Adventure hooks (deterministic quest seeds → Director library)

- **Jewel of the Desert** — a Hutt's caravel goes down in the E'Y-Akh; the Hutt hires the heroes to recover his "precious jewel" (which turns out to be a Twi'lek dancer) before the **flood + merdeths + a rival Hutt's mercenaries** close in. *(Ties Tatooine ↔ Geonosis ↔ Hutt Cartel; Marmio Mio is the local fixer.)*
- **Into the Catacombs** — flush surviving warriors from booby-trapped hive catacombs (covered pits, charges). Ends when a target leader/queen is taken. *(Per the book's own era-flex note: recast as bounty hunters pursuing a Geonosian fugitive.)*
- **Arena!** — captured (or sold by a Hutt-connected promoter) into the games; win freedom through victories, or break out. Acklay Chopper runs the barracks.
- **Bug Hunt** — slavemaster forces the party to **capture a live acklay** at the Ebon Sea; subdue it — or use the trip to escape.
- **Hive Alive** — Stalgasin vs. Gehenbar **tunnel war**; the heroes are hired by one side. *(CW recast: Republic-backed Typtus offers Stalgasin's fall in exchange for support; Separatists hold Stalgasin with droidekas + dwarf spider droids + clone-trooper mercs.)*

## 1.7 Gear — the petranaki array (D6 stubs)

Five traditional ritual melee weapons; arena fighters (not the condemned) pick one. *(D6 stubs — calibrate on the dev box.)*

- **Picador's Spear** — `Melee, reach (4 m; can't strike adjacent). Damage STR+1D+2. Electro-stun: on a hit, target rolls STR/stamina vs Moderate or is stunned 1 round. Set-vs-charge: double damage vs a charging foe if readied. Specialized availability.` (Also the beast-handlers' working pike.)
- **Confessor's Whip** — `Melee, reach (6 m; can't strike adjacent; doesn't threaten). Three barbed tails. Damage STR+1D. Nerve-toxin: wounded target rolls stamina vs Moderate or suffers −1D to DEX actions for 2D rounds.`
- **Petranaki Scimitar** — `Melee (sword). Damage STR+2D. Well-balanced — the popular arena pick.`
- **Caster's Net** — `Thrown (~4 m). No direct damage. On hit: entangled (−2D actions; may move only within the rope) and stamina vs Moderate or stunned 1D+1 rounds; if the thrower holds the rope, opposed STR to control. Escape = Difficult STR, full action.`
- **Beastwarden's Shield** — `Spiked bronze disc. Defensive: +1D melee parry / cover (not while caught flat-footed). May be hurled: thrown STR+1D, short range.`
- **Flitknot Speeder** *(vehicle stub)* — `Repulsorlift speeder bike. Crew 1 (+1 passenger). Maneuverability +2; top speed ~630 km/h; Body 2D. Weapons: none (strike variant: 1 light blaster cannon, 3D). Easily flown by non-Geonosians.` Used by perimeter scouts to track acklay/merdeth herds and fresh meteor impacts.

# 2. KAMINO

## 2.1 Planet stat line

Terrestrial / **ocean world** · Temperate · Type I (breathable) · Standard gravity · **Aquatic (saturated hydrosphere)** terrain · 27-hour day · 463-day local year · Sapients: **Kaminoans** (100%) · Population ~1 billion · Government: **ruling council** · Major exports: **clones, covert technology, military weapons & hardware** · Major imports: foodstuffs.

System: **13 worlds around an aging star; Kamino is the 5th**, the only inhabited one (one moon, Korasa, holds abandoned facilities). **~12 parsecs beyond the Rishi Maze — classified as Wild Space**, beyond Republic jurisdiction; **Kamino's records were scrubbed from astronav databases** (incl. the Jedi Archives). Receives almost no visitors; traffic is supply runs and clients with cloning business.
→ *Use:* slots straight into the **Wild Space space system** as a hidden / database-scrubbed destination.

## 2.2 The Great Flood & the stilt-cities (setting)

Kamino was once a largely terrestrial, glaciered world. Over **less than two centuries** its ice age ended, the caps melted, and the planet drowned under one churning ocean (the **"Great Flood"**), the atmosphere roiling into permanent storms. Survivors used **repulsorlift construction droids and gravitonic engineering** to build **hermetically sealed, domed stilt-cities** on anchor-columns driven into the seabed — sloped roofs shed the constant rain, **lightning rods** harness the storms. Architecture borrows the **Alderaanian "Oversea" stilt style** plus Kashyyyk influences. The drowned former cities are **submerged-ruin fields** (archaeological treasure).

## 2.3 The Kaminoans — cloner society (NPC behavior model)

One of the galaxy's most **homogeneous** societies: aloof but outwardly polite, **bred to fill specific roles**, intolerant of physical imperfection (aimed at off-worlders and the occasional flawed genetic result), almost never seen off-world, and dealing with outsiders **only in business terms.** Cloning is the lifeblood and primary revenue, but Kamino is **no longer self-sufficient** and **trades its tech for raw materials** (primary partner **Rothana**; also **Sullust** and **Iotra**, via quiet channels). Cloning-for-hire goes far beyond the Republic army order: **private security forces, task-specific laborers** (e.g., the cloned mining corps on **Subterrel**), custom creatures, and **covert weapons contracts**.
→ *Use:* drop-in Kaminoan dialogue/behavior model; **cloning-for-hire as an economy/quest engine**, including illicit/black-market jobs (see Ko Sai).

## 2.4 Locations (mappable POIs)

- **Tipoca City** — the capital and prestige cloning hub; a stilt-city stretching **100+ km along the western equator**; seat of **Kamino Space Control** and **Planetary Defense**. Runs 27 hrs × 463 days nonstop. **No public space and almost no residences** — workers commute from satellite cities; only the Prime Minister, military staff, and (rotating, ~3-month shifts) cloning scientists live in-city. Defenses: passive energy shields + **turbolaser emplacements** + comm towers; shields don't stop boarders, so combat-bred Kaminoan troopers garrison it.
- **Military Education Complex** *(within Tipoca)* — the largest clone-training center; reconfigurable troop-combat/conditioning facilities; the (restricted) **ARC-trooper training center**; and the **VABS — variant-atmosphere battle simulators**: kilometer-wide rooms in the city-dome bases whose advanced atmospherics + shifting repulsor floor-plates recreate **any** climate/terrain (snowfields to volcanic badlands).
- **Malia Preserve Units** — an isolatable **trio of peripheral platforms** holding a **genetic-template vault**: alpha/beta/secondary/tertiary templates of nearly **every being and creature the Kaminoans have cloned** (clone-trooper variants, aiwhas, svaper variants, bio-agents), **plus** exotic *uncloned* samples (Roon mudmen, sentient Filar-Nitzan, a mysterious organic snake-staff). Each unit can be sealed off in a breakout. → **vault / heist site.**
- **Derem City Ruins** *(submerged former capital, ~3 km down — INTERIOR/EXPLORATION MAP, 1 square = 250 m):* **Ministration Hall · Razoral Reefs · Ancient Transit Tubes ×2 · Sunspires · Ta'An Fissures · Chol Ka (Gardens) · glascretia-bedrock transit-tube labyrinths.** Predator-haunted but ripe for xenoarchaeology; a Kaminoan **pilgrimage** site (a quiet rebellion against being "bred for a purpose"). A Jedi **sea-crystal holocron** rests here (see hooks). → submerged exploration site.
- **Slici Canyon ruins** — an **abyssal trench ~10,000 m down** in the southern hemisphere; forgotten, sealed industrial-station chambers; the hidden base of the rogue seer **Kina Ha**. → deep-abyss **Force-resonant landmark.**
- *Security craft:* **KE-8 enforcer craft** handle dangerous incidents; **Observation Ships** (staffed by Kaminoan scientists) patrol the cities.
→ *Use:* Kamino interior tier (Tipoca / Military Complex / Malia vault) + ocean & abyss wilderness tier (Derem ruins, Slici trench, open seas).

## 2.5 NPC archetypes & named NPCs

- **The Prime Minister** *(canon: Lama Su → archetype).* The cloning council's premier; the apex authority Tipoca answers to.
- **The Ministerial Assistant** *(canon: Taun We → archetype).* Smooth, outwardly gracious, **duplicitous when needed**; the **clone-project coordinator** who manages off-world clients and contracts. **This is your Kamino quest-giver archetype.**
- **The Chief Cloner** *(canon: Ko Sai → archetype).* An **amoral master geneticist** who will take *any* off-world cloning/gene-mod job — even ones other Kaminoans frown on ("credits are credits; what clients do with the product is their business"); fascinated by exotic side-projects (toxic-saberdart variants, hybrid creatures). **Vanished after the attack on Kamino** (abducted? defected?). → illicit-cloning patron **and** a missing-person hook.
- **The Rogue Force-Kaminoan Seer** *(canon-obscure: Kina Ha → archetype).* A **long-lived, self-taught Force-sensitive Kaminoan** (from a deep-space-explorer genetic line) who fled "reeducation," trained under a rogue Jedi, **foresaw the clone-army dark-side vision**, and now keeps a hidden sentinel-vigil in the deep ruins with her own lightsaber. → Force-resonant hidden NPC; ties to Ossus / Jedi lore.
- **The Escaped Prototype Clone** *(canon-obscure: Spar → archetype).* A **"defective," free-thinking** first-batch ARC prototype who refused anonymity and **escaped Kamino** as a youth; now an Outer Rim drifter living on his training. → player-facing contact/ally; a ready **clone-PC backstory anchor.**
- **ARC Trooper** *(generic archetype).* Elite Fett-template clone with personality-dampening reduced — independent, creative, somewhat unpredictable; held in stasis between deployments. Use as a high-end clone NPC/ally/enemy.
- **Qalsneek the Bull** *(original historical name).* Ancient Jedi Master whose **holocron** lies in Derem City (lore object only).

## 2.6 Adventure hooks (deterministic quest seeds)

- **Deep Research (Derem City)** — an escort/xenoarchaeology **dive** to recover a Jedi **sea-crystal holocron** (Master Qalsneek's) from the drowned transit-tube warrens, warding off the predators that now den there; **Quarren tomb-raiders** compete for it. *(Force/Jedi-lore + exploration; chains from an Ossus seed.)*
- **The Sample They Won't Surrender** *(recast of "Not Suitable for Cloning," Vong frame stripped)* — a hidden patron hires the heroes to extract the **mysterious organic snake-staff** from the Malia Preserve; the Kaminoans refuse to relinquish it; **Kina Ha** takes a wary interest. *(Vault infiltration + Force mystery; recast the patron as a Sith agent, a collector, or a CIS bio-weapons division.)*
- **The Missing Cloner** — **Ko Sai** vanished after the Battle of Kamino. Track the master geneticist: abducted, defected, or running a black-market clone lab in the Outer Rim? *(Crime / black-market cloning.)*

## 2.7 Gear (D6 stub)

- **Pocker (Merr-Sonn Pocker LJ)** — `Spear-rifle / laser-sighted harpoon. Treat as a low-tech ranged weapon: Damage ~4D; short range (~8 m optimal). Laser sight: +1 to hit. Cheap, durable, fires improvised spears — no power cells/Tibanna needed.` A common first hunting weapon (aquatic quarry, near-surface prey) across backwater worlds.

## 2.8 Creatures (D6 stubs — net-new vs. CotG)

- **Aiwha** — `DEX 2D · PER 2D · STR 6D · swimming 8D · flight (breaches into flight) · buoyancy ballast (dive ↔ fly) · Slam STR+1D · Move 20 swim / 20 fly · 20–30 m wingspan · Creature/Mount.` The **iconic Kaminoan mount** (cloned from Naboo stock) — carries rider + cargo beneath the storms, breaches into flight near the destination; docile but defends its pod fiercely. → primary **Kamino mount** candidate.
- **Saberjowl** — `DEX 1D+2 · PER 2D · STR 9D (vehicle/walker-scale) · Bite (swallow whole) STR+3D · Foreclaws · breathe underwater · Move 18 swim · ~100 m.` The apex "kaiju" of the southern seas — a cloned colo claw fish big enough to **swallow a starfighter**; rare (the Kaminoans suppress its breeding) but lethal. → region-boss / launch-and-landing **hazard.**
- **Rollerfish** — `DEX 3D+1 · PER 2D · STR 2D · swimming 4D · Spines STR+2 · "Roll" attack (on a grab, wraps prey and skewers: +1D dmg) · breathe underwater · Move 12 swim.` Common 1-m surface predator around Tipoca; preys on the diminutive **iiaa** ("sea mouse"). → frequent surface nuisance/ambush.
- **Spike-Finned Sounder** — `DEX 3D · PER 2D+1 · STR 2D · Bite STR+1D · glide/leap (5 m+ out of water) · pressure-sense (food detection) · Move 14 swim / glide.` 2-m "flying" fish that **follows the saberjowl** and scavenges its scraps. → scavenger shoal; shallow-water harasser.
- *Referenced / cross-ref:* **Svaper** (cloned "variant" here) is already statted in `creatures_of_the_galaxy_extraction_v1.md` §2.2/§2.4 — reuse it. *Trivial:* **razoral** (Derem reefs), **iiaa** (prey), **sounders** (shoal fish).

# 4. How to use this in SW_MUSH (build hooks)

- **World data:** enrich the existing **Geonosis** and **Kamino** planet files with the era-clean setting framing in §1.1/§2.1–2.2 (no rules changes).

- **Wilderness tier:** Geonosis **E'Y-Akh** (annual-flood mechanic + merdeth herds + the Ebon Sea predator zone) and Kamino **seas/abyss** (aiwha mounts, the saberjowl region-boss, the Derem & Slici ruins).
- **Encounters (G24):** 8 net-new D6 creatures → wire as `npc_template` encounters; **aiwha** as a mount candidate; **saberjowl**/**merdeth** as region-boss hazards.
- **Force-resonant landmarks (G08 / landmarks lane):** Kina Ha's **Slici abyss**, the **Derem holocron**, and (teaser) Iego's angels.
- **Economy / crime:** **cloning-for-hire** and **illicit-clone** jobs (Kamino, via the Ko Sai archetype); the **Hutt-caravel** hook (Geonosis ↔ Tatooine ↔ Hutt Cartel).
- **Director quest library:** **8 deterministic seeds** above (5 Geonosis, 3 Kamino).
- **Faction tension:** Geonosian **inter-hive war** (Stalgasin vs. Gehenbar) + **drone unrest**.
