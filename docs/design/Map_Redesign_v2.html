What changed (1-4)

① Per-style room footprints. Docks gain a notched bay door, cantinas read as pills, civic structures gain a pediment, gates a chevron, landmarks a hex, hutt structures a doubled border. The style field already in the schema drives the silhouette; no data changes.

③ Street ribbon depth. Each ribbon is shoulder-gradient + core surface + lighter centerline, with intermittent foot-traffic hatching. Now reads as road.

④ HUD chrome. Chamfered bezel, etched 1px highlight, slow scan flicker, screen-glare. Compass dial enlarged to own its corner.

What changed (5)

The Tier-2 city's district polygon outlines are extracted at transition start, animated to the city's pin location on the planet sphere, and held as a glowing pulse for ~600 ms before fading. The pin underneath fades in from 0 → full opacity over the same window. The viewer sees the city become the pin instead of cutting between two unrelated visual systems.

Implementation cost is small: keep the Tier-2 SVG mounted, lerp its `viewBox` toward the planet `viewBox`, and animate a new <g class="city-trail"> with the polygons translated and shrunk. The planet sphere fades in below it. The polygons land on the city pin's coordinates and disappear.

03 · Same five fixes · Coruscant palette — proves the renderer is still theme-agnostic

CORUSCANT SENATE · TIER 1 · V2 (panel-grid substrate, metallic ribbons, cyan bezel)

Substrate swap

Tatooine's three substrate layers — dunes, grit, debris — become Coruscant's three: an etched panel grid, a fine scan hairline, and sparse spire-light dots. Same z-ordering, same render cost, different planet.

Street ribbons map cleanly: streetShoulder stays dark steel, streetSurface is brushed cyan-steel, the lighter centerline becomes a faint magnetic-track stripe. Foot-traffic hatching is replaced by occasional scan-pulse dashes.

HUD bezel uses the same chamfer geometry; the etched highlight shifts from amber to cyan. Compass needle stays amber so the orientation cue is consistent across planets.

Renderer changes: ① ~30 lines in map-view.jsx Rooms component (shape resolver). ② ~25 lines in Substrate (two new <pattern> defs + a debris layer). ③ ~20 lines in StreetRibbon (shoulder gradient + centerline). ④ pure CSS in tokens.js/artboards.jsx HudFrame — no SVG. ⑤ new TransitionLayer component, ~60 lines.
Data-model changes: none. The style field already in AreaGeometry drives the new room shapes. Substrate variants and ribbon details are render-time only.
