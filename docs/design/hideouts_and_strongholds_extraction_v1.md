# SW_MUSH — *Hideouts & Strongholds* Extraction
## Version 1.0 — June 2, 2026
## Source: WEG40111, *Star Wars: Hideouts & Strongholds* (Sterling Hershey, West End Games, 1996; D6)

---

## 0. What this is, and how it maps

This closes the **Hideouts and Strongholds** entry in the roadmap (Tier 1, §1) and **completes
step 1 of the crime-core sequence**: GG11 Criminal Organizations ✅ → Wretched Hive ✅ →
**Hideouts and Strongholds ✅**. It follows the GG7 loop: OCR → paraphrase → era-translate to
~20 BBY → reorganize as design notes → prune the scan.

The book is **WEG D6** (our ruleset), so we keep content *and* stats. It is OT/Imperial-era in
*setting*, era-agnostic in *structure* — most of it is environmental base archetypes that translate
untouched; two entries are explicitly faction-framed (Imperial Rapid Deployment Station, Rebel
Outpost) and a scatter of OT references (Hoth, the second Death Star, the ISB, Imperial walkers)
get recast. Q1 policy holds: no canonical figure becomes a named NPC; everything here is original
or reduced to an archetype.

**What the book actually contains, in priority order for us:**

1. **A library of ~20 stronghold archetypes** (§1) — the highest-value content. Each is a defended
   layout with an environment, a scale, signature defenses, notable rooms, a sample owner, and
   adventure hooks. This feeds **Player Cities (G12)** building/district themes, **Org-HQ** flavor
   variants, **Territory (G11)** objective sites, and — most directly — the **Director quest-template
   library** (§7).
2. **An NPC archetype roster** (§2) — base commanders, pirate warlords, gunrunners, water bosses,
   fugitives — reusable, era-translated, with D6 stats.
3. **A defensive-emplacement catalog with full D6 stats** (§3) — laser/ion/turbolaser batteries,
   missile and torpedo launchers, sensors, jammers, base and planetary shields.
4. **A base-construction & `(A) Engineering` model** (§4) — WEG-canonical costing for building and
   customizing a site.

**Read §6 before treating §3 or §4 as buildable.** Two locked decisions constrain them: Territory
Control §12.3 explicitly lists **"automated territory defense (turrets, mines, traps)" under
*What NOT to Build*** ("Guard NPCs are sufficient"), and Cities/Housing price bases with **abstract
tier costs**, not per-feature construction sums. So §3 and §4 land as *content, quest-objective
fodder, NPC-base stats, and optional future depth* — not drop-in systems. §1, §2, and §7 are the
parts that slot in as-is.

## 1. Stronghold archetype library (20 templates)

Each profile is compressed to what a builder/author actually uses. "Era" notes only flag a recast;
unmarked entries translate untouched. Page refs are to the book for re-checking a floorplan plate.

### 1A. Space / orbital

**Asteroid Base — Fortified** *(pp. 15–20; Type: Space Station)*
Hollowed asteroid pirate stronghold: hangar bays, carved-rock private quarters and barracks, captive
holding cells, smuggling hub. Signature defenses: turbolaser/ion emplacements on the rock face,
stun-grenade launchers in corridors. Sample owner: a Chevin pirate warlord running dirtside raids,
spice smuggling, and kidnapping out of a remote belt (era-agnostic — keep). **Feeds:** Hutt/pirate/BHG
space HQ, smuggling hub, space POI, "assault the pirate rock" quest.

**Asteroid Base — Primitive** *(pp. 21–23)*
Minimally developed asteroid hideout — a jagged central passage and a few carved chambers, low-tech,
cheap, deniable. **Feeds:** budget criminal hideout, fringe bolt-hole, the "we set up in a rock with
no money" tier of space hideout.

**Battle Station** *(pp. 24–31)*
A former orbital-defense platform repurposed as a free-port marketplace and haven — heavily armed,
with a docking level, administrative levels, escape pods, and a reputation for *no* pirate attacks
because the guns are still live. **Feeds:** neutral/black-market space hub, contested-zone landmark,
large org HQ. *(Strip the Death Star comparison.)*

**Space Platform** *(pp. 113–117)*
Small, functional deep-space platform: command / hangar-ops / living / "mission-specific" sections,
cargo stored in pods floating nearby. Sample is a clandestine rescue-station **front**. **Era:** recast
the front as an **Independent smuggler relay**, a **Hutt-cartel front**, or a **Republic black-site**
posing as a civilian rescue station. **Feeds:** space POI, supply-drop point, smuggling cover-identity
quest.

**Lunar Surface Station** *(pp. 84–90; Loronar modular)*
Catalog-modular prefab — owners pick components, scaling from a small research outpost to a
manufacturing facility; airless/low-g, so life-support is mandatory. **Feeds:** corporate/research
outpost, modular Org-HQ flavor, Kamino/Kuat-adjacent industrial content, "spacetrooper assault"
boarding quest (recast assault force to clone/droid commandos).

**Crashed Starship** *(pp. 32–38)*
A downed freighter turned squatter home/hideout — intact engineering section, a salvaged laser
cannon, debt-driven backstory (crew shot down over a Hutt loan default — era-agnostic, keep).
**Feeds:** wilderness POI, salvage/debt quest, derelict-as-home content.

### 1B. Wilderness / planetary

**Deep Sea Plant** *(pp. 39–45; Rendeel Type-07034)*
Modular deep-water processing plant — main / support / storage / habitation pods rated for crushing
pressure, plus a hidden-munitions cache. **Feeds:** aquatic-world POI (strong **Kamino** fit), industrial
site, "find the concealed arms cache underwater" quest.

**Desert Oasis** *(pp. 46–51)*
A ramshackle remote-settlement town built around the only water for miles — rudimentary or absent
government, a grey/black market for rare and illegal goods, and a **"Water Boss"** controlling the
well. **Feeds:** **Tatooine / dune-sea** settlement template, frontier-town POI, vaporator economy hook,
water-boss faction. (Strong starting-zone fit — pairs with the GG7 Mos Eisley geography.)

**Fortified Compound** *(pp. 52–60)*
A private-military-company compound on the Outer Rim — water-attaining gear, small-unit and speeder
training grounds, field artillery, a droid shop, barracks. **Feeds:** mercenary / BHG Org-HQ, Independent
merc faction site, training-ground content, "hire/audition the mercs" hook.

**Fortress** *(pp. 61–68)*
A self-sustaining citadel grown inseparable from a river town over centuries — armored ancient walls,
a walker barrier, an anti-infantry cannon, designed to be hard to capture or destroy. **Era:** recast
"Imperial walkers" the fort can defeat to CW walkers (AT-TE / OG-9 / etc.). **Feeds:** Org-HQ *Fortress*
tier flavor, defended-town POI, the "Attack!" siege quest.

**Glacier Base** *(pp. 69–74)*
A base carved into a glacier — laser-cutter expansion is trivial, the ice masks heat signatures (even
generators, if deep enough), and the misery of the location keeps neighbors away. **Feeds:** cold-world
POI, hidden base, sensor-masking content, hard-posting NPC commander.

**Volcano Base** *(pp. 134–138)*
A short-term base in a dormant volcano — abundant geothermal power, mineral wealth in the ejecta, and
heat that masks sensors; usually corporate ore-extraction, active only a few months. **Era:** recast the
"Rebel takeover of abandoned plants" note. **Feeds:** hidden base, mining outpost, geothermal/masking
content.

**Island Hideout** *(pp. 80–83)*
A rented atoll getaway for a fugitive — blue lagoon, reefs, tropical storms that flood the island,
island huts. Sample is a corporate manager who fled **Kuat** under a cover identity to escape pursuers.
**Era:** keep Kuat (it's a launch planet); recast the ISB pursuers as **Republic Intelligence** or **KDY
corporate security**. **Feeds:** fugitive-hideout POI, protect/expose-the-fugitive quest, Kuat-linked
backstory.

**Waterfall Cavern** *(pp. 139–145)*
A natural cavern behind a waterfall, stalactite chambers, remote enough to be quiet but not abandoned;
used as an arms-dealer **dead-drop**. Sample owner: see Karbo Jaxx in §2. **Era:** recast the dead-drop's
client from "Rebel agents" to **Independent partisans / syndicate cells**. **Feeds:** smuggling dead-drop
POI, gunrunner content, "Korba's Gun Drop" — the book's capstone worked example, a deal-gone-wrong
quest.

### 1C. Urban / built

**Safehouses** *(pp. 100–112)*
Urban townhouse and apartment safehouses — concealed rooms, basement workshops, garages, access by
password / keycode / visual or genetic ID; ownership is among the most dangerous jobs going (discovery
means capture). **Era:** recast the operative network from "the Rebellion" to **Republic Intelligence /
CIS agents / a criminal syndicate**. **Feeds:** **Coruscant-underworld** safehouse, spy/agent housing,
**Espionage (G22)** content, the "safe room behind the closet" trope.

**Urban Sewer System** *(pp. 128–133)*
Labyrinthine waste tunnels — storm-runoff, organic-waste, and hazardous-liquid sub-systems, used as
homes, hideouts, and alternate routes; populated by maintenance droids, pests, larger creatures, and
patrolling law enforcement chasing the criminals who use them. **Feeds:** Coruscant-underworld traversal
layer, hideout, urban-encounter content, "lose the tail in the tunnels" chase.

**Starport** *(pp. 118–127)*
A ready-made base — rent one or more docking bays, get the buildings, services, and basic security for
free, so long as you keep your appearance legal and nonthreatening; includes a security station, tractor
beams, and a supply cache. **Feeds:** **Player-Shop (G17)** / docking content, port-as-base, and a direct
tie to **Platt's Starport Guide** (the next economy book, which extends this docking model). (Book
explicitly cross-references it.)

### 1D. Faction-framed (recast required)

**GAR / CIS Rapid Deployment Station** *(pp. 75–79; was "Imperial Rapid Deployment Station," Nen-Carvon)*
A prefab, light-duty modular outpost dropped from a starship — an octagonal platform on four short
wide legs, ringed by an electrified metal-mesh skirt (keeps animals and intruders from crawling under),
with five habitable and four support modules; repeating-blaster turrets, an ion cannon, missile
launchers. **Recast:** a **Grand Army of the Republic Forward Deployment Station** (clone garrison) or a
**CIS Droid Deployment Station**. **Feeds:** military forward base, contested-zone objective, "take the
forward outpost" quest.

**Hidden Cell Base** *(pp. 91–95; was "Rebel Outpost," DVL-181 "The Quagmire")*
A small, multi-purpose hidden base — resupply and repair for friendly ships passing through, an
infantry bivouac, a staging/training area, a starfighter hangar; location is so compartmentalized
that ~90% of the staff don't know which planet they're on; sensor and comms array. **Recast:** a **CIS
partisan cell base**, an **Independent anti-Hutt hideout**, or a **clandestine Jedi watch-post**;
recast SpecForce staging to clone-commando or CIS-commando staging. **Feeds:** hidden faction base,
staging-area content, "find the secret base" investigation.

**Recon Field Base** *(pp. 96–99; "Outpost Cinder," Team Razor)*
A short-term forward surveillance camp — a command center tucked under a ledge, overwatch posts on
ridgelines, a retrieval plan, minimal footprint, "don't let them know we're here yet." **Recast:** a
clone recon team / CIS scout team / Independent scouts. **Feeds:** temporary wilderness camp, recon
quest, **Spacer/Scout (G25)** and **Encounters (G24)** content, the "Rescue Mission" hook.

## 2. NPC archetype roster

Reduced to reusable archetypes. The gunrunner carries a full faithful stat block (the book's capstone
GMC); the rest are templates with the stat ranges the book uses. All era-agnostic or recast as noted.

**Gunrunner / arms dealer** — *Karbo Jaxx (alias of Nain Lup), Rodian* — keep as-is, on-theme.
Runs a legal-merchant front (Nain Lup, a respectable small-time arms seller on Outer Rim worlds) over
an illicit dead-drop operation (Karbo, who works alone, prefers caches to face-to-face, and demands
payment to a secure account before naming the drop). Era recast: client base = Independent partisans
or a syndicate, not the Rebellion.

```
PERCEPTION 3D+2: bargain 5D+2, con 5D, persuasion 5D
STRENGTH 3D+1:  lifting 4D+2
TECHNICAL 2D+1: armor repair 3D+2, blaster repair 4D+2, starship weapon repair 4D+1
Character Points: 15   Move: 10
Gear: heavy blaster pistol (5D), holdout blaster (3D), light repeating blaster (6D),
thermal detonators (10D), grenades (5D), vibroblade (STR+3D), comlink, datapad,
macrobinoculars (+1D search beyond 50 m), blast vest (+1 energy / +1D physical),
blast helmet (+1 energy / +1D physical)
```

**Pirate warlord** — *Chevin raider boss type.* Commands a fortified asteroid; trades in slaving,
spice, kidnapping; surrounded by fringers hoping for a cut. Combat-leader stat spread (Blaster,
Intimidation, Command high; PER/STR forward). On-theme — keep.

**Water boss** — *desert-settlement power broker.* Controls the only water source in a remote town;
sells access; the de-facto government where none exists. Social/leverage build (bargain, con,
persuasion, willpower). **Strong Tatooine fit** — slots into the Desert Oasis / dune-sea template.

**Fugitive exec / noble** — *corporate manager under a cover identity.* Fled a Core/Colonies world
(Kuat in the source) to a rented hideout; jumpy, well-funded, watching for pursuers. Knowledge/
bureaucracy-forward, low combat. Recast pursuers to Republic Intel / corporate security.

**Mercenary commander** — *PMC leader.* Runs a fortified compound, sells small-unit ground-assault
work, can coordinate with air/space support but is "strictly a ground shop." Command + tactics +
heavy-weapons build. Maps to an Independent / BHG merc faction.

**Base commander** — *hard-posting CO.* Competent, resentful of a miserable assignment (the glacier
base), keeps the lights on. Mid-range military officer spread. Generic — drop into any defended site.

  auto-lands ships in cramped hangars when slaved to the base computer.
- **Vaporators** — ~400 cr each (up to 60% bulk discount for 200+), max ~3 m tall, still programmed in
  binary. As a sole water source, **7 units per person** is the minimum. (Pure economy/Tatooine flavor.)

### 3F. Planetary shields (two types, with rules)

- **Encasing shields** — older, on/off, a solid "wall" closer to armor than a shield; cheaper, common,
  durable. No friendly fire out except through permanent **fire-gaps** (high-priority weak points; many
  systems have none). **Rule:** add the generator's shield dice to all sensor difficulties for sensing
  *inside* the shield (including fire-control through it); roll shield dice vs. a transiting ship's hull
  as damage (its own shields don't apply). Dropping a starfighter through a gap = Difficult-to-Heroic
  terrain; a capital ship = Heroic at best, often impossible. *Example:* MerrWeapons WorldArmor 9 —
  Death Star scale, Crew 25, Cost 12 billion, Body 3D, **Shield 3D**.
- **Shutter shields** — interlocking, adjustable; overlap to combine defenses or open to allow
  counterfire, sallies, and blockade runners; rely on decentralized power. **Rule:** treat as encasing
  for sensors/power when closed; with a gap open, fly-through is Moderate-to-Heroic, or opposed against
  the operator's planetary-shields skill (typically 4D–5D) vs. the ship's starship-dodge. Starfighters
  fare much better than capital ships. *Example:* DefenStar Nyalsan II — Capital scale, Crew 18, Cost
  500 million, Body 3D, **Shield 10D**.

**Missile types** (apply to any launcher above): **Dumb** — straight line to impact/fuel-out.
**Smart** — FC 2D lock-on; a locked target shakes it on a roll vs. the missile's lock + 5; "orphaned"
if fired before lock, then tries to acquire. **Savant** — a "surprise weapon": flies one round as a
dumb rocket, then a Moderate missile roll tags the target; if it tags, the missile is 4D to hit at
point-blank (attacker still makes the straight-line attack roll). **Stalker** — FC 3D, can *reacquire*
every round if lock is lost, 30-round fuel; designed for the RAX-I.

## 4. Base-construction & `(A) Engineering` model

**Framing (see §6):** reference depth. Useful for (a) sanity-checking the abstract tier prices in
Cities/Housing, (b) fleshing out the **`(A) Engineering` skill** and **Crafting (G07)**, and (c) an
*optional* "advanced base customization" layer. It is **not** a drop-in replacement for the existing
abstract per-tier costing.

**The skill** — `(A) Engineering`, an *advanced* skill: double CP cost to advance, plus prerequisite
skills. Specializations: armor, battle station, capital ship, civil/industrial, computer, droid, ground
vehicle, hover vehicle, **installation**, repulsorlift, space transports, starfighter, walker, weapon.
Only *installation* is detailed (rules also apply to civil/industrial). Tasks it permits on a site:
**build, maintain, repair, modify, jury-rig.** Workflow: rough plan → GM approval → cost → time → build
→ install weapons/defenses (always *after* the base is built) → integrate.

**Feature cost factors** (materials in credits; labor scales per being per hour/day/week/month):

| Feature | Materials cost |
|---|---|
| Living/working space (per cramped unit: bunk, 1-person refresher, workdesk) | 5,000 |
| Life-support: atmosphere | 500 × planned occupants |
| Life-support: pressure | (× occupants) |
| Life-support: temperature | +2D×1,000 to cool, +1D×1,000 to heat |
| Life-support: gravity | (judgment call; spin-gravity is a free-ish alternative but degrades weapon accuracy and risks catastrophe if the spin is disrupted) |
| Life-support: radiation shielding | 1,000 × planned occupants |
| Vehicle hangar | 200 × number of speeder-scale vehicles |
| Repair bay | 500 × starfighter-scale / 600 × walker-scale vehicle |
| Airlock | 500 each (no extra labor) |
| Machine shop | base = basic; ×2 cost → +1D to repair/engineering rolls in it; ×3 → +2D (cap) |
| Med bay | 30,000 / bed · 400,000 clinic (20 beds + bacta) · 1,500,000 hospital (100+ beds) |

- **Comfort upgrades** multiply both materials cost *and* time taken (base difficulty unchanged).
- **Labor cost = 0** if the work-force is droids, slaves, contract labor, or volunteers.
- **Power generator cost** = (occupants ÷ 5) + 10 (hangar) + 5 (machine shop) + 5 (clinic) or 10
  (hospital) + 15 (if *any* weapons/defenses) → × type multiplier. Generator types and their trade-offs:
  **Reactor** (most common; small fuel, enormous output, volatile — core damage chains and destroys the
  site), **Solar** (clean but inefficient; 50%+ of surface as collectors; damageable), **Wind** (cheap,
  renewable; needs constant high wind = a harsh world), **Hydroelectric** (needs strong current/tides),
  **Fossil fuel** (inefficient, scarce fuel, detectable smoke/heat, heavy maintenance — bad for
  concealment), **Municipal** (settled areas only; cheap fee or theft via energy taps, but the utility
  can trace fluctuations to source), **Geothermal** (very efficient, near-limitless once in; hard to
  install via crust-drilling; waste heat is sensor-detectable).

**Build difficulty ladder** (difficulty / what it covers / time / work-force):

| Difficulty | Structure | Time | Work-force |
|---|---|---|---|
| Very Easy | Small static, no moving parts (sheds, walls, arches) | 1D hours | 1 sentient/droid |
| Easy | Small dwelling (1–2 rooms), simple drainage/irrigation, basic road | 1D days | 1–2D |
| Moderate | Medium dwelling (3–5 rooms) | ~1D days | 1–2D |
| Difficult | Fortified/multistory dwelling, complex drainage (acres), load-bearing (bridges), multistory complex (shield-gen complex, scanner array), small space platform | 1D weeks | 1–2D × 10 |
| Very Difficult | Fortress/military buildings, protective walls, town-scale roads/drainage/waste, planetary irrigation/road network | 1D × n weeks | 1D × 10 |
| Heroic | Giant structures (skyhooks), large cities (pop 100,001–10M), large platform/shipyards | 1D–3D months | 1D × 100 |
| Incredibly | Orbital cities, Coruscant-scale urban integration, planetary treatment facilities | beyond | — |

**Installing equipment/weapons** (after the base exists), by device scale: Character — Very Easy, 1D
hours · Speeder — Easy, 2D hours · Walker — Moderate, 1–2D hours · Starfighter — Difficult, 3D hours ·
Capital — Very Difficult, crew 2D, 2D days · Death Star — Heroic, crew 10D, 3D weeks.

**Condition modifiers** (difficulty / time / cost): zero or no gravity (+1D / +1D / cost up sharply) ·
unstable terrain (— / +1D weeks / —) · poor supply network (+2D / — / —) · poor/unskilled work-force
(+1D / — / —) · favorable environment (−1D to −2D / time halved / —) · mild temperatures (−1D / — / —)
· superior construction materials (−1D / — / ×2 materials) · superior supply network (— / −1D weeks /
—) · motivated/skilled work-force (−1 difficulty level / −1D time / ×1.5 labor, optional).

**Integrating systems:** count the features + weapons/devices used and roll an integration check (harder
with more systems). Every newly installed item triggers a fresh integration roll.

## 6. How this maps to what we've actually built (read before §3/§4)

**Slots in as content, now:**
- **§1 archetypes → Player Cities (G12):** building/district themes and the frontier-city / hidden-city
  variants. A "fortress" city, a "sewer-tunnel" hidden district, a "deep-sea plant" frontier site, a
  "desert oasis with a water boss" — these are theme + flavor on top of the existing claim/governance
  schema, not new mechanics.
- **§1 archetypes → Org-HQ flavor:** the existing Small Outpost / Chapter House / Fortress tiers get
  named, themed variants (pirate asteroid, merc compound, glacier base, fortress-citadel) without
  touching their cost/guard mechanics.
- **§1 + §7 → Territory (G11) objective sites & Director quests:** every entry is a defended layout with
  hooks — exactly the deterministic quest-template fodder the roadmap §4 wants.
- **§2 NPC roster → encounter/quest NPCs** and the Director library.
- **Vaporators (§3E) → economy/Tatooine flavor** (water as a metered resource; the water-boss faction).

**Does *not* slot in without a decision:**
- **Defensive emplacements (§3) as a player-buildable system** collides with **Territory §12.3**, which
  lists *automated territory defense (turrets, mines, traps)* under **What NOT to Build** — "Guard NPCs
  are sufficient," "too complex for the current codebase." So emplacements are best used as **set-dressing,
  named landmarks, quest objectives** ("sabotage the ion cannon," "open a fire-gap in the shutter
  shield"), and **canonical D6 stats for NPC bases** the space/ground combat systems can reference. A
  player-facing base-defense layer is a *future* feature that would explicitly reopen that decision.
- **The per-feature construction model (§4)** is far more granular than the **abstract tier pricing** in
  Cities/Housing (founding cost, expansion cost per room, weekly maintenance). Use §4 to **sanity-check**
  those abstract numbers and to give the **`(A) Engineering` skill / Crafting (G07)** real content, not
  to replace the abstract model. The book's own framing — "this is GM-facing campaign content," weapons
  installed "for rules purposes after the base is built" — matches treating it as reference depth.

The honest one-line summary: **the archetypes, NPCs, and quest hooks are the payload; the emplacement
and construction tables are a reference annex plus optional future depth, deliberately not wired against
two locked decisions.**

## 7. Director quest-seed library (era-translated)

These are the book's adventure hooks, recast — they drop into the deterministic quest-template library
(roadmap §4), with flavor text optional (Haiku/Mistral top layer).

- **"Attack!"** *(Fortress)* — assault or defend a walled citadel; objective is the wall breach / the
  anti-infantry cannon. Scales as a faction siege or a small strike.
- **"Spacetrooper Assault"** *(Lunar Station)* — a boarding assault on a modular orbital station; recast
  the assault force to clone or B2/commando-droid teams.
- **"Rescue Mission"** *(Recon Field Base)* — extract a downed team or stranded asset from a hot zone
  before the enemy realizes anyone's there.
- **"Hidden Munitions"** *(Deep Sea Plant)* — discover and seize a concealed arms cache in a pressurized
  underwater facility.
- **"Korba's Gun Drop"** *(Waterfall Cavern, capstone)* — a dead-drop arms deal goes wrong: wrong cache,
  a rival at the drop, or a client the dealer didn't vet.
- **Island fugitive** *(Island Hideout)* — protect, expose, or extract a corporate fugitive hiding under
  a cover identity while pursuers close in.
- **Free-port intrigue** *(Battle Station)* — the still-armed marketplace station as a neutral-ground
  meet, heist target, or hostage standoff.
- **Water-boss politics** *(Desert Oasis)* — a remote town where control of the well is control of the
  town; leverage, sabotage, or succession.
- **Salvage & debt** *(Crashed Starship)* — a derelict freighter with a live engineering section, a
  salvage claim, and a Hutt creditor who hasn't forgotten.
- **Lose the tail** *(Urban Sewer System)* — a chase or escort through waste tunnels patrolled by
  enforcement and inhabited by worse.
