## At a glance

The 501st Legion is the **most famous clone unit of the Clone Wars** — General Anakin Skywalker's command, Captain Rex's squad of squads, the elite legion that bore the heaviest fighting and the most personal loyalty in the Republic military. Their distinctive blue-and-white markings and their personal connection to Anakin make them the canonical model for "the General's beloved unit." After Order 66, they march on the Jedi Temple under the now-Vader Skywalker, becoming **"Vader's Fist"** — the legion that ends the Order.

For SW_MUSH purposes, the 501st is the canonical reference unit when authors need to depict a Republic line unit with personal Jedi-General loyalty.

## Composition and identity

- **Formal designation:** 501st Legion, Grand Army of the Republic.
- **Informal name:** "**Vader's Fist**" (post-Order 66 Imperial-Era retroactive — but the name attaches to the legion's CW history).
- **Markings:** Phase I and Phase II clone trooper armor with **blue-and-white** markings (deep blue trim on white armor). Distinctive across the GAR.
- **Composition:** Multi-corps legion comprising battalions, regiments, platoons. Scaled up significantly from a "battalion" in colloquial usage; in actual TO&E, the 501st is a **legion** comprising thousands of troopers.
- **Specializations attached to the 501st:**
  - Standard clone trooper line infantry
  - Clone trooper pilots (starship and ground vehicle groups)
  - Clone gunners
  - Officers with customized armor
  - **Advanced Recon Commandos (ARCs)** for special operations — including ARC Trooper **Fives** (CT-5555) and **Echo** (later joins the Bad Batch)
  - Advanced Recon Force (ARF) Scout Troopers for reconnaissance and light vehicle support
  - Clone jetpack troopers for aerial combat
  - Battlefield medics and other specialists
- **Vehicles:** All Terrain Tactical Enforcers (AT-TEs); Republic gunships; multiple support and assault platforms.

## Notable Clone Wars deployments

- **First Battle of Geonosis (22 BBY).** The legion's birth as a fighting force.
- **Battle of Christophsis (early CW).** Where Ahsoka first joined Anakin and the 501st.
- **Battle of Arantara.** Early mission; Rex incapacitated; Anakin teaches him "a true leader always leads from the front."
- **Mission to Benglor.** Investigating Separatist activity; trooper Bellow killed by indigenous creature.
- **Siege of Hisseen.** Joint operation with the 7th Sky Corps (Obi-Wan / Cody / 212th) and the 104th Battalion (Plo Koon / Wolffe / Wolfpack). Multi-Jedi Republic operation.
- **Various Outer Rim deployments** throughout the war — Felucia, Saleucami, Mandalore, Ryloth, Mon Cala, Cato Neimoidia, Kamino, multiple.
- **Battle of Coruscant (19 BBY).** Defends the Republic capital during Grievous's surprise assault.
- **Order 66 / Jedi Temple massacre (19 BBY).** Marches on the Temple under Vader-Skywalker. Kills the Jedi inside, including younglings. The legion's defining moment of horror.

## Voice and culture (for Director AI rendering)

The 501st's culture is canonically **most personal of all clone units**.

- **Loyalty to Anakin specifically.** Where the 212th respects Obi-Wan, the 501st loves Anakin. The Director AI should let troopers express personal attachment to their General.
- **Individuality is permitted.** Anakin actively encourages clones to express personality through armor customization and personal combat style — to the chagrin of the more orthodox Council members.
- **High casualty tolerance.** The 501st is the unit most often deployed to the heaviest fighting; trooper losses are constant; veterans pass experience to rookie replacements.
- **Rex's voice is the unit's voice.** Captain Rex's calm-professional cadence is canonical for the 501st. The Director AI should use Rex's voice register for unnamed 501st troopers when the unit speaks collectively.
- **Iconic verbal patterns:** "Sir, yes sir, General Skywalker." "We've got contacts, sir." "For the Republic." Echoes of Anakin's banter when the General is present.

## Use in `weight_of_war_design_v1.md`

The 501st is the **canonical line-unit reference** for Republic-side player content. Concrete uses:

- **Squad-scale player party content.** Players running 501st troopers should encounter the unit's culture: personal-Jedi loyalty, casualty tolerance, individualistic flair within professional discipline.
- **Mass-combat unit reference (CWCG §2 additive ruleset).** A "501st Legion" mass-combat unit could be templated using the rules in `cwcg_extraction_v1.md` §2.5 (Clone Trooper Battalion sample). The 501st is a multi-battalion legion, so several units would be needed at mass-combat scale.
- **Named-trooper content.** Beyond Rex, Fives, and Echo, the legion includes many named individuals canonically. The system should support named-NPC clone characters with the same individuality treatment Anakin's culture canonically permits.
- **Order 66 inflection point.** The 501st marching on the Temple is the canonical "loyal unit turns" moment. Players in late-CW content who interact with the 501st must reckon with this looming pivot.

## Use in `clone_wars_director_lore_pivot_design_v1.md`

The Director AI must:

- **Voice 501st troopers with Rex-cadence.** Calm, professional, capable of warmth toward their General.
- **Treat their loyalty to Anakin as canonical and active.** When the General is present, troopers respond with visible personal attachment.
- **Treat their loyalty to Ahsoka as canonical.** During her time as Padawan-Commander, she is the unit's "Commander Tano" with full respect. Post-departure, the unit feels the loss.
- **Treat the inhibitor chip as canonical.** Every 501st trooper has one. After Order 66, the chip drives compliance unless removed (as Ahsoka does for Rex). Players who investigate post-Order-66 troopers may find chip-removal opportunities.
- **Treat the Order 66 / Temple massacre as a fixed campaign clock event.** Before this, the 501st is heroic. After, they are agents of horror under Vader.
