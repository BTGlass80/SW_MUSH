## At a glance

The **Federal District** is **Coruscant's central government district** — the political and administrative heart of the Galactic Republic, encompassing the **Galactic Senate Building**, the **Jedi Temple**, the **Chancellor's Office**, the **500 Republica residential complex** (where senior senators live), the **Republic Judiciary Central Detention Center**, the **Avenue of the Core Founders**, and the major executive and legislative buildings. Multiple alternate names: **Senate District**, **Executive District**, **Legislative District**, **Military District** (during the Clone Wars), and colloquially "**the Core of Coruscant**" or "**Core Square**."

For SW_MUSH purposes, the Federal District is the **canonical "central government district" reference** complementing `wookieepedia_extracts/coruscant.md` — useful for player content involving Senate operations, Jedi Council institutional politics, executive-branch encounters, or military command coordination.

## Location and physical character

- **Coruscant.** Surface levels (high-altitude). The planetary capital's most prestigious zone.
- **Architecture:** Iconic monumentally-scaled government buildings. Wide thoroughfares; ceremonial plazas; security-controlled access; controlled airspace. Distinct from the working districts (CoCo Town) and entertainment districts (Uscru) by its scale and gravitas.
- **Air/skylane traffic** is heavily restricted; unauthorized flight in Federal District airspace is a serious offense.
- **Security infrastructure:** Coruscant Security Force (CSF) and Coruscant Guard maintain a constant presence; checkpoints, patrols, building security at every major site.

## Clone Wars events in the Federal District

### The Battle of Coruscant (19 BBY)
- **Grievous's strike team landing.** During the Battle of Coruscant, **General Grievous and his MagnaGuard strike team land directly in the Federal District** to capture Chancellor Palpatine from the Senate Building / Chancellor's office area. This is the canonical "kidnapping" that opens *Revenge of the Sith*.
- **Power-grid attack.** During the war, the CIS canonically disabled Coruscant's central power distribution grid, causing a Federal District power outage. Date and arc unspecified; useful as a war-event reference.

### The Zillo Beast incident
- **Captured on Malastare** by the Republic. Brought to Coruscant for study (Doctor Sionver Boll's research).
- **The beast escapes containment** in the Federal District; rampages through the streets; damages multiple buildings before being killed (canonically by Mace Windu, with the Zillo Beast eventually killed under Palpatine's secret order despite its sentience). The Federal District suffers significant property damage.

### The Senate hostage crisis
- **Cad Bane raids the Senate Building** during a session, taking senators hostage to free Ziro the Hutt from imprisonment. The Federal District's security forces respond; Anakin Skywalker is involved; Bane and his team escape with Ziro. This is canonical mid-CW content.

### The Sidious arrest party (19 BBY)
- **Mace Windu, Kit Fisto, Saesee Tiin, Agen Kolar** confront Sidious in the Chancellor's Office. The duel takes place there. Three Council members die; Mace defeats Sidious; Anakin intervenes; Mace dies. Order 66 commences within hours.

### Order 66 / Jedi Temple massacre (19 BBY)
- **The 501st Legion** secures the Jedi Temple under newly-christened **Darth Vader**. The Temple massacre takes place here. Younglings, padawans, masters, and staff killed.
- **Temple → Imperial Palace.** Sidious confiscates the Temple and refurbishes it as the Imperial Palace.

## Voice and culture (for Director AI rendering)

### Federal District register
- **Authoritative cosmopolitan formality.** Senators, bureaucrats, military officers, Jedi. Speech is educated, careful, often political-coded.
- **Wartime weariness (mid-to-late CW).** By the late war, even the Federal District feels the conflict — security tightened, anti-war protests outside the Temple, Senate floor speeches lengthening.
- **Class confidence.** District inhabitants speak with the assumption that they are at the center of galactic affairs.

### Working class within the district
- **Service staff, security personnel, building maintenance.** Multi-species working-class population that supports the district's operations. The Director AI should let these voices be present alongside the political class — a Coruscant Guard trooper saluting at a checkpoint, a Senate building cleaning droid going about its work.

## Use in `coruscant_underworld_landmarks_design_v1.md`

The Federal District provides:

- **The "respectable surface" reference.** Player content set here represents the gleaming, ordered, corrupt-but-functional upper-Coruscant aesthetic that contrasts with the underworld levels.
- **Government-encounter location.** Players who need to interact with Senators, Jedi Council, Chancellor's office staff, or Republic military command will do so in the Federal District.
- **Restricted-access content.** The district's security infrastructure makes it harder to enter than other Coruscant zones. Player content involving infiltration, unauthorized access, or pursuit through restricted areas is supported.

## Use in `clone_wars_director_lore_pivot_design_v1.md`

The Director AI must:

- **Treat the Federal District as the era's most-default Coruscant content location.** Most major government and Jedi NPCs are encountered here.
- **Voice it with wartime weariness as the war progresses.** Early-CW Federal District is bustling-confident; late-CW Federal District is anxious-tired.
- **Treat the Battle of Coruscant (19 BBY) as a canonical fixed event.** Players present can witness or participate in the Federal District phase of the battle (Grievous's strike, the kidnapping, the recovery).
- **Treat the Order 66 / Temple massacre as a fixed pivot.** After this, the district transforms: Temple becomes Imperial Palace; Senate becomes Imperial Senate; aesthetic shifts.
