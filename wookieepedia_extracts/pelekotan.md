## At a glance

**Pelekotan** is the **Korunnai term for the Force** — but the term carries philosophical weight that distinguishes it from the Jedi understanding. Translates loosely as "world-power"; literally as "**jungle mind**." Among the Korunnai of Haruun Kal, Pelekotan is not merely a power one wields but **an entity one negotiates with** — a mystical force that rules the jungle's danger and demands struggle from those who use it.

For SW_MUSH purposes, Pelekotan is the **canonical alternate Force-philosophy reference** for the project, complementing the broader Korunnai material in `wookieepedia_extracts/korunnai.md`. Useful for Force-tradition content where the Director AI needs a non-Jedi philosophical framing of the Force.

## Core concept

- **"Jungle mind" / "world-power."** The Pelekotan is the Force as understood through the lens of Haruun Kal's environment — toxic cloudsea below, predator-rich jungle above, herd-life balanced on the edge of survival every day. The philosophy is shaped by what the Korunnai's environment teaches: power is dangerous, the world is hostile, survival demands struggle, and the line between desire and fear is thin.
- **An entity, not a tool.** Where Jedi speak of the Force as something to be channeled and aligned with, Korunnai speak of the Pelekotan as a presence that **answers** when called. Not always what the caller wanted; not always safely.
- **Canonical Vastor quote:** "*Pelekotan brings us waking dreams of our desires and our fears. When we desire what we fear and fear what we desire, Pelekotan always answers... Only Pelekotan is real. Everything else is forms and shadows: less even than a cloud, or a memory. We are Pelekotan's dream.*" This quote is the central canonical articulation of the philosophy. The Director AI may use it (or paraphrase it) for atmospheric content.

## The Lor Pelek

- **Lor Pelek = "jungle master."** A shaman-figure among the Korunnai who embodies the power of the Pelekotan. The Lor Pelek is canonically described as **"a person of great power and great peril, as unpredictable as the jungle."**
- **Capabilities (canonical):**
  - **Healing powers.** Including the unusual ability to drive parasitic fever wasps out of an infected person's brain and nervous system via the Force — a healing technique unknown to and never witnessed by the Jedi Order.
  - **Fierce warrior capability.** The Lor Pelek's combat power is sourced from the Pelekotan's struggle-demanding nature. Korunnai who reach this level of practice canonically pay costs for their power.
  - **Unique Pelekotan techniques** unknown to the Jedi. Mace Windu observed during his Haruun Kal mission that **Kar Vastor possessed Force powers never seen before by the Jedi Order** — capabilities discovered through Pelekotan-tradition practice rather than Jedi instruction.
- **The cost.** Kar Vastor canonically **lost the ability to speak any comprehensible language** in exchange for the greater Force powers he needed to survive in the jungle. This is the canonical "Pelekotan demands struggle" pattern — every gain has a cost. Useful for any player content involving non-Jedi Force-traditions where consequences are tracked.
- **Kar Vastor as canonical example.** Mace Windu's *Dôshalo* (clan-brother) Kar Vastor was the Lor Pelek at the beginning of the Clone Wars. Useful as an NPC / antagonist / mentor figure for any Haruun Kal content.

## How Korunnai relate to Pelekotan

- **Survival framing.** The Korunnai use the Pelekotan to survive — preserving their herds, defending their Ghôsh, navigating the jungle's dangers. The relationship is **utilitarian-spiritual rather than ethical-philosophical**.
- **Demanding rather than granting.** The Pelekotan does not give freely. It answers when called, but the answer is shaped by the caller's deepest motivations — including the parts the caller would prefer not to see. The Director AI should let Pelekotan-related content carry this "the Force will show you what you wanted, even if you didn't want to want it" quality.
- **No formal religion.** There is canonically no organized Pelekotan religion or temple system equivalent to the Jedi Order. The Lor Pelek is a tradition-figure, not a priestly office. Practice is transmitted through Ghôsh family-clan teaching, not academic instruction.

## Comparison to Jedi Force-philosophy (for Director AI rendering)

| Dimension | Jedi understanding | Pelekotan understanding |
|---|---|---|
| **What is the Force?** | An energy field generated by all living things. | A jungle mind / world-power that rules the wild. |
| **How does one use it?** | Mindful alignment; meditation; ethical training. | Negotiation under struggle; the wild demands a price. |
| **Light-vs-dark framing** | Central organizing distinction. | Less prominent; the Pelekotan rules darkness as much as light. |
| **Training tradition** | Formal Order; Master-Padawan; Temple academies. | Family-clan transmission; Lor Pelek shaman-figures; experiential. |
| **Animal-bonding** | Not a primary practice. | Akk dog and grasser bonding are core practices. |
| **Healing techniques** | Standard Jedi healing forms. | Unique techniques (e.g., parasitic-wasp expulsion) unknown to Jedi. |
| **Cost framing** | Detachment from passion is the path; cost is renunciation. | Power demands struggle; cost is concrete and individual. |

The Director AI should let players who study the Pelekotan find it **legitimately distinct** from Jedi philosophy — not a flawed version of it but a different framing of the same energy.

## Use in `force_resonant_landmarks_design_v1.md`

Pelekotan content provides:

- **A canonical alternate Force-philosophy.** Useful for any player content where Force-tradition diversity matters.
- **Specific unique techniques.** The fever-wasp expulsion and Kar Vastor's untranslatable speech-loss exchange are both concrete examples of "Pelekotan techniques unknown to Jedi." The system might support player content where Force-sensitive PCs learn Pelekotan techniques at narrative cost.
- **Haruun Kal as a Force-resonant landmark.** The whole world is Pelekotan-active. The jungle itself is "alive" in the Force in this tradition's framing.

## Use in `clone_wars_director_lore_pivot_design_v1.md`

The Director AI must:

- **Voice Pelekotan content with weight and danger.** Don't make it a quaint local variation. The Pelekotan canonically demands struggle and exacts costs.
- **Treat the Lor Pelek as available NPC content** for Haruun Kal player visits.
- **Treat unique Pelekotan techniques as canonical and rare.** Not every Korunnai knows them; not every Jedi can replicate them.
- **Treat Mace Windu's awareness of these techniques as deep lore.** Players who study Pelekotan with Mace's awareness may receive unusually nuanced teaching.
