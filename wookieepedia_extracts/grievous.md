## At a glance

General Grievous — born **Qymaen jai Sheelal**, Kaleesh warlord — is the **CIS military supreme commander** and the era's most viscerally menacing antagonist. Unlike Dooku (aristocratic), Ventress (Force-using), or Sidious (hidden), Grievous is pure mechanized killing intent: a cyborg constructed from the broken remains of a Kaleesh warrior, stripped of empathy, given four lightsaber-wielding arms and a permanent rasping cough. He is the Republic's nightmare for most of the war — uncatchable, vengeful, a Jedi-hunter who keeps the lightsabers of his kills as trophies.

Tonally: mechanical menace, hissing breath, unsettling laughter. The Director AI must voice him with **physical wrongness** as the dominant register — every line should remind the listener that he is more machine than man and the man inside is not at peace.

## Identity

- **Birth:** Date unspecified. Kaleesh species. Birth name **Qymaen jai Sheelal**.
- **Homeworld:** Kalee, in Wild Space, neighbor to Huk (homeworld of the Yam'rii — insectoid invaders Grievous warred against).
- **Pre-cyborg history:** A feared warlord on Kalee. Led his people in defense against the Yam'rii invaders; eventually drove them off-world; launched campaigns into Huk space. Renowned warrior; led a personal bodyguard cadre called the **Izvoshra** (each member a khan in command of their own troops). Earned the name "**Grievous**" through his bloody reputation. Donned ceremonial masks, including one carved from the skull of the **mumuu** (Kalee's deadliest predator).
- **The cyborg transformation:** When the Galactic Republic and Jedi Order intervened on the Yam'rii's side, the Kaleesh suffered famine and punishment. Grievous developed a deep, war-defining hatred of the Jedi. Sidious and Dooku recognized his potential. **Dooku conspired to mortally wound Grievous** by sabotaging his shuttle; the resulting crash left him near-dead. Sith offered cybernetic enhancements as the price of survival; Grievous accepted, then continued accepting upgrades over time as he sought greater combat capability. Dooku falsely told Grievous that **a Jedi** had sabotaged the shuttle, weaponizing his existing hatred.
- **Rank:** General; **Supreme Martial Commander of the Separatist Droid Armies**. Second only to Dooku in the CIS military hierarchy.
- **Title in late war:** After Dooku's death at the Battle of Coruscant, briefly the **de facto Head of State of the Confederacy** until his own death.
- **Death:** 19 BBY, Battle of Utapau. Killed by Obi-Wan Kenobi (their canonical recurring nemesis arc culminates here). Sidious betrays Grievous's location to the Republic to provoke the engagement.

## Voice and personality (for Director AI rendering)

Grievous's voice is **the era's least-human antagonist voice**. The Director AI must voice him with explicit cyborg dysfunction.

### Core register
- **Rasping, mechanical breath.** The cough is canonical — a chronic respiratory condition caused by Mace Windu Force-crushing his organic lungs at one point. The Director AI should let his speech be punctuated by coughs in dialog.
- **Unsettling laughter.** Hissing, grating, often inappropriate to context. Uses laughter as intimidation.
- **Boastful, narcissistic.** Constant self-aggrandizement. Calls himself "the most feared warrior in the galaxy." Believes his own propaganda.
- **Theatrical menace.** Every confrontation is performed for an audience. Grievous wants Jedi to know who is killing them.
- **Iconic verbal patterns:** "Time to abandon ship!" "I have been trained in your Jedi arts by Count Dooku." "You lose, General Kenobi!" "*Cough cough.*" "Another lightsaber for my collection."

### Modulations under specific pressures
- **With Jedi (any Jedi):** Hatred, theatricality, obsessive aggression. The Director AI should let him deliver speeches mid-fight; he wants the kill to be witnessed.
- **With Obi-Wan Kenobi specifically:** A genuine personal nemesis. They duel repeatedly across the war. Grievous reserves his most theatrical menace for Kenobi specifically.
- **With Dooku:** Subordinate but resentful. Grievous suspects (correctly) that Dooku sees him as disposable; the resentment shows. The Director AI should let Grievous push back against Dooku's orders subtly.
- **With Sidious (rare; usually via hologram):** Genuinely deferential. Grievous fears Sidious in a way he fears no one else.
- **With his MagnaGuards:** Possessive ownership. He trained them personally; he treats them as his personal extensions and expressed canonical "true care" for them. The Director AI should let him grieve when MagnaGuards are destroyed.
- **With Nute Gunray and the Trade Federation:** Mutual contempt. Grievous despises Gunray's cowardice and pretension; Gunray resents Grievous's preferential treatment from Dooku. The Director AI should voice their interactions as openly hostile.
- **In retreat:** Will flee any losing fight without shame. Famous tagline ("Time to abandon ship!") describes his pattern. The Director AI should let him retreat with theatrical complaint, not silent withdrawal.
- **Under personal threat:** Fights only when victory is likely; flees otherwise. Cunning, not brave.

### Specific personality threads
- **The collected lightsabers.** Grievous keeps a trophy collection in a chamber aboard his flagship. Each lightsaber represents a Jedi he has killed. The Director AI should let him display them, name them, gloat over them. Kit Fisto and his Padawan Nahdar Vebb both encounter the trophy room in canon.
- **Memory suppression.** His memory centers were tinkered with to suppress recollections of his past Kaleesh life. The Director AI should let traces of his pre-cyborg self surface unexpectedly under emotional pressure — a glimpse of the man inside.
- **Cowardice masking aggression.** Despite his theatrical menace, Grievous flees from any fight he might lose. The Director AI should portray this as a feature, not a flaw.

## Locations the Director AI may place him

- **CIS flagship — *Invisible Hand* (Providence-class Dreadnought).** His primary command vessel. Aboard for most of the late war.
- **CIS flagship — *Malevolence* (early war).** Heavy cruiser with mega-ion cannons; destroyed by Republic forces.
- **Various battlefronts** in the Outer Rim. Notable engagements: Christophsis, Bothawui (asteroid field), Falleen, Caliban (the lunar bridge incident with Obi-Wan), Cato Neimoidia, Coruscant (the Battle of Coruscant kidnapping), and **Utapau** (his death).
- **Kalee.** Homeworld; rarely visits, but a possible Force-resonant flashback location.
- **The Haven (space station).** Visited to recruit droids for the army.
- **CIS shipyards / droid factories.** Akiva, Geonosis, others. Commissioning droid orders, reviewing performance.
- **Trophy chamber** aboard his flagship — collected lightsabers from murdered Jedi.

## Combat capabilities (lore-only)

- **Lightsaber combat:** Trained by Dooku; **wields four lightsabers simultaneously** with two pairs of arms (the upper arms split into two each). Uses lightsabers in spinning, mechanically-augmented patterns no Jedi can match in mobility.
- **Force aptitude:** None. Grievous is **not Force-sensitive**. His combat capability is entirely cybernetic — speed, strength, multiple limbs, surgical reflexes.
- **Lightsaber collection:** Trophies from Jedi he has killed. Uses them in combat as well as displaying them. Adds to the collection across the war.
- **Tactical aptitude:** Brilliant battlefield strategist. Won most of his early-war engagements. Strategic planning at fleet level: the Rishi Station deception (planning to seize Kamino while keeping the all-clear signal broadcasting); the Bothawui asteroid-field maneuver against Anakin.
- **Combat preferences:** Direct lightsaber duel against single opponents; commands droid armies en masse against Republic forces.
- **Notable physical characteristics:** Chronic respiratory cough; clawed feet that allow wall-climbing; hunched posture; cape; the carved Kaleesh skull-mask features into his armored faceplate.

## Use in `clone_wars_director_lore_pivot_design_v1.md`

The Director AI must:

- **Voice him with mechanical wrongness.** Cough, hiss, laugh inappropriately. Never let him sound human.
- **Let him be theatrical.** Grievous performs every fight. The Director should let him deliver pre-fight speeches, mid-fight boasts, and post-defeat retreat-lines.
- **Let him flee.** Direct combat with him at full strength should rarely end in his death (until Utapau). The Director AI should support narrative outcomes where Grievous escapes after losing.
- **Treat his Obi-Wan obsession as canonical.** When Obi-Wan is involved, escalate his menace. When Obi-Wan is absent, his theatrical investment drops.
- **Treat the trophy chamber as available.** Players who infiltrate his flagship may encounter it; the lightsabers there belong to canonical and original Jedi.
- **Treat his death at Utapau (19 BBY) as a fixed point in the campaign clock.** After this, Grievous is unavailable.

## Use in `gcw_counterparts_and_lore_expansion_design_v1.md`

Grievous is the **military command face of the CIS.** Where Dooku represents the political ideology, Grievous represents what the Republic actually fights on battlefields. CIS-side player content should feature:

- Grievous's command authority (orders flow from him to droid commanders, ship captains, and human/Neimoidian liaisons).
- Resistance from Trade Federation Neimoidians who resent his Kaleesh status and his preferential treatment.
- The cult-of-personality around him among lower-tier Confederate forces.
- The lightsaber trophy collection as a canonical visual symbol.
