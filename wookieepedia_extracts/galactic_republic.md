## At a glance

The **Galactic Republic** is the **Republic-side political institution during the Clone Wars** — a millennia-old democratic union of star systems, headquartered on **Coruscant**, governed by the Galactic Senate under an elected Supreme Chancellor. By the time of the Clone Wars (22–19 BBY), the Republic is **deeply corrupted**, increasingly militarized, and being deliberately destroyed from within by Sidious to manufacture his Galactic Empire. The Republic dies the day Sidious declares the Empire, immediately after Order 66.

For SW_MUSH purposes, the Galactic Republic is the **canonical political institution for Republic-side player content** — the Senate, the Chancellor's office, the bureaucracy, the Jedi-Senate relationship, the militarization process, the corruption running through every level.

## Government structure

### Galactic Senate
- **Composition:** Elected representatives from member systems and sectors. Each member world (or sector) sends a **Senator** plus support staff (aides, advisors, security).
- **Location:** **Senate Building** in the Senate District of Coruscant — the iconic mushroom-domed structure with the floating senatorial pods inside.
- **Speaker / Vice Chair:** **Mas Amedda** (Chagrian) — fully complicit in Sidious's plans.
- **Notable Senators (CW era):**
  - **Padmé Amidala** (Naboo) — peace advocate; Anakin's secret wife.
  - **Bail Organa** (Alderaan) — peace advocate; secretly building rebel networks late-war.
  - **Mon Mothma** (Chandrila) — peace advocate; eventually leads the rebel formation.
  - **Onaconda Farr** (Rodia) — peace advocate; murdered (poisoned) during anti-war organizing.
  - **Riyo Chuchi** (Pantora) — moderate.
  - **Lott Dod** (Trade Federation) — corrupt; CIS-aligned despite Senate seat.
  - **Orn Free Taa** (Ryloth) — corrupt loyalist.
  - **Halle Burtoni** (Kamino) — military-enhancement bill supporter.

### Office of the Supreme Chancellor
- **Chancellor:** Elected by the Senate. **Sheev Palpatine** (Naboo) holds the office throughout the Clone Wars; previously **Finis Valorum** (his predecessor) was famously weak and ineffectual.
- **Powers (CW era):** Increasingly expansive. Palpatine accumulates emergency powers, then constitutional amendments that permanently centralize authority in the Chancellor's office:
  - **Emergency Powers** (granted at war's outset by Jar Jar Binks's emergency motion).
  - **Reflex Amendment** — gives the Chancellor near-total authority over the military.
  - **Sector Governance Decree** (late war) — empowers Chancellor-appointed governors over multiple star systems.
  - Direct oversight of the **InterGalactic Banking Clan** (mid-war).

### Judicial system
- **Republic Judicial Forces** — the original peacekeeping arm. By the late Republic, effectively supplanted by the Jedi Order in galactic peacekeeping roles.
- **Galactic Court** — handles legal matters. Tarkin and the Senate manage the Ahsoka Tano trial through this venue.

### Military (during the Clone Wars)
- **Grand Army of the Republic (GAR)** — clone-trooper army; led by Jedi Generals. (See `wookieepedia_extracts/grand_army_of_the_republic.md`.)
- **Republic Navy** — fleet of *Venator*-class Star Destroyers, *Acclamator*-class assault ships, *Consular*-class cruisers, etc.
- **Coruscant Security Force (CSF)** — civilian police; coordinates with Coruscant Guard for capital security.
- **Coruscant Guard** — clone-trooper garrison force on Coruscant; ceremonial and security.

### Jedi Order
- **Allegiance:** Officially to the Senate. The Jedi Order "served the Republic" but maintained doctrinal independence.
- **Wartime role:** Leads GAR forces as Generals. The **Jedi Military Integration Act** (passed by the Senate post-Geonosis, 22 BBY) formally established Jedi as officers in the Republic military. This is canonically uncomfortable for many Jedi.
- **Public perception:** Wartime support waned. Anti-war protests outside the Jedi Temple by mid-war. Wilhuff Tarkin and other Republic officers begin agitating to revoke Jedi military authority.

## Key institutional dynamics during the Clone Wars

### 1. The corruption fact
Per architecture v35 §30 and per CWCG §3.2, **rampant corruption is canon**. The Republic is steeped in:
- Senators selling votes.
- Corporate lobbyists buying legislation.
- Trade Federation maintaining Senate seats while leading the CIS militarily.
- War profiteers benefiting from the conflict's continuation.
- Military officers selling substandard equipment.

The Director AI must let players encounter corruption at every institutional level.

### 2. The militarization fact
Per CWCG §3.4, the Republic transforms from a peacetime democratic union into a wartime militarized state. Civilian infrastructure (factories, hospitals, transportation networks) gets converted to military use. Public discourse shifts from civic democracy to wartime emergency.

### 3. The dwindling Jedi fact
Per CWCG §3.1 and architecture, the Jedi Order shrinks throughout the war. By Order 66, the Order is significantly fewer in number than at the start. Many junior Jedi die; replacement Knights are not raised quickly enough.

### 4. The financial reform
The **Republic Financial Reform Bill** (mid-war) deregulates the banks to allow new lines of credit for the war effort. Bail Organa and Padmé Amidala oppose this; it passes anyway. The Banking Clan (already CIS-aligned) gains huge influence over Republic finances. The Director AI may use this as backdrop for player content involving Republic economics.

### 5. The Senate's sclerosis
The Senate is canonically dysfunctional throughout the late Republic — too many factions, too many old precedents, unable to act decisively. The Chancellor's emergency-powers expansion is partially the result of Senate paralysis. By the war's end, the Senate has effectively voted itself into irrelevance.

## Capital infrastructure (Coruscant — late Republic)

- **Senate District** — Senate Building, Senate Plaza (later Imperial Plaza), 500 Republica residential building (where Padmé and other Senators live), Chancellor's Office, surrounding government buildings.
- **Federal District** — Coruscant administrative center.
- **Jedi Temple** — Jedi headquarters; administrative, training, and council functions.
- **Republic Special Weapons Group** — military R&D division (Wilhuff Tarkin, Orson Krennic). Canonically begins Death Star development from captured Geonosian schematics. This work continues into the Imperial Era.
- **Galactic City Medical Center** — medical hub; connected to the Jedi Temple infirmary via dedicated underground transport (per CWCG §5).
- **The underworld** — already covered in `wookieepedia_extracts/coruscant_underworld.md`. Late Republic government does not effectively administer the lower levels.

## Republic culture (for Director AI rendering)

- **Galactic Basic Standard** is the dominant language. Other languages used regionally; Gungan dialect is canonically heavily-accented Basic.
- **Republic credit** is the standard currency. Notably **does not function on Tatooine** — that frontier world uses local currency or barter.
- **Pre-war culture:** Decadent peacetime — galaxy-wide entertainment, podracing circuits (the Boonta Eve Classic), tourism economy, sleek aesthetic design in starships and architecture. Public attention drifts from politics toward escapism.
- **Wartime culture:** Anxiety, militarization, Senate-floor speeches replacing public debate, anti-war protests, propaganda (Separatist Shadowfeed media campaigns by Dooku spreading Republic-defeat narratives, accelerating militarization on the Republic side).

## Use in `gcw_counterparts_and_lore_expansion_design_v1.md`

The Galactic Republic is the **central reference for Republic-side player content** in the design. Concrete uses:

- **Player-character Republic roles.** Players might play:
  - A Senator's aide on Coruscant.
  - A Republic Judicial agent or Coruscant Security officer.
  - A peace-faction political operative working for Padmé, Bail, or Mon Mothma.
  - A Republic Intelligence operative.
  - A Coruscant journalist covering the war.
  - A weapons-development engineer for the Republic Special Weapons Group.
  - A clone trooper or GAR specialist (covered in the GAR extract).
  - A Jedi Knight or Padawan (covered separately).
- **Republic-side moral seriousness.** Republic citizens canonically support the war for genuine reasons (defense against perceived Separatist aggression). The Director AI should treat Republic loyalty with the same weight as Confederate loyalty — both are sincerely held by ordinary people.
- **The hidden truth.** Players who deeply investigate the Chancellor's emergency-powers accumulation may find the corruption pattern. Most do not realize Palpatine is Sith. Discovery is high-stakes.

## Use in `coruscant_underworld_landmarks_design_v1.md`

The Galactic Republic is the **administrative authority** that nominally governs Coruscant — but its administration of the lower levels is canonically weak. The Director AI should let players experience the contrast:
- Upper levels: gleaming, ordered, corrupt-but-functional.
- Underworld: neglected, unpoliced (except by the Coruscant Underworld Police, which operates in semi-autonomous mode), increasingly dangerous as the war drains resources.

## Use in `clone_wars_director_lore_pivot_design_v1.md`

The Director AI must:

- **Voice Republic institutional contexts with mid-war weariness.** The Senate is tired; the bureaucracy is overstretched; the public is anxious.
- **Treat the corruption pattern as constant.** Every institutional encounter has corruption potential.
- **Treat the Republic's dissolution (19 BBY, immediately after Order 66) as a fixed campaign clock event.** Within days of Order 66, Sidious declares the Empire. The Republic ends.
- **Allow nostalgic Republic-loyalty content post-Empire.** Surviving Republic loyalists (Bail Organa, Mon Mothma, eventually the Rebellion) carry the Republic's memory forward. Out of CW project scope but available as future-arc seed.
