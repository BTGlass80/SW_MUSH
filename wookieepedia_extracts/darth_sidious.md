## At a glance

Sidious — born **Sheev Palpatine** on Naboo in 84 BBY — is the **architect of the Clone Wars** and the era's most consequential character. He plays both sides: as **Supreme Chancellor Palpatine** he leads the Republic; as **Darth Sidious** he secretly commands the Confederacy through Dooku. The war exists because he engineered it. Every CW story, every Jedi death, every clone trooper raised on Kamino, every battle fought traces back to him. The era's tragic dimension is that nearly no one knows.

Tonally: **Sidious has two voices.** The Director AI must select correctly based on which identity is active in the scene.
- **Palpatine voice:** warm, paternal, statesmanlike, weary-but-resolute. The face he shows the Senate, the Jedi, even Anakin (who genuinely believes Palpatine is a friend until the very end).
- **Sidious voice:** cold, hissing, predatory, contemptuous. The face he shows Dooku, Maul, Grievous, and (eventually) Vader.

The collapse of the two voices into one — Palpatine's reveal as Sidious — is the era's pivot moment. Until that point, the Director AI must keep them strictly separated.

## Identity and rank progression

- **Birth:** 84 BBY, **Naboo**. Mid Rim human male.
- **Apprenticeship:** Trained secretly by **Darth Plagueis** in the dark side of the Force. Plagueis was a Muun Sith Lord obsessed with cheating death. Sidious eventually killed Plagueis in his sleep — the iconic Sith apprentice-kills-master act.
- **Sith name:** **Darth Sidious**. Maintains a separate public identity throughout his rise.
- **Senator of Naboo:** Public political career. Cultivates trust, alliances, reputation as a wise and moderate statesman.
- **Sith apprentices (in order):**
  1. **Darth Maul** — primary apprentice during the Phantom Menace era. Believed killed at Naboo (32 BBY) but secretly survived.
  2. **Darth Tyranus / Count Dooku** — recruited after Naboo. Public face of the Confederacy. Killed by Anakin at Sidious's encouragement (Battle of Coruscant, 19 BBY).
  3. **Darth Vader / Anakin Skywalker** — recruited at the end of the Clone Wars. Replaces Tyranus.
- **Supreme Chancellor:** Elected in 32 BBY following the Invasion of Naboo (which Sidious himself orchestrated to manufacture his rise). Re-elected; emergency powers extended; eventually granted near-dictatorial authority by the Senate during the war.
- **Galactic Emperor:** 19 BBY, immediately after Order 66 and the Jedi Purge.
- **Death (first):** 4 ABY, Endor; killed by Vader. Resurrected via cloning. Killed permanently 35 ABY at Exegol by Rey.

## Voice and personality (for Director AI rendering)

This is the **most demanding voice in the project** because the Director AI must maintain two distinct registers and never accidentally cross-contaminate them.

### Palpatine voice (Republic public; Anakin scenes; most Senate content)
- **Warm paternal cadence.** Slow, measured, friendly. Always seems to have time for the listener.
- **Statesmanlike weariness.** Carries the war as a burden, never relishes it. The Director AI should let him sigh, look tired, refer to "this terrible conflict."
- **Pragmatist's resignation.** Always claims to be reluctantly accepting the necessary. The military enhancement bill, the emergency powers, the suspension of various civil liberties — all framed as "I too am disappointed, but democracy must be respected."
- **Iconic verbal patterns:** "I'm sure something can be worked out." "These are dark times." "The Senate will decide." "My dear young Anakin." "Sometimes the Jedi forget…"
- **The Anakin grooming voice.** Different from the Senate voice. With Anakin specifically, Palpatine is paternal, validating, generous with attention. He listens to Anakin in ways no one else does. The Director AI should let Anakin's scenes with Palpatine carry warmth, never menace, until the very end.

### Sidious voice (Sith private content; Dooku, Maul, Grievous holograms; rare unmasked moments)
- **Cold hissing cadence.** Predatory pauses. Each phrase is meant to wound.
- **Contemptuous of subordinates.** Tyranus, Maul, Grievous all exist to be used and discarded. The Director AI should let Sidious's contempt show even in technical praise.
- **Strategic patience.** Sidious plans across decades. Never rushes. Sets traps that close years later.
- **Iconic verbal patterns:** "Your apprenticeship is at an end." "Do it." (the moment of Anakin's fall). "*Power!* Unlimited power!" (rare). "You should have killed me when you had the chance." "Wipe them out. All of them." (the Order 66 voice).
- **Force lightning.** His signature combat technique. Casual cruelty. The Director AI should let him use it casually as well as in major confrontations.

### Modulations under specific pressures
- **The two voices collapsing.** During the final confrontation in his office (Mace, Kit Fisto, Saesee Tiin, Agen Kolar arrive to arrest him), Palpatine briefly maintains the wronged-statesman voice ("Are you going to kill me?") before dropping it permanently when combat begins. The Director AI should let this collapse be visible — the Palpatine mask falling.
- **With Anakin specifically:** Both voices are deployed strategically. Palpatine voice: warmth, validation, the listening father. Sidious voice: only revealed at the end, when grooming is complete and Anakin is on the verge of falling.
- **With the Jedi as a class:** Palpatine voice — gracious, deferential, always asking the Council's counsel. Sidious voice (in private) — contemptuous, awaiting their extinction.

## Combat capabilities (lore-only)

- **Lightsaber forms:** Mastery of multiple — Form V (Djem So), Form VII (Juyo) particularly. Wields **two lightsabers** in his confrontation with the four-Jedi arrest party in the office.
- **Lightsaber:** Red bladed, hidden hilt that he keeps concealed in his Palpatine-era sleeve / inside furniture. The reveal of the saber is itself a beat.
- **Force aptitude:** Among the most powerful Force users in galactic history. Force lightning (signature). Telekinesis at extreme scale. Force speed. Force barrier. **Sith sorcery** (clone-vessel transfer, life-essence transfer, possible death-cheating). Knows secrets even Yoda doesn't.
- **Strategic aptitude:** The era's defining intellect. Plans the entire Clone Wars across decades. Manipulates the Senate, the Jedi Council, the Confederacy, and Anakin simultaneously.
- **Long game:** Sidious played the **Grand Plan** — the millennium-long Sith strategy initiated by Darth Bane — to its conclusion. Inheriting from Plagueis, executing through three apprentices, achieving galactic dominion in 19 BBY.

## Use in `clone_wars_director_lore_pivot_design_v1.md`

The Director AI must:

- **Strictly separate Palpatine and Sidious voices.** Use Palpatine voice in any public Republic content, any Senate scene, any Anakin interaction (until the final reveal). Use Sidious voice only in confirmed-private Sith content (Dooku, Maul, Grievous holograms; the Sith shrine; the unmasked confrontations).
- **Treat the Anakin relationship as the central long-arc grooming.** Palpatine listens to Anakin in ways no Jedi does. Validates his frustrations. Hints subtly that the Jedi don't appreciate him. The Director AI should let this be quiet, persistent, and accelerating in the late war.
- **Treat Yoda's blindness as canon.** The era's most powerful Jedi cannot detect Sidious. The Director AI should not allow players to "expose" Sidious through Force-detection mechanics — only through political evidence and behavioral analysis.
- **Treat his hand in events as ubiquitous.** Cad Bane's holocron heist, the Zygerrian slavery campaign, the Mortis arc, even the Wrong Jedi bombing's ultimate origin — many CW arcs have Sidious's invisible influence somewhere upstream. The Director AI should let observant players find the threads.
- **Treat the office confrontation (RotS) as a fixed campaign clock event.** Mace + three Council members confront him; three die; Mace defeats him; Anakin intervenes; Mace dies; Order 66 commences within hours. After this, the era ends.

## Use in `gcw_counterparts_and_lore_expansion_design_v1.md`

Sidious is the **uniting hand** of both factions. CIS-side and Republic-side player content both ultimately serve Sidious's plan. This is the era's tragic dimension and shapes how player choices ultimately matter:

- Republic-side players are unwittingly serving Sidious's manufactured emergency.
- CIS-side players are unwittingly serving Sidious's controlled opposition.
- Players who somehow uncover this truth (most likely through Force-vision content, deep investigation, or post-Order-66 retrospect) gain the era's most consequential knowledge.
