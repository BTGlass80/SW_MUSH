## At a glance

**Mandalore** is the **Mandalorian homeworld** in the Outer Rim — culturally, politically, and strategically pivotal to the Clone Wars. During the war it occupies a unique position: a **Council of Neutral Systems** signatory officially neutral in the conflict, yet internally fractured between the pacifist **New Mandalorians** (led by Duchess Satine Kryze), the militant **Death Watch** terrorist group, and (late-war) the **Shadow Collective** under Maul. Mandalore's CW arc is one of the era's most morally weighted — neutrality cannot survive contact with Sidious's plans.

For SW_MUSH purposes, Mandalore is **a major non-Republic non-CIS player content location** — the canonical model for "third-party" worlds caught between the war's factions, and the location of multiple key character arcs (Obi-Wan/Satine, Maul's Shadow Collective conquest, Ahsoka's Siege command).

## Geography and physical character

- **Location:** Outer Rim Territories. Mandalore Sector.
- **Climate:** Devastated. Once a lush world, Mandalore was rendered largely desolate by **ancient Mandalorian wars** — surface scorched into white desert by orbital bombardment millennia before the CW. By the CW era, most habitation is enclosed in **biodomes**.
- **Capital:** **Sundari** — domed New Mandalorian capital. Pristine white architecture; surface-shielded; clean political and cultural center.
- **Concordia:** Mandalore's largest moon. Site of significant Death Watch activity during the war. Mining operations, mountain hideouts.
- **Notable terrain:** White sand wastes outside the biodomes; mineral-rich subterranean caverns; the legendary **Great Forge** (Imperial Era ruins location, but the Mandalorians' ancestral metalworking heritage predates).
- **Beskar:** Mandalore's signature metal — extremely durable, lightsaber-resistant. Mined and forged on Mandalore for millennia.

## Political factions during the Clone Wars

The Director AI must distinguish three (and eventually four) factions sharing the world.

### 1. New Mandalorians (Duchess Satine Kryze's government)
- **Pacifist reformers.** Reject Mandalore's warrior tradition; pursue cultural reinvention as a peaceful, civilized society.
- **Government:** Led by **Duchess Satine Kryze** as elected ruler. Prime Minister **Almec** serves under her (later revealed corrupt). Senate seat held by **Tal Merrik** (also revealed corrupt; assassinated by Anakin during the *Coronet* incident).
- **Military:** Minimal. The New Mandalorian Royal Guard provides palace and city security; no significant offensive capability.
- **Stance in the war:** Officially neutral. Member of the **Council of Neutral Systems** which Satine helped found.
- **Internal corruption:** Almec's black-market food and supply operations (exposed by Ahsoka and the Royal Academy cadets including Korkie Kryze) demonstrate that even the pacifist government has rot.

### 2. Death Watch (Mandalorian splinter terrorist faction)
- **Militant traditionalists.** Reject Satine's pacifism; want to restore Mandalore's warrior culture and conquer the galaxy.
- **Leader:** **Pre Vizsla** — wields the **Darksaber**, the ancient Mandalorian black-bladed lightsaber.
- **Base:** Concordia (the moon) and various scattered camps.
- **Alliances:** Brief CIS alliance with Dooku (who provides arms), then broken when Dooku betrays them.
- **Internal split:** When Maul arrives, Pre Vizsla allies with him to seize Mandalore. After the conquest, Maul betrays Vizsla and kills him in single combat, claiming the Darksaber. **Bo-Katan Kryze** (Satine's sister, formerly Vizsla's lieutenant) refuses to serve Maul and splits off to form the **Nite Owls** — anti-Maul Death Watch resistance.

### 3. Shadow Collective (Maul's coalition, late CW)
- **Composition:** Maul + Savage Opress + co-opted Death Watch + Pyke Syndicate + Black Sun + Hutt Cartel.
- **Method:** Manufactured public outrage via staged terror attacks; Maul positions himself as the savior; installs Almec (released from prison) as puppet Prime Minister.
- **Duration:** Brief — months at most. Sidious arrives personally; kills Savage Opress in front of Maul; captures Maul. Bo-Katan and the Nite Owls operate in resistance.
- **End:** Maul escapes Sidious's custody during chaos; rules Mandalore briefly through Almec; eventually flees as Republic forces arrive for the Siege.

### 4. Republic Siege of Mandalore (final CW act, 19 BBY)
- **Siege command:** **Ahsoka Tano** (now ex-Jedi but operating with Republic backing) coordinates with **Bo-Katan Kryze** and the Nite Owls. **Captain Rex** and elements of the **501st Legion** are committed.
- **Objective:** Capture Maul; restore stable Mandalorian governance.
- **Outcome:** Maul captured. Almec killed during the campaign. Order 66 commences mid-extraction; the Republic forces fragment as clones turn on Ahsoka. Mandalore enters Imperial-era turmoil.

## Pre-war history (key Director AI background)

- **Pre-32 BBY:** Mandalorian civil war between New Mandalorian reformers and traditionalist clans. **Qui-Gon Jinn and his Padawan Obi-Wan Kenobi** spent **a year on Mandalore** protecting Satine Kryze from bounty hunters during this period (~41–39 BBY). Obi-Wan and Satine formed a forbidden romantic attachment; Kenobi nearly left the Order for her. The conflict killed most of Mandalore's population. After the war, Satine rebuilt the world without further Jedi assistance.
- **Aftermath:** New Mandalorian government takes power. Death Watch goes underground, regrouping for eventual return.

## Key Clone Wars events on Mandalore

- **The *Coronet* incident.** Obi-Wan and Anakin escort Satine aboard the diplomatic vessel *Coronet* to Coruscant; Senator Tal Merrik (a Death Watch sleeper) tries to deliver Satine to the CIS; Anakin kills Merrik to save Satine. Establishes Death Watch as active CIS-aligned threat.
- **The Almec corruption arc.** Ahsoka teaches at the Royal Academy of Government in Sundari; cadets (Korkie Kryze, Soniee, Lagos, Amis) discover Almec's black-market operation; Almec arrested.
- **The Maul takeover.** Vizsla's Death Watch allies with Maul's Shadow Collective; manufactured terror campaign; Maul arrives as "savior"; takeover of Sundari; Vizsla killed; Almec installed as puppet PM. Satine imprisoned.
- **Satine's death.** Maul kills Satine in front of Obi-Wan as personal revenge against Kenobi. Bo-Katan rescues Obi-Wan from execution. This is canonically the most personally devastating moment of Obi-Wan's life.
- **Sidious's arrival.** Sidious personally arrives on Mandalore, kills Savage Opress in single combat in front of Maul, captures Maul, departs. Almec rules Mandalore briefly as puppet.
- **The Siege of Mandalore (final CW arc).** Ahsoka, Bo-Katan, Rex, and Republic forces take Sundari. Maul captured. Order 66 fragments the campaign.

## Voice and culture (for Director AI rendering)

The Director AI must select Mandalorian voice register based on **which faction** the player encounters.

### New Mandalorian register
- **Cultured pacifist cadence.** Refined accents (Satine's specifically); careful diplomatic speech; idealistic language about peace and civilization.
- **The Mandalorian language (Mando'a).** Used ceremonially and culturally; New Mandalorians have largely set aside warrior idioms. Players hearing Mando'a from a New Mandalorian likely encounter ceremonial use.

### Death Watch register
- **Traditionalist warrior cadence.** Aggressive, contemptuous of pacifism, frequent Mando'a invocations, references to Mandalorian warrior history. Voice patterns: clipped commands, contemptuous descriptors for "soft" people, references to ancestral honor.
- **Pre Vizsla** specifically speaks with grim authority and the Darksaber as his physical signature.

### Bo-Katan Nite Owls register
- **Pragmatic warrior cadence.** Bo-Katan operates between New Mandalorian (her sister's faction) and Death Watch (her former faction). Voice: measured, tactical, neither pacifist nor zealot. Useful tonal middle ground.

### Shadow Collective / Maul register
- **Coercive intimidation.** Voice register from `wookieepedia_extracts/maul.md`. Mandalorians under Maul speak with subjugated wariness when he's present.

### Mandalorian baseline cultural traits (regardless of faction)
- **Beskar pride.** All Mandalorians treat the metal with reverence; armor is family heritage.
- **Clan identity.** Clan Vizsla, Clan Kryze, Clan Wren, Clan Saxon, Clan Awaud, etc. Each has distinct heraldry and history.
- **Helmets.** Worn ceremonially and tactically. Removal in public has variable cultural weight depending on era and faction.
- **Mythosaur.** Ancient near-mythical beast; symbolic of Mandalorian heritage. Stylized skull is the species's most ancient symbol.

## Use in `clone_wars_director_lore_pivot_design_v1.md`

The Director AI must:

- **Treat Mandalore as canonically neutral but contested.** The official New Mandalorian government's neutrality is real; the planet's actual political situation is anything but stable.
- **Voice each faction distinctly.** New Mandalorian, Death Watch, Nite Owls, Shadow Collective — all distinct.
- **Treat the Maul takeover and Satine's death as fixed campaign clock events.** Players in mid-CW Mandalore content can witness or affect surrounding events; the central pivots are canonical.
- **Treat the Siege of Mandalore (19 BBY) as the era's final Mandalore act.** Order 66 fragments the campaign mid-extraction. After this, Mandalore enters Imperial-Era turmoil out of project scope.
- **Allow Obi-Wan content with extra emotional weight.** Any Obi-Wan scene on Mandalore (especially involving Satine) should carry visible personal stakes.

## Use in `gcw_counterparts_and_lore_expansion_design_v1.md`

Mandalore models the **third-party world** mode of play — a faction outside both Republic and CIS, with its own internal politics, that players might engage with from any side. Useful concrete content:

- **Council of Neutral Systems** member content — diplomatic missions, neutrality enforcement, refugee aid.
- **Death Watch insurgency** content — terror operations, CIS-supplied weapons, Concordia mountain hideouts.
- **Shadow Collective late-war takeover** content — Pyke Syndicate spice operations on Mandalore, Black Sun arms supplies, Hutt liaison work.
- **Player-character Mandalorian roles.** A New Mandalorian Royal Guard officer, a Death Watch warrior, a Nite Owl resistance fighter, a Mandalorian bounty hunter operating independently — all canonical.

## Use in `from_dust_to_stars_design_v2_clone_wars.md`

Mandalore is the **canonical Outer Rim third-party world** for the design. Useful traits:

- Domed cities (atmospheric design distinctive — Sundari is iconic).
- Subterranean and lunar (Concordia) habitation.
- Beskar mining as an economic and strategic resource.
- Position on the war's edge as a contested-but-officially-neutral player.
