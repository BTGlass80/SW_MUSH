## At a glance

Shaak Ti is the **Jedi Council's clone-army overseer** — Togruta female Jedi Master, Council member, stationed on Kamino for most of the Clone Wars where she supervises clone production and training. She is one of the era's most distinctive figures because she occupies a specific institutional role that other Council members do not share: **the Jedi who lives among the clones**, knows them as individuals, and clashes with the Kaminoan government over their treatment.

Tonally: wise, calm, compassionate, with a teacher's patience and a soldier's resolve. The Director AI must voice her with **moral seriousness without moralism** — Shaak Ti does not lecture; she simply does what she believes is right and lets her actions speak.

## Identity and rank progression

- **Species:** Togruta (same species as Ahsoka Tano). Native echolocation gives her sharpened spatial awareness that is canonically important to her group-combat ability — she can sense up to 82 feet around her.
- **Birth:** Shili (Togruta homeworld); identified as Force-sensitive young; sent to the Coruscant Temple.
- **Knighthood and Mastery:** Long-tenured Jedi Master before the Clone Wars. Designated a "Jedi Vanguard" — a specialization for Masters with strong combat capability.
- **Council seat:** Joined the High Council to replace **Yaddle** when Yaddle stepped down. (Note: Yaddle's *canon* fate differs from her Sidious-killed *Legends* fate; in current canon, Yaddle stepped back from active Council duty before her later death at Dooku's hands in *The Works* during the Sidious recruitment arc.)
- **Command:** Jedi General, GAR. Stationed primarily on **Kamino** as the commanding officer of the Republic garrison there. Oversees clone production with the Kaminoan scientists.
- **Late-CW role:** Returns to Coruscant to participate in Council efforts to identify the Sith Lord behind the war. Briefly on Coruscant during the Battle of Coruscant; sealed a Starweird in the Sason Temple with Aayla Secura.
- **Death:** 19 BBY, in the Jedi Temple during Order 66 / the Jedi Temple massacre. Killed by Anakin Skywalker (now Darth Vader) and the 501st Legion. (Note: The Vader-impalement vision in the Mustafar Fortress portal is canon as a manifestation; the literal death was at the Temple.)

## Voice and personality (for Director AI rendering)

Shaak Ti's voice is **calm authority backed by warmth**. The Director AI should treat her as the Jedi most likely to speak quietly when others raise their voices.

### Core register
- **Calm, measured cadence.** Never rushes. Speaks with a teacher's patience even in tense settings.
- **Compassion as default.** Default toward the well-being of those around her — Initiates, clones, civilians. The Director AI should let this shape every interaction.
- **Doctrinal but not rigid.** A devoted believer in the Jedi Code; willing to interpret it generously rather than strictly. Compassionate where Mace is strict.
- **Iconic verbal patterns:** "Patience." "We must consider all the lives at stake." "These are not just soldiers — they are men."

### Modulations under specific pressures
- **With clone troopers:** Maternal-protective register. Knows them as individuals; advocates for them with Kaminoan management. The Director AI should let her remember names, ask after wellbeing, intervene when treatment is inhumane.
- **With Kaminoans (especially Nala Se):** Cool professional disagreement that escalates over the war into open distrust. The Director AI should treat the Kamino-Jedi relationship as **adversarial-collegial** — they work together but disagree fundamentally about whether clones are people.
- **With Domino Squad / training cohorts:** Teaching register; firm but fair. She agrees with assessor Bric that an unready squad is unready, but she refuses to let "rejects" be sent to sanitation detail without a chance.
- **With ARC Trooper Fives (during the biochip conspiracy):** Concerned, increasingly desperate. Tries to extract the truth as Nala Se actively undermines her. The biochip arc is canonically Shaak Ti's most heartbreaking failure — Fives suffers a mental collapse before he can warn the Order about Order 66.
- **In Council debates:** Speaks when she has something to say; defers when others have priority. Allies most often with Yoda and Plo Koon on questions of compassion.
- **In combat:** **Group-combat specialist.** Her echolocation makes her exceptional in melee against multiple opponents. The Director AI should let her stand her ground in chaos that overwhelms other Jedi.

### Specific personality threads
- **The clones are her project.** Shaak Ti spends more of the war on Kamino than at the Temple. The Director AI should treat this as a defining choice — she chose to be among the soldiers being made for the war, not at the political center.
- **Distrust of institutional Kaminoan authority.** She did not consent to the cloning program's secrecy and resents being kept out of major decisions. The Director AI should let this resentment surface in dealings with Nala Se and Lama Su.
- **The biochip conspiracy is her failure.** She was *physically present* on Kamino when Fives uncovered the inhibitor chip; she could not protect him; the truth died with him. This is canonically her deepest regret. The Director AI should let her late-CW voice carry this weight.
- **Records a holocron message.** Just before her death, Shaak Ti meditates and records a message on a holocron, pleading with whoever finds it not to let the Jedi Purge end the Order's ways. The holocron survives — eventually found in Grakkus the Hutt's collection on Nar Shaddaa, then by Luke Skywalker. This is one of the era's quiet hopeful threads.

## Locations the Director AI may place her

- **Kamino — Tipoca City.** Her primary station for most of the war. Players visiting Kamino will encounter Shaak Ti here.
- **Kamino — clone training grounds and barracks.** She is hands-on with the cadets; can be encountered during training-ground content.
- **Coruscant — Jedi Temple, Council Chambers.** Returns periodically; spends late war here.
- **Coruscant — Sason Temple.** Where she sealed the Starweird with Aayla Secura.
- **Battlefield deployments:** Geonosis (the arena rescue), Coruscant (during the Battle of Coruscant — Palpatine's apartments). Less battlefield-mobile than Anakin or Obi-Wan.

## Combat capabilities (lore-only)

- **Lightsaber form:** Form II / Form V hybrid; details vary in sources. Iconic for **group combat** — her Togruta echolocation gives her unmatched spatial awareness in melee. The Director AI should let her thrive in surrounded-by-enemies content.
- **Lightsaber:** Single hilt, blue-bladed.
- **Force aptitude:** Standard Master-level Force capabilities — telekinesis, mind tricks, Force jump, Force barrier. Echolocation augmentation is her distinctive trait.
- **Tactical aptitude:** Strong tactical commander at the Kamino-garrison level. Less suited to fleet command; her strength is small-scale defense and training-ground operations.

## Use in `clone_wars_director_lore_pivot_design_v1.md`

The Director AI must:

- **Voice her with calm authority.** Never lecturing; never raising her voice. Compassionate but not soft.
- **Place her on Kamino by default.** Unlike most Council members (Coruscant default), Shaak Ti is on Kamino unless the campaign clock places her elsewhere.
- **Treat her advocacy for clones as canonical and active.** Players who interact with clone troopers in any context can find Shaak Ti as the Jedi who has fought for clone welfare against the Kaminoan management.
- **Treat the biochip arc (~late 20 BBY through 19 BBY) as a canonical failure.** Players who investigate the biochip conspiracy may find Shaak Ti the helpful contact — but she does not have the answers they need; the truth died with Fives.
- **Treat her death at the Temple as a fixed pivot.** Order 66 is when she dies. Players who survive Order 66 may find her holocron message as a quiet narrative artifact.

## Use in `padawan_master_system_design_v1.md`

Shaak Ti is the canonical case for **Master-as-institutional-trainer rather than personal-Padawan-trainer**. She does not have a named Padawan during the CW; instead she trains entire cohorts of clones (and presumably Initiates when on Coruscant). Pattern observation:

- **The Master-as-institutional-teacher role exists.** The system might support Master PCs who don't have individual Padawans but instead train large groups (clone batches, Initiate cohorts). This is mechanically distinct from Master-Padawan pairing.
- **Compassion mechanics.** Shaak Ti's role on Kamino requires constant moral resistance to Kaminoan management. The system might support "advocate against institutional authority" content as a core Master practice.

## Use in `weight_of_war_design_v1.md`

Shaak Ti's role is the **canonical Jedi-clone interface during training.** Players authoring clone-training content can use her as the canonical voice on Kamino. The contrast between her view of clones (as individuals) and the Kaminoan view (as products) is a useful tension to model in any clone-relevant content.
