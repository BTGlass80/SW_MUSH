## At a glance

**Dex's Diner** is a small eatery in **CoCo Town** (Collective Commerce Town) — a dilapidated middle-class industrial district of Coruscant. Owned and operated by **Dexter Jettster**, a four-armed Besalisk with a colorful prospecting and travel background and a long friendship with Obi-Wan Kenobi. The diner is one of the era's most useful **canonical underworld-adjacent NPC contact locations** — Obi-Wan visits it explicitly when he needs information the Jedi Archives can't provide.

For SW_MUSH purposes, Dex's Diner is the **canonical Coruscant working-class location for player content** — the place to meet a non-Jedi non-Senate NPC contact for casual information, food, drink, and conversation that doesn't carry institutional weight.

## Location and physical character

- **District:** CoCo Town (Collective Commerce Town) — a middle-class commercial and light-industrial district. Dilapidated but functional. Working-class population.
- **Neighborhood character:** Dingy, busy, multi-species. Skylanes and pedestrian traffic. Workshops and small businesses surround the diner. Smell of grease, stim-coffee, and machine oil.
- **The diner itself:** Small. Counter seating; a row of booths. Open kitchen behind the counter — Dex cooks visibly. Window-side seating with views of the ambient industrial street. Standard Coruscant temperature-controlled interior; warm.

## Staff

- **Dexter "Dex" Jettster** — Besalisk owner-operator. Four arms (Besalisk biology); large, jovial, weathered face. Pre-diner career: prospector, traveler, smuggler-adjacent associate. Long friendship with Obi-Wan Kenobi. Kept his Outer Rim contacts and knowledge after settling on Coruscant. The Director AI must voice Dex with **warm working-class friendliness** — slightly gruff, instantly affectionate to friends, willing to share information with people he trusts.
- **Wanda Bagwa** — human waitress (sister to Hermione).
- **Hermione Bagwa** — human waitress (sister to Wanda).
- **FLO** — WA-7 waitress droid. The Bagwa sisters' droid partner. Iconic for her hover-base and disc-tray-arm design.

## Menu and pricing (canonical detail)

- **Ardees ("Jawa Juice")** — freshly brewed; the diner's signature beverage.
- **Pie by the slice** — 2.5 credits per slice.
- **Photon Fizzle** — 1.7 credits.
- **Home-style cooked meals** primarily. Hearty rather than refined.
- **Credit chips accepted.**

The Director AI may use these prices as canonical anchors for any Coruscant economy player content.

## Clone Wars-era status

- **Active throughout the war.** The diner remains open during the CW; serves a steady working-class clientele plus occasional Jedi (specifically Obi-Wan).
- **Notable Obi-Wan visit (~22 BBY).** **Shortly before the outbreak of the Clone Wars**, Obi-Wan visits Dex with the Kamino saberdart from the Zam Wesell pursuit. Dex identifies it as a Kamino-manufactured weapon — knowledge the Jedi Archives lack. **This single conversation triggers Obi-Wan's discovery of the clone army and the entire Clone Wars investigation.** The diner is therefore canonically the location that exposed Kamino to the Republic.
- **Recurring use as Obi-Wan's contact.** Throughout the war, Obi-Wan canonically returns to Dex's Diner when he needs informal Outer Rim or underworld knowledge. The diner serves as his "non-Jedi consultation point."
- **Closure (post-CW or late-CW).** The diner closes at some point — Dex presumes temporary closure but never reopens it. By the New Republic Era it is abandoned. The exact CW-era closure timing is not specified; players may encounter the diner functional throughout the war proper.

## Voice and culture (for Director AI rendering)

### Dex Jettster register
- **Jovial, gruff, working-class warmth.** Talks loudly. Laughs often. Tells stories about prospecting in the Outer Rim. The Director AI should voice Dex like a barkeep who's seen everything and still likes the company.
- **Affectionate to old friends.** With Obi-Wan specifically, Dex drops formality entirely — wraps him in a four-armed hug, calls him "Obi-Wan, old buddy." This warmth is canonical and useful.
- **Willing to share information.** Dex's value as an NPC is that he knows things the institutional channels don't. He'll talk readily to people he trusts, but he protects friends — won't sell out a contact for profit.
- **Iconic verbal patterns:** "Obi-Wan! Old buddy!" "Whaddya need to know?" "Now, that didn't come from any factory I know..."

### Bagwa sisters / FLO register
- **Working-class busy banter.** The waitresses are friendly, professional, occasionally exasperated. FLO is a chipper service droid with patter that mixes service prompts and observations.

### General customer register
- **Multi-species working-class.** Construction droids' operators, mechanics, light-industrial workers, occasional underworld characters. The clientele establishes that this is a real working-class space, not a tourist attraction.

## Use in `coruscant_underworld_landmarks_design_v1.md`

Dex's Diner is **one of the era's three or four most useful Coruscant landmark locations** for player content. Specific uses:

- **Obi-Wan's underworld-information contact.** Players who consult Obi-Wan about Outer Rim or underworld matters may be told, "Let me speak with an old friend. Meet me at Dex's."
- **Player NPC contact location.** Dex Jettster himself is a hireable information broker — Besalisk pre-prospector with extensive Outer Rim contacts. Player content involving exotic weapons, obscure planets, smuggling routes, or rare materials can route through Dex.
- **A "warm" Coruscant location.** Most canonical Coruscant locations carry institutional weight (Senate, Temple, Chancellor's Office). Dex's Diner is one of the few warm, casual, non-institutional places. Players who need a breather location have Dex's.
- **Crossover contact point.** Different player parties might both have reasons to be at Dex's; the diner can serve as a player-to-player contact node.
- **The Kamino saberdart conversation** — canonical pre-war scene. Players who research the war's origins may eventually trace the discovery back to this diner.

## Use in `clone_wars_director_lore_pivot_design_v1.md`

The Director AI must:

- **Voice Dex with consistent warmth.** Don't make him gruff-and-suspicious-by-default. Dex is genuinely friendly; players earning his trust earn real assistance.
- **Treat the diner as Obi-Wan's preferred non-Temple location.** When Obi-Wan needs to think, eat, or get away from the Council briefly, he goes to Dex's.
- **Allow it as a recurring meeting place.** Player parties can use Dex's Diner as a recurring rendezvous point without Dex's hospitality wearing thin.
- **Treat Dex's information as reliable but Outer-Rim-flavored.** He knows what he knows because he's *been there*; the knowledge is practical, not academic. Useful for distinguishing his info from Jedi Archive material.
