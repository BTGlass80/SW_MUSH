## At a glance

The **Korunnai** (singular, "Korun") are a **race of Force-sensitive humans** native to the planet **Haruun Kal**. **Mace Windu's people** — his Korunnai heritage is one of the canonical "Force-using traditions outside the Jedi Order" referenced in the Clone Wars Campaign Guide §4. The Korunnai call the Force ***Pelekotan***, organize themselves into tribal clans called **Ghôsh**, and hold to a four-pillar code centered on **Herd, Family, Duty, and Honor** (in inverse order of importance — Herd above all).

For SW_MUSH purposes, the Korunnai are the **canonical "Force-using non-Jedi tradition" reference** for the project — useful for any player content involving alternate Force-using cultures, Force traditions outside the Order, or Mace Windu's heritage specifically. They sit alongside the Nightsisters of Dathomir and the Bando Gora as the era's catalog of non-Jedi Force-using groups.

## Identity and physiology

- **Species:** Genetically human, with two important biological/Force distinctions:
  1. **Universal Force-sensitivity.** All Korunnai are Force-sensitive. Not "some" — the entire population. This makes them unique among humans.
  2. **Toxin tolerance.** Higher resistance to poisons than baseline humans, due to evolutionary adaptation to the toxic cloudsea covering Haruun Kal's lowlands.
- **Origin myth:** Tradition holds that **a Jedi craft crashed on Haruun Kal during the Sith War** (millennia pre-CW), and the Korunnai are descendants of those marooned Jedi. This origin would explain the universal Force-sensitivity. Whether the myth is literally true is canonically unclear; what matters is that Korunnai believe it and shape their identity around the connection.
- **Homeworld:** **Haruun Kal** — a humid, jungle-and-toxic-cloudsea world. Korunnai live in the highlands ("uplanders"); the **Balawai** (lowlanders, non-Force-sensitive humans who arrived later as colonists) live in the lowland cities.
- **Self-name:** "Korunnai" translates roughly as "uplanders." The Balawai's insulting term for them is "**Kornos**."

## The Pelekotan (Korunnai Force-tradition)

- **Pelekotan** is the Korunnai term for **the Force itself** — but the conception differs subtly from the Jedi understanding. The Pelekotan emphasizes:
  - **Bond with living creatures.** Korunnai use their Force-sensitivity to bind **akk dogs** (large quadrupedal predators) and **grassers** (their primary herd animals) to themselves as lifelong companions and herd-guardians. This is canonical core Korunnai Force-practice.
  - **Connection to the wild.** Pelekotan is more visceral and ecological than the Jedi's contemplative-ethical framing. The Korunnai feel the Force in plants, weather, predators, and prey.
  - **No formal training tradition equivalent to the Jedi Order.** Korunnai children grow up in a Force-aware culture without specific institutional pedagogy. Practice is transmitted through family and Ghôsh, not academies.

## The Four Pillars (Korunnai code)

In **inverse order of importance** — the higher pillar overrides the lower when they conflict:

1. **Honor** (lowest) — Obligation to oneself. Honesty, courage, loving without reservation, integrity. Foundation but not supreme.
2. **Duty** — Obligation to others. Doing one's job, working hard, obeying elders, standing by one's Ghôsh.
3. **Family** — Caring for parents, teaching children, defending family blood.
4. **Herd** (highest) — Literal: the grasser herds on which Ghôsh life depends. **Nothing outranks the Herd.** If saving the herd requires sacrificing honor, the Korunnai sacrifices honor. If it requires shirking duty, the Korunnai shirks duty. If it requires breaking with family, the Korunnai breaks with family.

This **survival-pragmatic ethical framework** is canonically distinct from the Jedi Code's idealism and from the Sith Code's individual-power focus. The Korunnai code is **collectivist and ecological**.

## Society and culture

- **Ghôsh organization.** The Ghôsh is the basic Korunnai social unit — extended-family-clan combining biological relatives and adopted kin. **Ghôsh Windu** (Mace Windu's clan) and **Ghôsh Vastor** (Kar Vastor's; Vastor and Windu are *Dôshalo* — clan-brothers) are canonical examples.
- **Language:** **Korunnal**, distinct from Galactic Basic. Mace Windu canonically speaks both.
- **Idiomatic detail:** A Korunnai shrug is a head-tilt of one centimeter to the right. The phrase for nonsense is "tusker poop."
- **Distance measurement:** **By transit time, not by length.** A Korunnai says "two days' walk" rather than "twenty kilometers." Useful for Director AI dialogue authenticity.
- **The Summertime War.** Pre-CW conflict between Korunnai uplanders and Balawai lowlanders. Long-running, generational, brutal. Mace Windu witnessed and participated in some phase of this conflict in his youth or family history; the war shapes Korunnai cultural consciousness.

## Voice and culture (for Director AI rendering)

### Korunnai register
- **Practical, weathered, blunt.** Korunnai speech is canonically less ornate than Jedi-Master cadence. Direct statements, minimal flourishes.
- **Force-aware vocabulary.** Korunnai speak of the Pelekotan in concrete terms (the Force lives in *that grasser*, in *this storm*, in *that weapon*). The Director AI should let their dialogue feel embodied rather than abstract.
- **Code-invocation under stress.** Under pressure, Korunnai may invoke the Four Pillars to justify decisions — particularly the Herd-pillar's primacy. Useful for character-conflict moments.
- **Idiom anchor:** "Tusker poop" is the canonical nonsense-phrase. "Walk of two suns" or similar transit-time language replaces standard distance measurements.

### Mace Windu's Korunnai aspects
- **Subtle in Jedi contexts.** Mace's Korunnai heritage is canonically present but not foregrounded in standard Jedi Council dialogue. Players who probe his background will find him willing to discuss it but reluctant to dwell on it.
- **Activated in Pelekotan-relevant content.** When Mace encounters Korunnai-related issues (Bando Gora, alternative Force-traditions, Haruun Kal events), his Korunnai voice surfaces. He may use Korunnal idiom or invoke the Four Pillars informally.

## Use in `force_resonant_landmarks_design_v1.md`

The Korunnai and the Pelekotan are the **canonical "Force-using tradition outside the Order" reference** for the design. Useful concrete content:

- **Haruun Kal as a Force-resonant landmark.** A whole world where the population is Force-sensitive; canonical Jedi-craft-crash legend; Pelekotan tradition active among the inhabitants. The planet itself is "alive" in the Force in a different mode than Dagobah's wild resonance.
- **Akk dog / grasser bonding** as a tradition-specific Force-practice. Players who learn from Korunnai may acquire animal-bonding capabilities distinct from Jedi mind-trick / animal-influence.
- **The Pelekotan as a competing Force-philosophy.** Players may find Korunnai teachings valuable as a "third path" alongside Jedi and Sith — pragmatic, embodied, herd-prioritizing.
- **Mace Windu's heritage as deep lore.** Players who go to Haruun Kal may find Mace's family Ghôsh, his clan-brother (or his memory), and Korunnai who knew him as a child.

## Use in `clone_wars_director_lore_pivot_design_v1.md`

The Director AI must:

- **Voice Korunnai with practical-blunt cadence.** Avoid Jedi-Master ornateness and avoid generic "tribal native" tropes.
- **Treat the Pelekotan as a sincere Force-philosophy.** Don't reduce it to "the Korun word for Force." It's a different framing of the same energy.
- **Treat Korunnai universal Force-sensitivity as canonical.** Every Korun encounter is an encounter with a Force-aware being.
- **Allow Korunnai content for player character backgrounds.** A player Korunnai PC could plausibly exist — a Force-sensitive human with non-Jedi training, native to Haruun Kal, who arrived on Coruscant or in Republic territory by other means.
