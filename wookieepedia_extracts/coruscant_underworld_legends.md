## Useful Legends-era detail

### 1. Mutant species evolved in the lower levels
- **Cthon** — humanoid mutant species; canonical Legends-era inhabitants of the deepest underworld levels. Live in tunnel systems below the lit areas. Cannibalistic; predatory; rarely seen.
- **Corridor ghouls** — Legends-era mutants haunting infrastructure tunnels. Atmospheric content for sewer/maintenance-corridor encounters.
- **Other minor mutant species** evolved across millennia in the buried lower levels. Useful as antagonist content for player exploration of the deepest levels.

### 2. The buried bottom fifty levels
The lowest fifty levels of the original surface city are now buried under duracrete and successive construction. This means:
- The deepest "underworld" content is canonically inaccessible to most travel.
- Specialized passage requires demolition, ventilation-shaft access, or knowledge of forgotten routes.
- These deepest layers are essentially **archaeological** — the Coruscant of millennia ago, preserved beneath modern construction.

Useful as a layered-exploration mechanic: surface → upper city → mid-city → underworld → lower underworld → buried original surface.

### 3. Locked-down blast-door communities
The lowest levels canonically have such extreme crime rates that government decree has **sealed off entire communities** behind massive blast doors. These doors:
- Are voice-passcode operated (the canonical phrase "Coruscant has lovely sunsets" was once used).
- Are operated by intellectual government agents stationed at access points.
- Quarantine specific areas of poverty and violence from the rest of the city.

Useful Director AI content for high-stakes underworld arcs: getting past a blast-door requires either passcode knowledge, official authority, or coercion of the door operator.

### 4. The Padmé Amidala / Captain Panaka underworld incident
A canonical Legends event: when Padmé visited Coruscant as Queen of Naboo, mercenaries took her hostage in a sealed-off lower community. Captain Quarsh Panaka (her head of security) gained access by holding the door operator at gunpoint, fought through hostile inhabitants to recover the Queen, and re-sealed the community on exit using the passcode phrase "Coruscant has lovely sunsets."

This event is **Legends-canon** (i.e., not part of current canon) but provides:
- **Template content** for "VIP rescue from sealed underworld community" missions.
- **Atmospheric detail** about how the blast-door community system works.
- **Naboo/Panaka backstory** that can be re-skinned for non-Padmé player content.

### 5. Black Sun underworld presence
**District Mayor Acros-Krik** — Legends-era Black Sun ally who held formal civic authority while serving the syndicate. Useful Director AI content for layered governance: official civic offices in the underworld are often controlled by criminal syndicates wearing legitimate-authority masks.

The general pattern (Black Sun controlling middle-management civic positions through bribery and threat) is **broadly compatible with canon** even where Acros-Krik specifically is Legends.

### 6. Jedi Purge refuge
In the Imperial Era (post-19 BBY), the underworld becomes a **refuge for Jedi Purge survivors and dissidents**. While this is out of CW project scope, it informs late-CW / immediately-post-Order-66 content: a Jedi who survives the Temple massacre would naturally flee to the underworld. Players running Order-66-survivor characters might use the underworld as an escape destination.
