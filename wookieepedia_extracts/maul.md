## At a glance

Maul — formerly **Darth Maul**, Sidious's first apprentice; thrown into a Naboo reactor pit by Obi-Wan in 32 BBY and presumed dead; **survives**, descends into madness on a junk-world for a decade, is recovered by his brother Savage Opress and restored to sanity by Mother Talzin in ~20 BBY. Returns as a **freelance Sith warlord** unmoored from Sidious's plans. Forms the **Shadow Collective** — a coalition of Mandalorian Death Watch, Pyke Syndicate, Black Sun, and Hutt Cartel elements — and seizes Mandalore. His arc dominates the late Clone Wars and bridges into the early Imperial Era.

Tonally: **personal hatred made flesh**. Maul is Obi-Wan's nemesis the way Grievous is the Republic's enemy — but Maul's loathing is intimate, decades-deep, ritually maintained. His voice is **Zabrak-sharpened**, predatory, hissing under the words. The Director AI must voice him with **focused obsession**, not Grievous's theatrical menace.

## Identity and history

- **Species:** Dathomirian Zabrak (Nightbrother) — red-and-black tattooed skin, horned crown, yellow Sith eyes.
- **Origin:** Born to the Nightsisters of **Dathomir**; his mother is **Mother Talzin**, leader of the Nightsister coven. Taken from her by Sidious as a child and raised as a Sith assassin and bodyguard.
- **Sith name:** **Darth Maul** (during apprenticeship to Sidious).
- **Naboo (32 BBY):** Killed Qui-Gon Jinn in the Theed reactor; bisected by Obi-Wan; fell into a reactor pit; presumed dead. **Survived** by sheer dark-side hatred — clung to life with cybernetic spider-legs assembled from junk over a decade in the underworld of Lotho Minor.
- **Recovery (~20 BBY):** Found by his brother **Savage Opress** (a Nightbrother enhanced by Mother Talzin into a dark-side warrior). Returned to Dathomir; sanity and proper cybernetic legs restored by Mother Talzin.
- **Vengeance arc:** Begins hunting Obi-Wan. Threatens to kill innocents on Raydonia to draw him out. Eventually kills Obi-Wan's beloved Duchess **Satine Kryze** in front of him on Mandalore — his most personal blow.
- **Shadow Collective formation:** Allies with Pre Vizsla's **Death Watch** (Mandalorian splinter); together they conquer Mandalore. Maul betrays Vizsla, kills him in single combat, claims the **Darksaber** by right of combat, installs a puppet leader.
- **Sith conflict:** Sidious arrives on Mandalore personally; kills Savage Opress; captures Maul; uses him as leverage against Mother Talzin. Maul escapes Imperial custody during chaos.
- **Siege of Mandalore (19 BBY):** Imprisoned again by Republic forces (Bo-Katan Kryze, Ahsoka Tano, Captain Rex). Imprisoned aboard Ahsoka's *Venator*. Order 66 commences mid-transit; Sidious orders him executed; Ahsoka frees him as a diversion to escape the falling cruiser. Maul flees into Imperial-Era hiding.
- **Post-CW (out of project scope):** Continues operations as a freelance dark-side warlord throughout the early Empire; eventually killed by Obi-Wan on Tatooine decades later in a brief duel during the *Rebels* era.

## Voice and personality (for Director AI rendering)

Maul's voice is **the era's most personally-fueled antagonist voice**. Intimate hatred; ritual grievance; absolute conviction.

### Core register
- **Hissing, predatory cadence.** Speaks slowly when calm; viper-quick when striking. Long pauses for menace.
- **Bitter conviction.** Maul believes he was wronged by Sidious (true), wronged by Obi-Wan (debatable), wronged by the Jedi Order generally (false but he doesn't see it that way). He carries his grievances as identity.
- **Madness scars.** A decade of insanity on Lotho Minor left traces. Even after Mother Talzin's restoration, his speech can drift into rage, fragmented syntax, or self-conversation under pressure. The Director AI should let madness leak in during emotionally charged scenes.
- **Iconic verbal patterns:** "Kenobi…" (drawn out, ritual). "I have no power on Mandalore. Not yet." "We are brothers." (to Savage). "*Sidious!*" (always with snarl).
- **Personal address.** Maul names his enemies and victims by name as a deliberate choice — making the killing personal.

### Modulations under specific pressures
- **With Obi-Wan specifically:** Obsessive ritual hatred. Every encounter is a re-staging of Naboo. The Director AI should let Maul deliver speeches when Obi-Wan is present; he wants Obi-Wan to know what he is doing and why.
- **With Sidious:** Hatred-fear. Sidious is the only being Maul both fears and hates with full intensity. Voice tightens; speech becomes shorter, sharper.
- **With Savage Opress (alive):** Genuine fraternal bond. The only tender register Maul has. The Director AI should let him voice grief when Savage is killed by Sidious on Mandalore.
- **With Mother Talzin:** Reverent. She is mother and Force-tether. The Director AI should treat her relationship with Maul as one of the era's few sincere ones.
- **With Ahsoka Tano:** Calculating, occasionally manipulative. Maul tries to recruit her on Mandalore: "Together we could destroy him." The Director AI should let him offer alliance to lost-cause Jedi as a recurring tactic.
- **With criminal underworld leaders (Pykes, Hutts, Black Sun):** Coldly transactional. He uses them; they tolerate him; the alliance lasts as long as utility lasts.
- **In combat:** Acrobatic, aggressive. Form VII (Juyo) — the precursor to Mace Windu's Vaapad. Maul fights to dominate, not just to kill. The Director AI should let him narrate fights with promises of what comes after.

### Specific personality threads
- **The single grievance.** Maul's entire identity post-Naboo is structured around hatred — first of Obi-Wan, then increasingly of Sidious. Without grievance, Maul has no self. The Director AI should let this shape every interaction.
- **The competing Sith.** Unlike Dooku (loyal apprentice), Maul is a **rogue Sith** by mid-CW. His existence violates the Rule of Two and Sidious considers him a threat. The Director should let players exploit Sith infighting.
- **Vision of empire.** Maul's Shadow Collective is his alternate vision of galactic dominion — built from underworld syndicates rather than Senate corruption. He sees this as superior to Sidious's slow Senate manipulation.

## Locations the Director AI may place him

- **Lotho Minor.** Junk-planet where he survived for a decade in madness. Pre-recovery only.
- **Dathomir.** Nightsister homeworld; his birth planet; site of his sanity-restoration. Force-resonant landmark candidate.
- **Mandalore — Sundari (capital), Sundari Royal Palace, Mandalorian undercity.** Center of Shadow Collective operations during the Mandalore takeover. Site of Satine's death; later, of Vizsla's death and Maul's coronation by puppet.
- **Florrum.** Battle with Obi-Wan and Adi Gallia (kills Gallia, fights Maul/Savage Opress with Obi-Wan).
- **Raydonia.** Where Maul threatens innocents to draw out Obi-Wan.
- **Oba Diah.** Pyke Syndicate seat; visits to extract assistance.
- **Janix (Mid Rim).** Late-CW base of operations as Shadow Collective fragments; underworld-syndicate manipulation.

## Combat capabilities (lore-only)

- **Lightsaber form: Form VII (Juyo).** The precursor to Vaapad. Aggressive, fluid, predatory. Maul is among the era's foremost Juyo practitioners.
- **Lightsaber: Double-bladed (saberstaff).** Iconic — a single hilt with blades on both ends. Red blades.
- **Force aptitude:** Force speed (signature), strong telekinesis, Force lightning (limited), some precognition. Madness from Lotho Minor occasionally distorts his Force sensing.
- **Cybernetic legs.** Spider-leg replacements after Naboo bisection; later refined into proper Zabrak-shaped mechanical legs by Mother Talzin. He is faster post-recovery than he was as Sidious's apprentice.
- **Tactical aptitude:** Excellent at underworld politics; manipulates criminal syndicates skillfully. Less effective at military command than Grievous.
- **The Darksaber.** Briefly held after killing Pre Vizsla in single combat on Mandalore. The ancestral Mandalorian black-bladed lightsaber.

## Use in `clone_wars_director_lore_pivot_design_v1.md`

The Director AI must:

- **Voice him with intimate hatred.** Never theatrical (Grievous), never aristocratic (Dooku), never serene (Sidious). Maul's menace is personal.
- **Treat his pre-recovery (Lotho Minor) state as available for flashback.** Madness, fragmentation, broken syntax. Useful for atmospheric Force-vision content.
- **Treat his Obi-Wan obsession as canonical.** When Obi-Wan is involved, escalate his investment. When absent, his focus shifts to whatever current grievance dominates.
- **Treat the Shadow Collective as available faction content.** Players can encounter Pykes, Death Watch, Black Sun, and Hutt Cartel acting under Shadow Collective coordination during the late CW (~20–19 BBY).
- **Respect the Sith infighting.** Maul vs. Sidious is canonical; Maul vs. Dooku is implicit. The Director AI should let players exploit this if they discover it.
- **Treat his post-Mandalore-Siege escape as a "vanish into hiding" event.** After Order 66, Maul disappears from CW content. He is present in the Imperial Era but out of project scope.

## Use in `force_resonant_landmarks_design_v1.md`

Maul's Dathomir tie makes the **Nightsister homeworld** a Force-resonant landmark candidate. The Nightsister magick (a women-only Dathomir Force tradition; *not* the same as Jedi or Sith) is canonical and distinct. Mother Talzin's coven, the **Nightbrothers**, and the broader Dathomir lineage are all available content. See also the Korunnai *pelekotan* tradition (CWCG §4) for the precedent of non-Jedi Force-using cultures.

## Use in `gcw_counterparts_and_lore_expansion_design_v1.md`

The **Shadow Collective** is the era's canonical example of an underworld-syndicate coalition operating outside both Republic and CIS structures. Useful for player content that engages the criminal underworld during the war:

- Pyke Syndicate (spice trade, Oba Diah HQ).
- Black Sun (Coruscant underworld, syndicate operations).
- Hutt Cartel (Outer Rim criminal authority).
- Death Watch (Mandalorian splinter, anti-Kryze).

Late-CW underworld content can use Shadow Collective allegiance as a faction-state for these syndicates.
