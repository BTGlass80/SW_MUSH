## At a glance

**Polis Massa** is a **planetoid in a dense asteroid field** in the Subterrel sector of the Outer Rim, far from major hyperspace lanes. Once a full planet that was destroyed (mechanism canonically unspecified — likely an ancient catastrophe), Polis Massa is now a debris-field with a planetoid base built into one of the larger asteroid fragments. Used as a **medical research outpost** during the late Republic; canonically the location where **Padmé Amidala gives birth to Luke and Leia** at the end of the Clone Wars era.

For SW_MUSH purposes, Polis Massa is a **secondary late-CW location** — a remote medical-base reference and a fixed campaign clock anchor for the Skywalker twins' birth.

## Geography and physical character

- **Location:** Outer Rim Territories, Subterrel sector, Polis Massa system, grid square K-20. Far from major hyperspace lanes; difficult to reach without specific coordinates.
- **Composition:** Dense asteroid field. The "planet" is a planetoid — large asteroid fragment that was once part of the original Polis Massa world before its destruction.
- **Atmosphere:** Type IV (non-breathable; requires environmental suits or pressurized facilities).
- **Gravity:** Low. The planetoid's mass is far below standard planetary norms.
- **Climate:** Vacuum exterior; pressurized interior facilities only. Cold, dimly-lit operational environment.
- **Lucas-designed aesthetic:** Per the canonical *Revenge of the Sith* DVD commentary, George Lucas designed Polis Massa as an homage to the Clavius moon base in *2001: A Space Odyssey* — clean, white, sterile interior architecture; subdued lighting; institutional medical aesthetic.

## Inhabitants and operations

- **The Polis Massans.** Native sentient species — small, gray-skinned humanoids who function as scientists and archeologists. The Polis Massans canonically operate the medical and research facilities here.
- **Medical research outpost.** Polis Massa hosts medical and archeological research facilities, particularly during the late Republic. The Polis Massans collaborate with off-world scientists and physicians.
- **No major civilian population.** Polis Massa is operationally a research outpost, not a civilian world. Population is limited to staff, researchers, and visitors.

## Clone Wars events

### Pre-CW and early-CW status
- Generally peripheral. Polis Massa is canonically not a major Clone Wars battlefield; the war passes it by.

### The Skywalker twins' birth (19 BBY) — canonical fixed event
- **Following the Vader-Obi-Wan duel on Mustafar.** Padmé Amidala — gravely wounded by Anakin's Force-choke — is brought by Obi-Wan Kenobi to Polis Massa for medical care.
- **Bail Organa is present.** The Alderaanian senator (already secretly building rebel networks) accompanies Obi-Wan and Padmé.
- **Padmé gives birth.** With Polis Massan medical assistance, Padmé delivers twins — **Luke** and **Leia**. Padmé dies shortly after the birth (canonically — "she has lost the will to live").
- **The twins are separated.** Luke is taken by Obi-Wan to Tatooine to be raised by Owen and Beru Lars (Anakin's stepbrother and his wife). Leia is taken by Bail Organa to Alderaan to be raised as his daughter.
- **Yoda joins them at Polis Massa.** Yoda has come from Coruscant; the four (Yoda, Obi-Wan, Bail, the deceased Padmé) plan the next moves. Yoda commits to exile on Dagobah; Obi-Wan to exile on Tatooine; Bail to building the rebellion in plain sight as a Senator.

This sequence is **the canonical end of the Clone Wars era** for Republic-aligned content. After this, the Imperial Era begins.

## Voice and culture (for Director AI rendering)

### Polis Massan register
- **Quiet, scientific, professional.** Polis Massans canonically communicate via translators (their native vocalizations are not standard speech). The Director AI should let their dialogue be terse, precise, medically-focused.
- **Discrete service.** Polis Massans canonically protect patient privacy and operational confidentiality. They will not gossip; they will not betray. Useful for player content involving sensitive medical operations.

### Atmospheric register
- **Sterile, contemplative, weighted.** Player content set on Polis Massa should canonically convey the medical-research-station atmosphere — quiet corridors, distant asteroid-field views, the sense of being far from anywhere.
