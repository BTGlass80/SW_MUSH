## At a glance

Lothal is an **Outer Rim agricultural-and-industrial world** that, during the Clone Wars era, exists as an independent member of the Galactic Republic but is unremarkable on the war's stage. Its prominence comes **immediately after the war**: in the early Imperial Era, Lothal becomes the home of the **Spectres** rebel cell, a Force-resonant Jedi Temple, and a long-form rebellion that prefigures the broader Rebel Alliance.

For SW_MUSH purposes, Lothal is **not a major Clone Wars-era player content location** in the way Coruscant, Mandalore, or Geonosis are. However, it has two CW-era features worth preserving:

1. **A Force-resonant ancient Jedi Temple.** Predates the war; available as Force-resonant landmark content for any Force-investigation arc.
2. **Pre-war frontier character.** Lothal during the Clone Wars is canonically a mostly-peaceful frontier agricultural world that gets dragged into Imperial industrialization only after the war. Useful as a "what the galaxy looks like before total militarization" reference.

The Director AI should default to treating Lothal as a low-priority CW-era location unless players seek out specific Force-resonant or frontier content here.

## Geography and physical character

- **Location:** Outer Rim Territories.
- **Composition:** Temperate, with **vast grasslands**, low rolling hills, and a primarily agricultural economy. **Loth-wolves** (large Force-attuned canid creatures) are native; **loth-cats** (smaller felid creatures) are common. Both species are canonically tied to the Force resonance of the world.
- **Atmosphere and climate:** Standard breathable; mild seasonal weather.
- **Capital City:** The administrative center, set on a plateau, with the iconic **LothalNet comm towers** dotting the surrounding plains.
- **Pre-Imperial environmental status:** Pristine. Imperial industrialization (post-CW) eventually pollutes the planet to near-collapse; in the CW era, this has not yet happened.

## Inhabitants

- **Diverse population.** Humans plus Xexto, Ithorians, Rodians, Aqualish, Gotals, Ugnaughts. Multiple languages spoken alongside Galactic Basic.
- **Frontier culture.** Farming, mining, and small-scale industrial work. Independent mindset; relatively unbothered by Core-world politics during the CW.
- **Loth-wolves.** Large, intelligent, **canonically Force-attuned** canid native species. They serve as guardians of Force-resonant sites; Ezra Bridger (Imperial Era) communicates with them. The Director AI should treat loth-wolves as available CW-era content — they exist throughout the era; players in the wilderness might encounter them.

## Clone Wars-era status

- **Political alignment:** **Republic-aligned**, but unremarkable. Lothal is a member world of no special strategic importance to either side. The war passes Lothal mostly by.
- **No major battles.** No canonical Clone Wars-era engagement of note on Lothal. The world is peripheral to the conflict.
- **The Jedi Temple.** A canonically ancient **Jedi Temple** exists on Lothal, predating the war. It is a Force-resonant site of significance — though most Jedi during the CW are not present there. The Temple becomes important post-CW (Yoda's Force-vision communion through it; Ahsoka, Kanan Jarrus, and Ezra Bridger using it during the Imperial Era).
- **Pre-Empire economic state:** Functional agricultural economy. The post-war "economic disrepair" that invites Imperial takeover has not yet occurred.

## Voice and culture (for Director AI rendering)

- **Frontier register.** Lothalites speak with a slight Outer Rim accent. Pragmatic, agricultural rhythms. Not impressed by Core-world politics or Senate machinations.
- **Tolerant of outsiders.** Diverse population means casual tolerance of multi-species community life.
- **Wary of distant authority.** Even before the Empire, Lothalites are skeptical of Coruscant-based government decisions. Useful for Director AI content where players represent Republic or CIS authority.

## Use in `force_resonant_landmarks_design_v1.md`

The **Lothal Jedi Temple** is the primary CW-era content here. Useful properties:

- **Ancient pre-war Force-resonant site.** The Temple existed long before the Clone Wars; predates much of the Order's recent history.
- **Force-vision connectivity.** Yoda canonically uses this Temple to commune with Qui-Gon Jinn's spirit late in the war. Players in late-CW Force-vision arcs might experience similar connections.
- **Loth-wolf guardianship.** Force-attuned native creatures protect access to deeper Force-resonant sites. Players seeking the Temple may need to earn loth-wolf trust (or be hunted by them if approaching with hostile intent).
- **Subterranean architecture.** The Temple has buried sections; canon notes that key knowledge is "buried underground" requiring Force-attunement to access. Useful for layered exploration content.

## Use in `from_dust_to_stars_design_v2_clone_wars.md`

Lothal as a **CW-era Outer Rim Republic frontier world** is useful as:

- A "low-conflict" location for content where players want a break from major-battle environments.
- A pastoral baseline against which the war's later effects (Imperial industrialization, environmental devastation) can be appreciated by players who experience both eras.
- An agricultural / mining economy reference for player content involving Outer Rim trade or supply.

## Use in `clone_wars_director_lore_pivot_design_v1.md`

The Director AI should:

- **Treat Lothal as low-priority CW-era content.** It's not an active battlefront. Players who arrive here during the war should encounter ordinary frontier life.
- **Allow the Jedi Temple as a Force-resonant landmark accessible during the war.** Most Jedi don't visit; Yoda's late-war Qui-Gon-spirit communion happens here.
- **Allow loth-wolves as natural CW-era creatures.** Players in Lothal's wilderness might encounter them. Force-sensitive players may sense their attunement.
- **Treat post-CW Imperial Era as out of scope.** The Spectres, Ezra Bridger, the Liberation of Lothal, etc., are all Rebels-era content beyond the project's CW pivot.
