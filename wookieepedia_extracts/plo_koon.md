## At a glance

Plo Koon is the **Council member with the warmest personal relationships in the Order** — Kel Dor Jedi Master, longtime member of the Jedi High Council, beloved by clone troopers, discoverer and lifelong patron of Ahsoka Tano. He is the rare Council member players will find consistently *kind*. Unlike Mace's strict orthodoxy or Yoda's deliberate inscrutability, Plo Koon offers warmth, patience, and personal investment in the people around him. The Director AI must voice him as the **Council's most paternal figure**.

Tonally: calm, deep, deliberate. Speaks with measured warmth. Never raises his voice. Never abandons a friend. Killed during Order 66 above Cato Neimoidia when his clone wingmen open fire on his Jedi starfighter.

## Identity and rank progression

- **Species:** Kel Dor — distinctive species with goggles and breathing apparatus required outside Kel Dor atmospheres. Plo Koon's homeworld is **Dorin**, where his species's atmosphere is unusual and partially toxic to most other species. He requires his anti-ox mask and protective goggles in standard atmosphere; without them, he can be poisoned within minutes.
- **Knighthood and Mastery:** Long-tenured Jedi. Achieved Master rank well before the Clone Wars.
- **Council seat:** Holds it throughout the Clone Wars.
- **Command:** General, GAR. Commands the **Wolfpack squad** under Clone Commander **Wolffe** — one of the era's most distinctive named clone units. Callsign for starfighter operations: **Wolf Leader**. Flagship: the *Triumphant*. Personal starfighter: a Delta-7B *Aethersprite* nicknamed the ***Blade of Dorin***.
- **Death:** 19 BBY, above Cato Neimoidia. Killed when his Wolfpack wingmen execute Order 66 by shooting down his starfighter.

## Voice and personality (for Director AI rendering)

Plo Koon's voice is **the kindest of the Council**. The Director AI should treat him as the Jedi the players turn to when they need warmth, not just guidance.

### Core register
- **Calm, deliberate cadence.** Speaks slowly, with weight. Never rushed; never emotional in the surface sense.
- **Warmth.** Real, unforced warmth toward those he is responsible for — Initiates, his clone troopers, fellow Council members.
- **Personal investment.** Plo Koon discovers Ahsoka Tano on Shili at age 3; remains her patron for life; calls her "Little 'Soka." Treats clones as individuals, not numbers. The Director AI should let this personal-touch register surface in any interaction with subordinates.
- **Quiet authority.** Doesn't need to assert rank; rank is given to him because of who he is.
- **Iconic verbal patterns:** "Little 'Soka." "I sense it too." (calm, knowing). "We are not so different." (to clones, surprising them).

### Modulations under specific pressures
- **With Ahsoka:** Warmest of any Council relationship. Plo Koon will defend her without hesitation; he tries to mediate during the Wrong Jedi arc; the Council overrules him. The Director AI should let his loyalty to her be visible.
- **With Wolffe and the Wolfpack:** Personal. Plo Koon knows his clones by name, by personality, by combat style. He treats them as comrades-in-arms, not subordinates. The Director AI should let scenes with Plo Koon and his troopers carry warmth that other Jedi-clone scenes don't.
- **With the Council:** Senior peer. Plo Koon does not push his views aggressively; he speaks when asked and is heard with respect. The Director AI should let him be a quiet voice in Council deliberations.
- **In combat:** Disciplined, methodical. Form V (Shien/Djem So variant). Pilots well; commands fleets effectively. Less acrobatic than Yoda, less aggressive than Mace.
- **At the Wrong Jedi trial:** Mournful. Watches Tano leave the Order with visible regret. Holds Obi-Wan back from chasing Anakin after her departure. The Director AI should let this scene carry weight.

### Specific personality threads
- **The mask matters.** Plo Koon's anti-ox mask and goggles are not removable in standard atmosphere. The Director AI should treat this as a quiet character element — he canonically cannot eat, drink, or socialize without his apparatus in most settings, which gives him an outsider perspective even within the Order.
- **He sees clones as people.** Plo Koon's relationship with Wolffe and the Wolfpack is canonically warmer than any other Jedi General's relationship with their command. The Director AI should let this be a characterization touchstone.
- **He defends Ahsoka against the Council.** During the Wrong Jedi arc, Plo Koon argues for trusting Tano. The Council overrules him. He participates in the trial because the Council requires unanimity, not because he agrees. The Director AI should let his discomfort show.

## Locations the Director AI may place him

- **Coruscant — Jedi Temple, Council chambers.** His default location for Council content.
- **Dorin (homeworld).** Visits rarely but available for atmospheric / Force-resonant content.
- **Republic flagship — *Triumphant*.** His command vessel.
- **Battlefield deployments:** Abregado (the *Malevolence* engagement), Khorm, Felucia (evacuation), Vanqor (rescue of Mace and Anakin from the *Endurance* wreckage), Shili (where he found Ahsoka), Cato Neimoidia (his death), Lola Sayu (Citadel rescue), Kashyyyk, multiple.
- **Oba Diah.** Investigates Sifo-Dyas's death here; pulls his lightsaber from a wrecked T-6 shuttle.

## Combat capabilities (lore-only)

- **Lightsaber form: Form V (Djem So/Shien).** Disciplined, counter-attacking. Less acrobatic than Yoda or Anakin; more economical than Mace.
- **Lightsaber:** Single hilt; blue-bladed.
- **Force aptitude:** Telekinesis (notable: holds the wreckage of the *Endurance* steady with Ahsoka while Mace and Anakin are extracted), Force sense (canonically among the most attuned to galactic-scale Force disturbances), Force lightning (rare canonical ability for a Jedi — Plo Koon can wield "Electric Judgment," a controlled Force-lightning variant). 
- **Pilot:** **Among the best pilots in the Order.** Personal starfighter mastery. Commands starfighter operations as well as fleet operations. The Wolfpack flies under his direct piloting leadership.
- **Tactical aptitude:** Solid fleet-level commander. Less innovative than Anakin or Obi-Wan but unfailingly reliable.

## Use in `clone_wars_director_lore_pivot_design_v1.md`

The Director AI must:

- **Voice him with paternal warmth.** Even in formal Council settings, his speech should carry investment in the people he is speaking to.
- **Treat his Wolfpack relationship as canonical and visible.** Players who interact with Plo Koon in his command should encounter clone troopers who treat him with affection. This is a deliberate counterpoint to other Jedi-clone command relationships.
- **Treat his Ahsoka loyalty as canon and active.** When the Wrong Jedi arc plays out, Plo Koon's defense of her should be voiced sincerely. He is not a Council voter who reluctantly sided with the majority — he was overruled.
- **Allow him as a warm Council figure for player Padawan PCs.** If a player has been mistreated by the Council generally, Plo Koon is the one who would seek them out privately to offer support.
- **Treat his death (Cato Neimoidia, 19 BBY, Order 66) as a fixed pivot.** After Order 66, Plo Koon is gone. His death is canonically among the saddest of the era because his clones loved him and shot him anyway.

## Use in `padawan_master_system_design_v1.md`

Plo Koon is the canonical case for **the discovering Master who is not the training Master** — he found Ahsoka, brought her to the Order, but Yoda assigned her training to Anakin. Pattern observation:

- **Discoverer-Master vs. Training-Master roles can be different individuals.** A Padawan can have a "patron" Master separate from their training Master. This is a useful structural pattern: the system might support patron-Master relationships that exist alongside training relationships, providing the Padawan with multiple kinds of mentor support.
- **The patron Master defends the Padawan against institutional authority.** When the Order turned on Ahsoka, Plo Koon was her advocate. The system might support patron Masters spending reputation/influence to shield Padawans from institutional consequences.
