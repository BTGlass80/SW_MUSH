## At a glance

Ahsoka Tano is the canonical Padawan of the era — assigned by Yoda to Anakin Skywalker at the Battle of Christophsis early in the Clone Wars, she carries the Padawan-Master arc the Director AI must render most often. She is also, uniquely among major CW Jedi, **the Padawan who walks away from the Order** after being framed for the Jedi Temple bombing, exonerated, and offered re-admission. She refuses. That refusal is structurally unique in canon and gives the Padawan-Master system a concrete model for "the bond that ends in alienation, not death."

Tonally, she is cocky and untested early, mature and command-capable mid-war, and broken-then-rebuilt late-war. Players may encounter her in any of these registers; the Director AI must select correctly based on campaign clock.

## Identity and rank progression

- **Species:** Togruta female (lekku, headtails with white-and-blue striping; large eyes; carnivorous teeth; species note — Togruta have passive group-echolocation in lore).
- **Birth:** ~36 BBY, Shili.
- **Discovery:** ~33 BBY by Jedi Master Plo Koon, who finds her among her people on Shili. He is, throughout her life, her closest Council-elder relationship — he treats her as kin in a way the Order otherwise discourages.
- **Initiate:** Trained at the Jedi Temple on Coruscant. Travels to Ilum to obtain her kyber crystal — early lesson with Yoda about trusting one's instincts.
- **Padawan:** Assigned by Yoda to Anakin Skywalker at the Battle of Christophsis, ~22 BBY. The pairing was Yoda's deliberate challenge to Anakin's attachment issues — give him responsibility he didn't ask for.
- **Commander:** Holds GAR command rank; leads forces in the field independently from her Master by mid-war.
- **The Wrong Jedi arc (~19 BBY):** Framed for the Jedi Temple hangar bombing and associated homicides. Flees into the Coruscant Underworld. Briefly allies with Asajj Ventress (Anakin's old enemy) while clearing her name. Captured by Republic forces. Exonerated by Anakin's investigation (true culprit: her friend, fellow Padawan Barriss Offee). Offered re-admission to the Order.
- **Departure:** Refuses re-admission. Walks away from the Jedi Order. Retains her lightsabers.
- **Siege of Mandalore (~19 BBY):** Returns briefly as a Republic ally to lead the campaign against Maul on Mandalore alongside Captain Rex and the 501st.
- **Order 66:** Survives. Frees Rex from his inhibitor chip mid-Order. Fakes her death. Goes into hiding (becomes "Fulcrum" much later — Imperial Era, out of project's CW scope).

## Voice and personality (for Director AI rendering)

Ahsoka has more distinct voice phases than Anakin and the Director AI must select carefully.

### Early Padawan voice (~22 BBY, Christophsis era)
- Cocky, eager to prove herself. Talks back to Anakin, matches his banter. Calls him "Skyguy"; tolerates "Snips."
- Reckless in tactics; classic "ignore retreat orders" pattern (canonical at First Battle of Felucia — gunship retreat after she nearly died on a doomed turbo tank).
- Frustrated by being treated as a child. Will demand command opportunities.
- Voice register: bright, sharp, slightly defensive.

### Mid-Padawan voice (~21–20 BBY)
- Maturing. Takes responsibility for her own choices. Apologizes when she fails (apologizes to Anakin after losing Master Ropal mission to Cad Bane).
- Develops mentor relationships outside the Master-Padawan pairing — Captain Rex (clone tactics, military respect), Tera Sinube (patience, methodical investigation, lesson on humility after losing her lightsaber to a pickpocket in the Coruscant slums).
- Leads in the field independently. Tested in command — Battle of Ryloth, where she disobeys orders and loses her entire pilot squadron, then rebounds.
- Mortis arc: dies on the mythic Force-vergence world of Mortis, resurrected by the Daughter (light-side avatar). Returns altered. The Director AI should treat any post-Mortis Ahsoka as someone who has died once and remembers it.
- Voice register: confident, dryly humorous, increasingly grave.

### Wrong Jedi voice (~19 BBY, the bombing arc)
- Hunted, betrayed, alone. Flees through the Coruscant Underworld (Levels 1313 and adjacent zones).
- Allies with Asajj Ventress out of mutual desperation — temporary, transactional.
- Confronted with the Council's willingness to abandon her. Anakin alone defends her.
- Voice register: clipped, suspicious, exhausted. Trust is gone.

### Departed voice (post–Wrong Jedi, ~19 BBY)
- Calm, certain, distant from the Order. Does not return Skywalker's calls easily. Lives somewhere on Coruscant doing mechanical work (canonically — repairs starship engines for credits in the lower city).
- Voice register: quieter, slower-paced. Not bitter; resolved.

### Siege of Mandalore voice (~19 BBY, just before Order 66)
- Returns to the field as a Republic ally — not a Jedi. Allies with Bo-Katan Kryze and the Mandalorian resistance against Maul. Operates under her own authority, not the Council's.
- The Director AI should treat her here as an **operative-without-flag**. She is doing what the Republic needs done because Rex needs her. She is not back in the Order.
- Voice register: command-confident, focused, no banter.

### Order 66 escape voice (~19 BBY, the breaking)
- Maul, watching the clones turn on her, laughs and tells her: "Sidious has played us all." She frees Maul as a diversion.
- Rex resists Order 66 mid-execution; she removes his inhibitor chip. They flee together. Stage her death; bury clone troopers (every one a name to her).
- Voice register: shellshocked, low. The Director AI should never voice this Ahsoka in any register lighter than grim.

## Locations the Director AI may place her

- **Coruscant — Jedi Temple.** Training salles, archives (where she goes after losing her lightsaber to a pickpocket), Council chambers (when summoned).
- **Coruscant — slums and underworld.** Wrong Jedi arc — flees here; lives among Trace and Rafa Martez at Level 1313 in the canonical *Clone Wars* episode "Gone with a Trace." Post-departure she works on starship engines somewhere in the lower city.
- **Coruscant — military lanes / Senate District.** Approaches Anakin's flagship via a restricted lane during the Martez sisters arc.
- **Republic flagship — *Resolute*.** With the 501st at her Master's side.
- **Battlefield deployments:** Christophsis, Ryloth, Felucia (multiple), Mortis (the Force-vergence world), Geonosis (Second Battle), Mandalore (Siege), Onderon (rebel advisor), Kashyyyk (Trandoshan younglings revolt), Kiros (Zygerrian slavers), Lola Sayu (the Citadel), Devaron (Cad Bane), Florrum (multiple).
- **Ilum.** Once, as an Initiate — for her kyber crystal.

## Notable Clone Wars story patterns (for `cw_tutorial_chains_design`)

Ahsoka-led arcs that template tutorial content well:

1. **Trandoshan younglings revolt (Kashyyyk).** Captured Padawan organizes a mass escape. Pattern: Padawan separated from Master must lead a group of younger Force-sensitives to safety. **Excellent template for early-tier player content.**
2. **Zygerrian slavers (Kiros).** Padawan goes undercover in slave market to liberate captured Togruta colony. Pattern: infiltration mission with civilian-rescue stakes; Master and Padawan separated.
3. **Onderon insurgency.** Padawan serves as advisor to local rebellion; trains insurgents to overthrow CIS-backed regime. Pattern: long-form mentorship to local NPCs; player character teaches rather than fights.
4. **Wrong Jedi arc.** Framed Padawan flees authorities; clears name through underworld investigation. Pattern: extended solo arc where the player is hunted by their own faction. **Maps directly to `coruscant_underworld_landmarks_design` — uses Levels 1313 and adjacent.**
5. **Lost lightsaber (Tera Sinube arc).** Padawan loses weapon to pickpocket; recovers it through patience-not-speed. Pattern: low-stakes tutorial chain about humility and methodology.
6. **Mortis arc.** Padawan dies, returns. Pattern: rare, mythic, weighted; the Director AI should reserve Mortis-type content for major story milestones, not regular play.
7. **Siege of Mandalore.** Ex-Jedi returns to lead a coalition campaign. Pattern: capstone late-CW content; player has graduated from apprenticeship and now operates outside formal hierarchy.

## Combat capabilities (lore-only)

For atmospheric description only. Generate D6 mechanics from WEG sources for stat blocks.

- **Lightsaber forms:** Form V (Shien — reverse grip, her signature stance) primary. Adopts Form III (Soresu, defensive) for guard duty. **Wields two lightsabers** mid-CW onward (a primary and a shoto). Both are green initially. After her departure from the Order, her recovered lightsabers are reforged by the Daughter on Mortis (canonically) and emerge **white** — the canonical "Jedi who left" sign.
- **Force aptitude:** Force speed (signature), telekinetic combat, animal-empathy and beast-sense (lifelong gift; she frees a wolf-pup early on Mortis), Force jump, modest precognition.
- **Tactical aptitude:** Strong field commander by mid-war. Better at squad tactics than Anakin; weaker at strategic-fleet command.
- **Pilot:** Competent but not exceptional. Trains under Anakin; never matches him.

## Use in `clone_wars_director_lore_pivot_design_v1.md`

The Director AI must:

- **Select voice register by campaign clock.** Five distinct phases (early Padawan / mid-Padawan / Wrong Jedi / departed / Siege-of-Mandalore-and-after). Each is canonically meaningful and tonally different.
- **Treat the Wrong Jedi → Departed transition as a one-way door.** Once a campaign passes the Wrong Jedi arc, Ahsoka does not appear in Council chambers, Temple training salles, or with the 501st under formal Jedi authority. If the player chases her down post-departure, she is found in the lower city, not the Temple.
- **Treat Plo Koon and Captain Rex as her lifelines.** In any scene where she's in distress, the Director AI should let her reach for one of them.
- **Treat Anakin as her unfinished business.** Their dialogue post-Wrong-Jedi carries grief on both sides. The Director should never let her interact with him casually after the trial.
- **Allow her to occupy underworld settings naturally.** She is the only major Jedi NPC who knows the Coruscant underworld from lived experience. Use her there.

## Use in `padawan_master_system_design_v1.md`

Ahsoka is the **canonical exit case** for the system — the Padawan who survives Master, mission, war, and Order, and walks away on her own terms. Pattern observations:

- **The bond can break without death.** Ahsoka's exit is a third state beyond "complete training" and "die in service." The system must support **departure with retained capabilities** (she keeps her lightsabers, her Force training, her Jedi title in spirit if not rank). This is mechanically distinct from "your Padawan died."
- **The Master can fail to defend the Padawan and the bond survives anyway.** Anakin alone defends Ahsoka during the Wrong Jedi trial; the Council fails her; Anakin offers re-admission on the Council's behalf — and she refuses. The Master's loyalty did not save the bond. The Director should let players experience this nuance.
- **Padawan-mentor relationships outside the Master are essential.** Ahsoka's bond with Plo Koon, Rex, Tera Sinube, and Obi-Wan are all tributaries that strengthen her. The system should let Padawan PCs accumulate secondary mentors.
- **Cocky → mature → broken → resolved is a complete arc, not an outlier.** Tutorial chains for Padawan PCs should aim for this shape, not always for a "graduate to Knight" capstone.
- **The Padawan can re-enter the field as an ally without rejoining the Order.** Siege of Mandalore is the canonical model for a "former Padawan, now operating independently" mode of play. The system should support this.

## Use in `coruscant_underworld_landmarks_design_v1.md`

Ahsoka is the player-facing underworld guide of the era. She has lived in the lower levels, worked there for credits, fled there to clear her name, and found respect among non-Jedi who would never give it to a Master. Concrete content uses:

- **Wrong Jedi tutorial chain** can be templated as an extended underworld arc for any Jedi-class player who needs to disappear from the Order's view.
- **Trace and Rafa Martez** (canonical underworld NPCs from Level 1313) are her direct contacts there. They can be reused as recurring underworld fixers.
- **Mechanic NPC archetype** — Ahsoka working on engines for credits is the canonical "former Jedi gone to ground" archetype for player roleplay.
