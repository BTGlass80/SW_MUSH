## At a glance

**Clone troopers** — also called Clone soldiers, Republic troopers/soldiers, "**Regs**" (informal), or "**Boys in White**" — are the **rank-and-file soldiers of the Grand Army of the Republic**. Bred on Kamino from the Jango Fett genetic template, accelerated-aged for combat-readiness in approximately a decade, embedded with inhibitor biochips for Order 66 compliance, deployed across the Clone Wars as the Republic's primary military force. They are not interchangeable; they develop names, nicknames, personalities, and unit cultures despite their genetic uniformity.

For SW_MUSH purposes, the clone trooper is the **default Republic-side soldier archetype** for player content. They map to standard line infantry; specialized variants (ARCs, Republic Commandos, the Bad Batch) are documented in adjacent extracts.

## Origin and biology

- **Genetic template:** **Jango Fett**, Mandalorian bounty hunter, killed by Mace Windu at the First Battle of Geonosis (22 BBY).
- **Genetic modifications from baseline Fett:** Clones were modified to be **less independent and more docile** than their progenitor — a deliberate cloning-program choice intended to ensure obedience. They were also engineered to **age at twice the natural human rate**, allowing combat-readiness in approximately a decade and presumably wearing out the army at controlled pace post-war.
- **Inhibitor biochip:** Embedded in every clone's brain. Designed (canonically) to suppress aggression in rogue clones; secretly retooled by Sidious and Tyranus to enforce **Order 66** — the directive to execute Jedi commanders on signal. Most clones do not know about the chip's existence.
- **Origin facility:** **Kamino**, Tipoca City. Kaminoan cloners under Prime Minister Lama Su and Chief Medical Scientist Nala Se conduct production.
- **Commissioning:** ~33 BBY by Jedi Master Sifo-Dyas (publicly); subverted by Sidious/Tyranus after Sifo-Dyas's murder (~32 BBY).

## Training and culture

- **Training:** Intensive, militarized education from creation through deployment age (~10 standard years biologically). Includes combat, tactics, weapons, vehicles, communications. Performed primarily on Kamino under Kaminoan oversight, with elements of training conducted by clone instructors and (rarely) external specialists.
- **Domino Squad** is the canonical example of a training cohort — five clones who initially failed pre-graduation exercises and were reassessed by Shaak Ti, El-Les, and Bric.
- **Maintenance clone 99** (named after the medical-defect designation he was assigned for being older and physically deficient) is a beloved canonical figure — knows clones by name; serves at training facilities; respected even though he never deploys.

## Identity and individuation

A canonical and unusually important point: clones develop individual identity despite genetic uniformity.

- **Names:** Most clones receive a four-digit designation (e.g., CT-7567) plus a nickname they give themselves or earn. Rex (CT-7567), Cody (CC-2224), Wolffe (CC-3636), Fox (CC-1010), Bly (CC-5052), Bacara (CC-1138), Ponds (CC-6454), Fives (CT-5555), Echo (CT-1409), Hunter, Wrecker, Tech, Crosshair, etc.
- **Armor customization:** Tolerated unevenly. Some Jedi Generals (Anakin, Plo Koon) actively encourage personalization; others (Kaminoan supervisors) discourage it.
- **Hairstyles, tattoos, piercings:** Canonical signs of individuation. Different clones look different despite shared DNA.
- **Voice and dialect:** Most clones speak with a slight Mandalorian-influenced accent (canonically, voice actor Temuera Morrison's New Zealand inflection — Jango's voice). Variations develop within unit cultures over time.

## Equipment

- **Phase I armor:** Used early war (22–~21 BBY). Less ergonomic; more uniform across clones.
- **Phase II armor:** Used mid-to-late war (~21–19 BBY). More ergonomic; allows more individual customization; better protection.
- **Imperial stormtrooper armor:** Post-Order 66 transition. Out of CW project scope.
- **Standard weapons:** DC-15A blaster rifle, DC-15S blaster carbine, DC-17 hand blaster (officers/ARCs), thermal detonators, EMP grenades, etc.
- **Standard vehicles:** AT-TE walkers, AT-RT scout walkers, BARC speeder bikes, LAAT/i gunships, Republic gunship variants, V-19 Torrent and ARC-170 starfighters (clone pilot units), various artillery and engineering platforms.

## Loyalty and service

- **Primary loyalty:** Their unit and their Jedi General. Most clones develop genuine personal attachment to their commanding Jedi over the war's duration.
- **Secondary loyalty:** The Republic and Supreme Chancellor (institutionally — what they were trained to serve).
- **Doubt and dissent:** Isolated cases of desertion and questioning service occur. **Cut Lawquane** (deserter living on Saleucami with a Twi'lek family) is canonical — Rex encounters him and lets him stay deserted. **Slick** (clone Sergeant who sold information to the CIS for credits, claiming the GAR enslaved clones) is the canonical traitor case.
- **Order 66 compliance:** Driven by the inhibitor chip, not genuine ideological betrayal. Most clones who execute Order 66 are not acting from hatred or choice — the chip overrides volition. Surviving clones who later have chips removed (Rex, Wolffe, Gregor) express grief and shame for their Order 66 actions.
- **Post-Order-66 clone fates:**
  - Most continue as Imperial stormtroopers initially, though their accelerated aging and increasing Imperial preference for human conscripts phases them out.
  - **Rex, Wolffe, Gregor** survive into the Imperial Era as deserters; Rex eventually joins Phoenix Cell and the Rebel Alliance. Cameos at Battle of Endor in canon.
  - **Hunter and the Bad Batch** survive (out of CW project scope; covered in `raw_clone_force_99.md`).
  - **Crosshair** (Bad Batch) initially serves the Empire willingly, then defects.

## Use in `weight_of_war_design_v1.md`

The clone trooper is the **canonical Republic-side soldier archetype** for the design. Concrete uses:

- **Player-character clone roles.** Players might play any of: standard line trooper (Private through Sergeant), specialist (medic, engineer, communications, pilot), officer (Lieutenant through Captain), Commander (Marshal Commander rank, like Cody or Rex), or special-forces variant (ARC, Republic Commando, scout, Bad Batch member). The system should support these as distinct character creation tracks.
- **Mass-combat unit reference.** Standard clone trooper companies, battalions, regiments scale via the CWCG §2 mass combat ruleset (additive, sample provided in `cwcg_extraction_v1.md` §2.5).
- **Inhibitor chip mechanic.** A canonical "ticking time bomb" that all clones carry. The system should support pre-Order-66 clone PCs with full agency and post-Order-66 clone PCs only if the chip is removed (canonical procedure: surgical extraction performed by Ahsoka on Rex, then Rex on Wolffe and Gregor).
- **Accelerated aging.** Clones age at 2x natural rate. Useful for character age calculations and for narrative weight on long-serving veterans.
- **Identity individuation.** Clone PCs should have names, nicknames, personalities, and customized armor — not be interchangeable templates.

## Use in `clone_wars_director_lore_pivot_design_v1.md`

The Director AI must:

- **Voice clones with unit-appropriate culture.** A 501st trooper sounds different from a 212th trooper sounds different from a Wolfpack trooper. The Mandalorian-accent baseline is shared but unit identity layers on top.
- **Treat individuality as canonical.** Don't generic-clone troopers in dialogue.
- **Treat the inhibitor chip as canon and pre-Order-66 inactive.** Most CW-era content takes place before the chips fire. Players should sense no behavioral disruption from the chips during normal play.
- **Treat the Sifo-Dyas/Tyranus subversion as deep lore.** Most clones don't know about it. Most Jedi don't know. Players who investigate may find traces.
- **Treat dissenting clones (Cut, Slick, individual deserters) as available content.** Not all clones serve happily.
