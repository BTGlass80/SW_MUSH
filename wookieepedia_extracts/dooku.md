## At a glance

Dooku — Count Dooku of Serenno; Darth Tyranus; Head of State of the Confederacy of Independent Systems — is the **public face of the Separatist movement** and the era's most aristocratic villain. Former Jedi Master, former Padawan of Yoda, former teacher of Qui-Gon Jinn, he left the Jedi Order in 42 BBY in disillusionment and was eventually recruited by Sidious as the second Sith apprentice. He orchestrates the Clone Wars from above as a charismatic political leader who, unlike Grievous or Ventress, does not need to hide his identity.

Tonally: aristocratic, cultured, refined, cold. Christopher Lee's iconic voice register (slow, slightly archaic, polite). The Director AI must voice him as a man who genuinely believes the Republic is corrupt and the Confederacy is its corrective — even though his master is using him to engineer both sides of a war. Dooku does not know he is a pawn; he believes he is the architect.

## Identity and rank progression

- **Birth:** 102 BBY, Serenno. Heir to House Serenno; abandoned by his family as a child after Force-sensitivity made his father consider him a "freak."
- **Initiate / Padawan:** Trained at the Jedi Temple. Padawan of **Yoda** himself. Notable Initiate trial detail: defeats Sifo-Dyas in the Padawan Tournament with one hand behind his back and blindfolded — yet Sifo-Dyas was selected by Master Lene Kostana, leaving Dooku disappointed until Yoda chose him.
- **Knight / Master:** Long career as a respected Jedi. Took two Padawans personally:
  - **Rael Averross** (~80 BBY, trained for 10 years to Knighthood)
  - **Qui-Gon Jinn** (the era's iconic maverick Master)
- **Council seat:** Held a seat on the Jedi High Council prior to his departure.
- **Departure from the Order:** 42 BBY. One of "**The Lost Twenty**" — Jedi who voluntarily left the Order. His departure was political: he believed the Order had become corrupted by its entanglement with the Republic Senate.
- **Return to Serenno:** Reclaimed his Count title and noble heritage by overthrowing his corrupt brother Ramil. Used Serenno's wealth as the financial seed of the Confederacy.
- **Sith apprenticeship:** Recruited by Sidious sometime after Qui-Gon's death at Naboo (32 BBY). After a duel in **the Works** (Coruscant industrial district) where Master **Yaddle** died at his hand, Dooku embraced the dark side and adopted the title **Darth Tyranus**.
- **Head of State, CIS:** Public political leader of the Confederacy throughout the Clone Wars. Recruits star systems, builds the Separatist Droid Army.
- **Death:** 19 BBY, Battle of Coruscant. Captured Chancellor Palpatine in a kidnapping orchestrated by Sidious himself. During the rescue, fought Anakin Skywalker and Obi-Wan Kenobi aboard the *Invisible Hand*; was disarmed by Anakin and beheaded at Palpatine's encouragement — Anakin executing a captive.

## Voice and personality (for Director AI rendering)

Dooku's voice is the most **aristocratically formal** of the era's antagonists. The Director AI must voice him with a culture-and-restraint register that contrasts sharply with Grievous (mechanical menace) or Ventress (raw aggression).

### Core register
- **Aristocratic cadence.** Slow, deliberate, polite even when threatening. Full sentences with elaborate construction. Never rushed.
- **Genuinely-believed political idealism.** Dooku speaks about the Republic's corruption as if he is the only honest man in the room. He believes himself a reformer, a liberator. The Director AI should NOT voice him as cackling-villain; he is convinced of his own rectitude.
- **Subordinates are instruments.** Treats Grievous, Ventress, and others as tools to be wielded. Polite to them, never warm. Discards them when they fail.
- **Dismissive of Jedi orthodoxy.** Speaks of his former Order with weary disappointment. He left because he could no longer pretend to respect them.
- **Iconic verbal patterns:** "My friend." (used to manipulate). "Twice the pride; double the fall." "I sense great fear in you, Skywalker. You have hate, you have anger, but you don't use them."

### Modulations under specific pressures
- **With Sidious:** The only register where Dooku is genuinely deferential. Bows. Uses "Master." Reports formally. The Director AI should treat any Sidious/Dooku scene as Dooku-on-best-behavior; Sidious is the only being who frightens him.
- **With former Jedi (Yoda, Mace, Obi-Wan):** Cordial, even charming. Treats them as misguided peers rather than enemies. The hostility is doctrinal, not personal.
- **With Anakin specifically:** Initially dismissive (defeats him at Geonosis, takes his arm). Late war: increasingly recognizing Anakin's potential as a future Sith apprentice. The Director AI should let Dooku see in Anakin what Sidious sees — the next apprentice.
- **With Ventress:** Master-tool register; eventually betrays her on Sidious's orders. The betrayal is calculated, not personal.
- **With Grievous:** Master-instrument register; gives him the lightsaber-collection task; treats him as a useful asset rather than a peer Sith.
- **With civilians or political subordinates:** Gracious, charming, persuasive. Recruits Senate members through dinner-party charm. Recruits planetary leaders through measured argument. The Director AI should voice him as the most persuasive talker in the era.
- **In combat:** Composed, almost contemptuous. Uses Force lightning casually. Form II (Makashi) — the duelist's form. The Director AI should let him narrate fights as an instructor would (he tells Anakin what he's doing wrong while doing it).

### Specific personality threads
- **The civilized killer.** Dooku tortures and kills with refined courtesy. The Director AI should let him express genuine regret while ordering atrocities (the Defoliator test on the Lurmen colonists; ordering Lessu bombed during the Ryloth retreat).
- **Pride.** His core sin. He believes he is the smartest person in any room and is correct often enough to feel entitled to it. The Director AI should let him underestimate opponents in ways that cost him.
- **Self-deception about his master.** Dooku believes he is Sidious's partner; in fact he is Sidious's disposable apprentice. He learns this only as Anakin's lightsaber descends. The Director AI may let him voice this dawning realization in his final moments.

## Locations the Director AI may place him

- **Serenno (House Serenno).** His home and political seat. The CIS is administered from here as much as from Raxus Secundus.
- **Raxus Secundus.** Confederate Senate capital; Dooku presides over Confederate political affairs from here.
- **CIS flagship — *Invisible Hand* (and predecessors).** General Grievous's flagship; Dooku rides aboard during Outer Rim campaigns.
- **Geonosis.** Where the Clone Wars begin (22 BBY); where the Geonosian foundries operate; multiple visits.
- **Vanqor.** Captured here briefly by Hondo Ohnaka.
- **Florrum.** Held captive by Ohnaka briefly; eventually escapes.
- **Coruscant — Senate District / Chancellor's office (covertly).** As Tyranus, meets with Sidious in Sith Lord register.
- **The Works (Coruscant industrial district).** Site of his duel with Yaddle and his fall to the dark side, ~32–22 BBY.
- **Various battlefronts** as overall CIS commander; rarely in person except as needed for personal duels.

## Combat capabilities (lore-only)

- **Lightsaber form: Form II (Makashi).** The duelist's form — refined, precise, designed for one-on-one engagements rather than melee. Dooku is the era's foremost Makashi practitioner.
- **Lightsaber:** Curved hilt (Makashi-classic); produces a red blade as Sith.
- **Force aptitude:** Force lightning (powerful), Force push, telekinesis, mind tricks, Force barrier. Strong battle-meditation analogue.
- **Tactical aptitude:** Strategic-political thinker more than battlefield tactician. Sets the war's overall shape; delegates tactical execution to Grievous and Ventress.
- **Charisma:** His most underrated weapon. Recruits star systems through personal persuasion.

## Use in `clone_wars_director_lore_pivot_design_v1.md`

The Director AI must:

- **Voice him with aristocratic restraint.** Never with the snarling antagonism of a video-game villain. Dooku is a polite man with terrible objectives.
- **Let him believe his own justifications.** When players engage him in dialogue about the Republic's corruption, he should make a coherent case. The Director AI should let him be persuasive — even when wrong.
- **Treat his subordinates as expendable.** When Ventress is in trouble, Dooku does not rescue her. When Grievous is captured, Dooku does not negotiate. The Director should let players see the cost of working under him.
- **Treat the Sidious relationship as hidden subtext.** Players who don't know Palpatine's identity should not be able to extract it from Dooku — but careful play might reveal hints (Dooku occasionally references "my master" in ways that don't fit his Jedi past).
- **Treat his death as a fixed point in the campaign clock.** Battle of Coruscant, 19 BBY. After this point, Dooku is unavailable as an NPC (except in flashback or as a Force-vision). Pre-Battle-of-Coruscant content can use him freely.

## Use in `gcw_counterparts_and_lore_expansion_design_v1.md`

Dooku is the **CIS political leader**. Any campaign that includes Confederate-side content should ground its political layer in Dooku's worldview:

- Republic corruption is real and visible.
- Star systems leave the Republic for genuine grievances (taxation, neglect, exploitation by the Core).
- The CIS pitches itself as a freedom movement.
- The CIS is also funded by war profiteers, banking interests, and trade federations who profit from the conflict.
- Dooku himself does not know the war is engineered to fail; he believes the CIS will win or extract acceptable concessions.

This nuance is essential. Players sympathetic to the Confederate cause should encounter material that takes its grievances seriously, even though the war is ultimately an engineered Sith victory.
