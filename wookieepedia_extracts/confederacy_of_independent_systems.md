## At a glance

The **Confederacy of Independent Systems (CIS)** — colloquially "Separatists" — is the **Republic's antagonist faction in the Clone Wars**, a coalition of star systems, mega-corporations, and trade interests led publicly by **Count Dooku** and secretly by **Darth Sidious**. Founded ~24 BBY in response to genuine Republic grievances (corruption, taxation, neglect of outlying worlds); militarized via massive battle-droid armies; declared full war at the First Battle of Geonosis (22 BBY); collapsed at the Battle of Coruscant (19 BBY) when Dooku is killed and Grievous is targeted; final disposition: the Separatist Council is killed by Vader on Mustafar at war's end; surviving Separatist worlds reintegrated into the Galactic Empire.

For SW_MUSH purposes, the CIS is the **canonical antagonist faction structure** — a real political movement with legitimate grievances, used as a Sith trojan horse by an architect neither side knows. Players engaging Confederate-side content should encounter this dual nature.

## Core institutional structure

### Political leadership
- **Head of State:** **Count Dooku** of Serenno (publicly); **Darth Sidious** (secretly, via Dooku).
- **Senate:** The **Separatist Senate**, modeled on the Galactic Senate. Located on **Raxus Secundus**. Includes legitimate political representatives like **Mina Bonteri** (Onderon, killed by Dooku for proposing peace), **Voe Atell**, etc. The Senate genuinely debates policy; it is not pure window-dressing, but Sidious manipulates outcomes through Dooku.
- **Executive Separatist Council:** The mega-corporate alliance that funds and supplies the Confederacy. The "true power" of the Confederacy at the corporate level. Members include:
  - **Trade Federation** — Viceroy **Nute Gunray** (Neimoidian; cowardly, pretentious, foolish; battle droids).
  - **Techno Union** — Foreman **Wat Tambor** (Skakoan; mining, manufacturing, droid factories; Mustafar operations).
  - **InterGalactic Banking Clan (IGBC)** — Chairman **San Hill** (Muun); Chairman **Rush Clovis** (Cato Neimoidia, briefly).
  - **Commerce Guild** — Presidente **Shu Mai** (Gossam).
  - **Corporate Alliance** — Magistrate **Passel Argente** (Koorivar).
  - **Hyper-Communications Cartel** (peripheral member).
  - **Geonosian Industries** — Archduke **Poggle the Lesser** (Geonosian; primary droid factories on Geonosis).
- **Military leadership:** **General Grievous** (Supreme Martial Commander); **Asajj Ventress** (Sith acolyte, field operative); various **Tactical Droids** (TX-series, T-series).

### Military
- **Separatist Droid Army:** Mass droid army (B1, B2, BX commando, droideka, etc.). Built by Trade Federation, Techno Union, Geonosian Industries.
- **Separatist Navy:** *Munificent*-class star frigates, *Providence*-class dreadnoughts (the *Invisible Hand*, *Malevolence*), *Recusant*-class destroyers, vulture droid starfighters, hyena bombers.
- **Allied non-droid forces:** **Umbaran Militia** (advanced technology), **Quarren Isolation League** (Mon Cala), various planetary forces, **Death Watch** (Mandalorian splinter, briefly), **Zygerrian Slave Empire** (Kiros operations), **Pyke Syndicate** (criminal alliance).

## Founding causes (genuine grievances — important nuance)

Per architecture v35 §30, the CIS's grievances should be taken seriously in player content. Canonical reasons star systems joined the Confederacy:

- **Republic corruption and ineffectiveness.** Decades of Senate paralysis under weak Chancellors (Finis Valorum); legislation favoring established Core Worlds at the expense of outlying systems.
- **Taxation of trade routes** that disproportionately burdened poorer outlying systems.
- **Refusal of Republic military protection** to peripheral worlds while honoring centuries-old agreements with Core Worlds.
- **The Invasion of Naboo (32 BBY)** as a galvanizing event. The Senate's failure to respond to the Trade Federation's invasion exposed institutional rot.
- **Bureaucratic capture by trade interests.** The mega-corporations had grown into political powers in their own right; outlying worlds saw the Republic as a tool of Core-world commerce.

## Founding chronology

- **~24 BBY:** Mass exodus of star systems from the Republic begins. Dooku, having left the Jedi Order in 42 BBY and reclaimed his Count title on Serenno, begins covert recruitment.
- **22 BBY:** The Confederacy formally exists. **First Battle of Geonosis** — Republic forces, including Jedi, attempt to free Obi-Wan, Anakin, Padmé from Petranaki Arena execution; the newly-revealed clone army arrives; the Clone Wars begin in earnest.
- **22–19 BBY:** Three years of galaxy-spanning warfare across hundreds of fronts.

## Major Clone Wars campaigns

The Director AI may use any of the following as canonical CIS-fronted content:

- **Geonosis** — primary droid foundries; opening battle and Second Battle.
- **Kamino** — CIS attack on the clone homeworld; defended by Shaak Ti and Republic garrison.
- **Ryloth** — Wat Tambor occupies; Republic liberates (Mace Windu, Anakin, Aayla Secura, Cham Syndulla).
- **Christophsis** — early CW Republic victory; site of Anakin and Ahsoka's first deployment together.
- **Felucia** — multiple campaigns; Republic forced to retreat; Sifo-Dyas died here on a peace mission.
- **Cato Neimoidia** — Trade Federation HQ region; multiple battles; the Cadesura disaster.
- **Mon Cala** — Quarren Isolation League allied with CIS; Republic + Gungan Grand Army intervene.
- **Naboo** — multiple invasion attempts (Rish Loo plot; Festival of Light kidnapping).
- **Onderon** — CIS puppet King Sanjay Rash overthrown by Republic-backed insurgency (Saw Gerrera, Steela, Lux Bonteri).
- **Mandalore** — neutral world becomes contested as Death Watch allies with CIS; eventually Maul takes control via Shadow Collective.
- **Umbara** — secedes to CIS; Republic invades; Pong Krell's treason; reincorporated.
- **Kiros / Kadavo** — Zygerrian Slave Empire alliance; Republic intervention.
- **Mygeeto** — Outer Rim Sieges; Ki-Adi-Mundi's death there during Order 66.
- **Utapau** — Grievous's last stand; Obi-Wan kills him there.
- **Coruscant (Battle of Coruscant)** — Grievous's surprise assault; kidnapping of Palpatine; death of Dooku.

## Collapse

- **Battle of Coruscant (19 BBY):** Dooku killed by Anakin Skywalker. Grievous flees.
- **Utapau (19 BBY):** Sidious betrays Grievous's location; Obi-Wan kills him.
- **Order 66 / Galactic Empire formation (19 BBY):** Sidious reveals himself; clones execute Order 66; the Republic transforms into the Empire within days.
- **Mustafar (19 BBY):** The Separatist Council retreats here, expecting a peace deal with the new Empire. Vader arrives instead; kills them all; deactivates the droid army.
- **Post-war:** Surviving Confederate worlds reintegrated into the Empire. Some Separatist holdouts persist (CIS droid armies still operating on isolated worlds, like General Kalani's command on Agamar — out of CW project scope but canonical).

## Voice and culture (for Director AI rendering)

The CIS is **not monolithic**. The Director AI must select voice register based on which CIS faction the player encounters.

### Political/Senatorial register (Raxus Secundus)
- Genuine political debate, Senate-floor cadence. Sympathetic representatives like Mina Bonteri sound like sincere reformers. Some are; others are mouthpieces for corporate interests.
- The Director AI should let Confederate political content carry **moral seriousness** — the grievances are real even though the war is engineered.

### Trade Federation / Neimoidian register
- **Cowardly, pretentious, weasel-voiced.** Nute Gunray and his Neimoidian colleagues are canonically the least sympathetic CIS faction. The Director AI should voice them with whining, scheming, self-preservation tones.

### Techno Union / Skakoan register
- **Cold, scientific, contemptuous of organic life.** Wat Tambor's Skakoan accent is canonically distinct (high-pressure suit, breathing apparatus). The Director AI should let his voice be measured, technical, cruelly clinical.

### Banking Clan / Muun register
- **Calculating, transactional, polite.** San Hill speaks like a banker because he is one. The Director AI should let banking-clan dialogue read as financial-meeting normal even when discussing war.

### Geonosian register
- **Insectoid, hierarchical, biologically alien.** Poggle the Lesser communicates through a translator droid. The Director AI should let Geonosian voice carry hive-society subtext.

### Confederate civilian register
- Many CIS civilians believe in the cause. The Director AI should let ordinary Confederate citizens express genuine political conviction, not stock villainy. Refugees, factory workers, Confederate parents — these voices should sound like ordinary people who chose secession for understandable reasons.

## Use in `gcw_counterparts_and_lore_expansion_design_v1.md`

The CIS is the **central reference for Confederate-side player content** in the design. Concrete uses:

- **Player-character CIS roles.** Players might play:
  - A Confederate citizen on a Separatist world.
  - A Separatist Senator's aide.
  - A trade corporation employee (Federation, Banking Clan, Techno Union).
  - A non-Federation military officer (Umbaran, Quarren, etc.).
  - A reformist who joined the CIS for the cause and is troubled by Dooku's authoritarianism.
  - A Confederate Intelligence operative.
- **Confederate-side moral seriousness.** Per architecture v35 §30, CIS players should encounter grievances treated with weight.
- **The hidden truth.** Players who deeply investigate the CIS's origins may discover the Sidious/Tyranus subversion. This is high-stakes content.

## Use in `weight_of_war_design_v1.md`

- **Mass-combat CIS unit reference.** B1 battle droid squads (CWCG §2.6 sample) scale to CIS battle-droid units; B2 super battle droid units; droideka units; tactical droid command units.
- **CIS naval engagements** for fleet-scale content.
- **The droid army's tactical advantage:** numbers; expendability. The droids are not afraid; they don't tire; they don't need rations. Republic forces win mostly through Jedi leadership and clone individual quality. The mass-combat ruleset should reflect this.

## Use in `clone_wars_director_lore_pivot_design_v1.md`

The Director AI must:

- **Voice CIS factions distinctly.** Trade Federation Neimoidians, Techno Union Skakoans, Banking Clan Muuns, Confederate Senators, civilian Confederates, droid commanders — all distinct.
- **Treat CIS grievances as real.** Don't reduce Confederate citizens to villainous stock. Many believe in the cause.
- **Treat Sidious's hidden hand as canon.** Most Confederates do not know they serve the Sith. Players who uncover this are uncovering deep lore.
- **Treat the Mustafar Council massacre (19 BBY) as a fixed pivot.** After this, the CIS political leadership is dead.
