## At a glance

**Order 66** is the **execution directive that ends the Clone Wars era and the Jedi Order**. Issued by Supreme Chancellor Palpatine (revealing himself as Darth Sidious) on the final day of the war (19 BBY), it commands every clone trooper across the galaxy to kill their Jedi commanders simultaneously. The order is enforced by the **inhibitor biochip** embedded in every clone's brain — the chip overrides volition and compels compliance. Most Jedi die within hours. The Galactic Republic dies within days.

For SW_MUSH purposes, Order 66 is **the era's defining campaign-clock terminus**. It is the canonical end of the Clone Wars period and the canonical beginning of the Imperial Era. Players whose campaigns span the war will encounter it as a fixed event.

## The Sith long game

Order 66 is the culmination of a **thousand-year-old Sith plan** initiated by **Darth Bane** after the Sith's near-extinction at Jedi hands (Hundred-Year Darkness, ancient era). Bane established the **Rule of Two** — only ever one Sith Master and one apprentice at a time, the apprentice required to eventually kill the master — to prevent the infighting that had destroyed previous Sith orders. For nearly a thousand years, the Sith hid and plotted.

Sidious is the culmination of this lineage:
- **Darth Plagueis** (Muun Sith Lord, obsessed with cheating death) trained Sidious.
- Sidious killed Plagueis in his sleep, becoming Dark Lord of the Sith.
- Through three apprentices (Maul → Tyranus/Dooku → Vader/Anakin), Sidious executed the final phase of the Grand Plan.

## Origin of the order itself

Order 66 was inserted into the Republic's clone-army contingency-order system through the **inhibitor biochip subversion**:

1. **~33 BBY:** Jedi Master **Sifo-Dyas** independently commissions a clone army from Kamino, foreseeing a great war. The Jedi Council rejected his proposal; he proceeds in secret.
2. **~32 BBY:** Sifo-Dyas is murdered by Dooku (acting as Tyranus) via the Pyke Syndicate. Tyranus assumes Sifo-Dyas's identity in dealings with the Kaminoans.
3. **~32 BBY onward:** Tyranus and Sidious **retool the inhibitor chip** the Kaminoans were already producing. Originally designed (per Sifo-Dyas's spec) to suppress aggression and prevent rogue clones, the chip is secretly modified to execute Order 66 on signal.
4. **22 BBY:** The clone army is "discovered" by the Republic at Kamino; the Jedi accept it; the Clone Wars begin at Geonosis.
5. **~21 BBY:** Clone Trooper **Tup** (501st) experiences premature chip activation on Ringo Vinda, killing Jedi Master Tiplar. Tup is sent to Kamino for examination. ARC Trooper **Fives** discovers the chip's true nature. Fives attempts to warn the Order; Nala Se and the Sith conspirators ensure his elimination before he can. Shaak Ti's investigation is undermined and the conspiracy buried.
6. **19 BBY:** Mace Windu and three other Council members (Kit Fisto, Saesee Tiin, Agen Kolar) confront Palpatine in his office to arrest him after Anakin reveals Palpatine is Sidious. Three die immediately; Mace defeats Sidious in the duel; Anakin betrays Mace; Sidious kills Mace. Sidious initiates Order 66 immediately after.

## Execution and immediate consequences

Within hours of the order:

- **Jedi Temple, Coruscant:** Anakin (now Vader) leads the 501st Legion in an assault. Most Temple Jedi killed. Younglings massacred. Shaak Ti killed during this. **Sevander** (a youngling who survived) later becomes the Inquisitor known as the Third Sister (Reva), out of CW project scope.
- **Cato Neimoidia:** **Plo Koon** killed when his Wolfpack wingmen shoot down his Jedi starfighter.
- **Kashyyyk:** **Yoda** survives; senses the disturbance; escapes with Wookiee assistance and Bail Organa's pickup. Goes into Dagobah exile.
- **Mygeeto:** **Ki-Adi-Mundi** killed by his own clone troopers (Bacara and the 21st Nova Corps).
- **Felucia:** **Aayla Secura** killed by Commander Bly and the 327th Star Corps.
- **Saleucami:** **Stass Allie** killed.
- **Utapau:** **Obi-Wan Kenobi** survives; Cody and the 212th open fire on him; Obi-Wan falls into a sinkhole and escapes via varactyl mount.
- **Mandalore (en route to Coruscant):** **Ahsoka Tano** survives; removes Captain **Rex's** inhibitor chip mid-Order; both fake their deaths and go into hiding.
- **Kaller:** Padawan **Caleb Dume** survives because Hunter (Bad Batch) chose not to fire on him. Dume becomes Kanan Jarrus.
- **Various other engagements:** Most Jedi commanders killed. **Quinlan Vos** survives (was already in disgrace from Dark Disciple arc). A handful of others survive in scattered hiding.

Within days:
- **Mustafar:** Vader kills the Separatist Council.
- **Coruscant Senate:** Sidious announces the formation of the **Galactic Empire**. Senate applauds.
- **The Republic ends.**

## The chip as both weapon and excuse

A canonically important nuance: **the inhibitor chip both compels Order 66 and partially absolves the clones**. Clones who execute the order are not acting from genuine ideological choice — the chip overrides their volition. Surviving clones with chips removed (Rex, Wolffe, Gregor) express grief and shame but are not held morally responsible by their surviving Jedi friends.

This is the era's central tragedy:
- The clones loved their Jedi.
- The clones killed their Jedi anyway.
- Most clones never knew about the chip.
- A few survivors carry the trauma forward.

## Use in `clone_wars_director_lore_pivot_design_v1.md`

The Director AI must:

- **Treat Order 66 as a fixed campaign-clock event at 19 BBY.** It is the era's terminus. Players cannot prevent it.
- **Treat it as the boundary between CW-era content and post-Order-66 content.** Any Jedi-PC content fundamentally changes after this point.
- **Treat clone-PC content as fundamentally changed.** Pre-Order-66 clones serve the Republic with full agency. At the Order, all clones except chip-removed ones execute Jedi commanders. Post-Order-66 clones who survive the chip (rare) become deserters or rebels.
- **Allow inhibitor-chip-removal as a canonical procedure.** Ahsoka's removal of Rex's chip is the template. Players who run clone PCs and who learn about the chip may seek removal.
- **Treat Order 66 survivor narratives as canonically rare and difficult.** Most Jedi die. Survival requires the survivor to immediately disappear, change identity, abandon active Jedi practice, and accept that they will never publicly be Jedi again.

## Use in `weight_of_war_design_v1.md`

Order 66 is the **terminus of all Republic-side player content in the CW era**:

- **Clone-PC campaigns** must reckon with the Order. The system might support pre-Order-66 campaigns that end at the Order, or post-Order-66 campaigns that begin at the Order with the survivor's escape.
- **Jedi-PC campaigns** must reckon with the Order. Most Jedi PCs die at this point unless the player explicitly designs an Order-66 survivor narrative.
- **Mass-combat content** ends at the Order. The GAR transforms into the Imperial Military within days; the CIS droid army is deactivated within weeks.
