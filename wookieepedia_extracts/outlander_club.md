## At a glance

The **Outlander Club** (also called the **Outlander Gambling Club**) is a **gambling-and-nightclub establishment** in Coruscant's **Uscru Entertainment District**. Two-tier venue: the upper levels cater to Coruscant high society (sabacc tables, sports betting, bottle service); the lower levels host the criminal element (death-stick dealing, illicit goods exchange, fugitive crowd-shaking). Famously the location of **Obi-Wan and Anakin's pursuit of Zam Wesell** in the days before the Clone Wars — including the canonical "you don't want to sell me death sticks" mind-trick scene.

For SW_MUSH purposes, the Outlander Club is **one of the era's most useful upper-Coruscant entertainment-district landmarks** — a "respectable" location where players can encounter both legitimate high-society NPCs and underworld contacts within the same building.

## Location and physical character

- **District:** **Uscru Entertainment District** — Coruscant's primary nightlife / entertainment zone. Mid-to-upper levels of the city. (See `wookieepedia_extracts/coruscant_underworld.md` for broader district context.)
- **Tier:** Borderline-respectable. Uscru's upper venues (Galaxies Opera House) are fully respectable; Uscru's lower venues are seedier; the Outlander Club sits in the middle — popular enough that elites visit, dangerous enough that fugitives frequent it.
- **Layout:**
  - **Central bar** offering drinks and food.
  - **Sports-betting cutouts** scattered around the bar where patrons place bets on galactic sporting events — **podracing** and **grav-ball** specifically referenced in canon. The cutouts display real-time odds and game feeds.
  - **Sabacc tables** for card-gambling.
  - **Less-developed lower regions** where illicit activities take place. Death-stick dealers operate; smugglers move goods; fugitives blend with crowds to shake pursuers.

## Ownership and operations

- **Founded** as a small, stylish, high-class establishment.
- **The game "Outlander"** was developed here — a sabacc-variant or original card game that became regionally famous.
- **Baath brothers** — current management (during the era). They expanded the club to accommodate broader gaming trends and broader clientele, transforming it from an exclusive elite lounge into a multi-tier venue serving both elites and criminals.
- **Pricing:** Mid-tier Coruscant prices — affordable to working-class visitors with disposable income; standard for elite patrons.
- **Music and atmosphere:** Lively. Crowded. Multi-species clientele. Standard Coruscant club aesthetic — neon, holographic projections, ambient noise of conversation and game results.

## Notable canonical events

### The Zam Wesell pursuit (~22 BBY, days before the war)
- **Background.** Bounty hunter **Zam Wesell** (Clawdite shapeshifter) attempted to assassinate Senator Padmé Amidala on Coruscant — failed; pursued by Obi-Wan Kenobi and Anakin Skywalker.
- **The pursuit.** Anakin and Obi-Wan chase Wesell through Coruscant traffic; she retreats into the Outlander Club to lose them in the crowds.
- **The death-stick scene.** Obi-Wan, posing casually at the bar, is approached by a death-stick dealer (Elan Sleazebaggano); uses **mind-trick** to redirect him: "You don't want to sell me death sticks. You want to go home and rethink your life." This canonical scene is among the era's most famous Force-mind-trick demonstrations.
- **The capture.** Anakin spots Wesell in disguise. The two Jedi corner her; chase her into the alley behind the club; disarm her. **Jango Fett** (concealed elsewhere) shoots her with a poisoned saberdart before she can identify her co-conspirator. The dart leads Obi-Wan to Dex's Diner and ultimately to Kamino — beginning the discovery of the clone army.
- **Significance.** The Outlander Club is canonically the location where the Clone Wars's discovery cascade begins. Players investigating the war's origins may trace the chain back to this nightclub.

### Recurring underworld presence
- **Criminal networks.** Coruscant's criminal networks frequently use the Outlander Club as a meeting place — the noise, the crowds, and the mixed clientele provide cover.
- **Smuggler patrons.** Greez Dritus (a Latero smuggler from the Imperial Era *Star Wars Jedi: Fallen Order* canon) recalls the Outlander Club nostalgically as a venue where he won memorable sabacc pots — confirming the club's reputation as a serious sabacc destination.

## Voice and culture (for Director AI rendering)

### Bartender / staff register
- **Professional, multi-species, sees-everything.** The Outlander Club's staff are canonically used to celebrities, criminals, and everything in between. They serve and don't ask questions.

### Patron register
- **Variable by tier.** Upper-floor / sabacc-table patrons speak with refined cadence (financiers, mid-rank officers, journalist class). Lower-floor patrons speak more roughly (smugglers, dealers, fugitives, freelance hunters).
- **Multi-species background.** Standard Coruscant urban demographics. The Director AI should populate the club with a mix.

### Atmospheric register
- **Loud, crowded, easy to disappear in.** Player content set here should canonically feel like one is being watched but cannot easily watch back.
