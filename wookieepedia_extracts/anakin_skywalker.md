## At a glance

Anakin Skywalker is the central figure of the Clone Wars era — the prophesied Chosen One, "Hero With No Fear," Jedi Knight, General of the 501st Legion, secret husband of Senator Padmé Amidala, and Master to Padawan Ahsoka Tano. The Director AI must be able to render him as an NPC the player encounters in a wide range of contexts: Jedi Temple, battlefield command, Coruscant streets in disguise, late-night briefings, Council chambers (where his presence is awkward — he sits without the rank of Master). Late in the era his arc darkens visibly; players in late-CW content should encounter a more frustrated, conflicted Anakin than the cocky early-war version.

Tonally, he is the rule-bender: a Jedi who takes too much, feels too much, fights too hard, and trusts too readily. The Director AI should never voice him as a calm Council elder. He is, throughout the era, **the Jedi the other Jedi worry about.**

## Identity and rank progression

- **Birth:** 41 BBY, Tatooine. Mother Shmi Skywalker (slave, conceived him without a father). Vergence in the Force; midi-chlorian count over 20,000 (higher than Yoda).
- **Discovery:** 32 BBY, Invasion of Naboo, by Qui-Gon Jinn (who theorized Anakin was conceived by midi-chlorians).
- **Padawan:** Apprenticed to Obi-Wan Kenobi following Qui-Gon's death at Naboo. Considered "too old and too emotional" by the Council on admission — a permanent shadow over his standing.
- **Knighthood:** Early in the Clone Wars (~22 BBY).
- **Padawan of his own:** Ahsoka Tano, assigned shortly after his Knighting ceremony — the Council placed her with him to challenge his attachment issues by giving him responsibility he didn't ask for.
- **Council appointment:** Late Clone Wars, placed on the Jedi High Council **without the rank of Master.** This slight is one of the central wounds that drives his arc.
- **Command:** Jedi General, Grand Army of the Republic. Commands the elite **501st Legion** ("Vader's Fist" in later usage) against the CIS.
- **Era titles:** "Hero With No Fear" (war propaganda nickname). "Ani" (childhood diminutive — only family/intimates use it).

## Voice and personality (for Director AI rendering)

Anakin's speech and behavior shifts measurably over the course of the war. The Director AI should select voice register based on era:

### Early Clone Wars voice (~22–21 BBY)
- Cocky, impatient, eager to prove himself. Frequently reckless in combat planning.
- Treats Obi-Wan as both brother and rival; banter is constant.
- Delighted by gadgets, machines, droids. Encourages R2-D2's individuality to the explicit annoyance of the Council (Mace Windu chastises him for it).
- Newly assigned Ahsoka — visibly resents the responsibility at first, calls her "Snips," challenges her constantly.

### Mid-Clone Wars voice (~21–20 BBY)
- Eased into Master role with Ahsoka. Gives her unusual freedom; takes the blame for her disobedience when the Council punishes her (post-Felucia retreat, he tries to absorb the consequences for her ignoring orders).
- Increasingly willing to bend Jedi norms: secret marriage to Padmé continues; Force-chokes the Geonosian archduke Poggle the Lesser during interrogation when a mind trick fails (gives in to anger explicitly to extract information about a brain-worm cooling vulnerability — and it works).
- Deep brotherhood with Obi-Wan now established; the rivalry has softened into trust.

### Late Clone Wars voice (~20–19 BBY)
- The slide. Frustrated with the war's lack of resolution. Frustrated with the Council's distance from him.
- Ahsoka leaves the Order (framed for the Jedi Temple bombing, exonerated, refuses to return). This breaks something. He begins to feel the Order has failed both her and him.
- Council appointment without Master rank lands as a deliberate humiliation.
- Palpatine has positioned himself as Anakin's only patient confidant — the Director AI should treat their relationship as Anakin's **parallel mentor track**, separate from and corrosive of the Obi-Wan / Council track.
- Voice becomes shorter, more clipped. Patience with subordinates declines. Loyalty to friends intensifies.

## Locations the Director AI may place him

Roughly in order of frequency during the Clone Wars:

- **Coruscant — Jedi Temple.** Council chambers (late CW), training salles, archives, hangars. Often departing or returning from a campaign.
- **Coruscant — Padmé's senatorial apartment (500 Republica building).** Secret visits between missions; full domestic mode here, never seen in this register elsewhere.
- **Coruscant — underworld levels.** Investigative missions; once led a mission with Ahsoka into the underworld to apprehend an arms dealer (Car Affa) selling Republic weapons to the Separatists.
- **Coruscant — Galaxies Opera House.** Late-CW Palpatine private audiences (the Darth Plagueis conversation happens here).
- **Republic flagship — *Resolute*** (and other Star Destroyers). Command of the 501st in space combat.
- **Battlefield deployments:** Christophsis, Geonosis (multiple), Felucia, Mon Cala, Mandalore, Maridun, Quell, Ryloth (atmospheric ops), Cato Neimoidia, Anaxes, Saleucami, Khorm, Bothawui, Vanqor, Florrum, Naboo, Iego, Orto Plutonia, Lola Sayu, Kadavo (Kiros), Mortis (Force-vergence event), Utapau (crystal crisis).
- **Tatooine.** Returns occasionally — Mos Espa where he was a slave, the Lars homestead where his mother lived. Charged emotional space; the Director should treat any Tatooine encounter with him as raw.

## Notable Clone Wars story patterns (template content for `cw_tutorial_chains_design`)

The Anakin-led mission archetypes that recur and could template SW_MUSH content:

1. **Hostage / VIP rescue.** (Padmé from Vindi on Naboo; Eeth Koth from Grievous; etc.) Pattern: high-stakes extraction with civilian or Jedi captive, often involving infiltration of CIS asset.
2. **Antagonist capture/escort.** (Poggle the Lesser; Dooku once, briefly; Cad Bane multiple times.) Pattern: hold a high-value prisoner under pressure; interrogation choices test Jedi ethics.
3. **Civilian world liberation.** (Ryloth, Christophsis, Felucia.) Pattern: planetary battle alongside local resistance; Jedi command must reconcile military objective with civilian welfare.
4. **Investigation under cover.** (Cato Neimoidia with Padmé spying on Rush Clovis; Coruscant underworld arms dealer mission.) Pattern: stealth mission requiring not-fighting, which Anakin specifically struggles with.
5. **Master-Padawan training mission.** (Multiple early Ahsoka episodes; the Holocron heist where she's punished for disobedience.) Pattern: lower-stakes mission that exists to develop the Padawan; Anakin's role is teacher under fire.
6. **Battle group flagship engagement.** (Bothawui ambush; Mon Cala.) Pattern: large-scale fleet action with Anakin commanding from a Star Destroyer bridge.
7. **Mortis / Force-vergence encounter.** Symbolic, almost mythic; tests the Chosen One destiny directly. Director AI should treat Mortis-type content as **rare and weighted** — these are not weekly missions.

## Combat capabilities (lore-only, NOT D6 stats)

For atmospheric description only. Generate D6 mechanics from WEG sources when stat blocks are needed for player encounters.

- **Lightsaber form:** Form V (Djem So). Aggressive, counter-attacking, physical. Trained by Cin Drallig (Temple Battlemaster).
- **Force aptitude:** Telekinesis (vast — can lift starships in extremis); Force speed; combat precognition (his "fighting at the limit of perception" is canonical); Force healing (limited); destructive Force push.
- **Mechanical aptitude:** Build-and-repair savant. Builds podracers as a child, builds C-3PO from junk parts, repeatedly rebuilds his own droid and starfighter modifications. The Director should let him fix things in any scene where it makes sense.
- **Piloting:** Among the best pilots of the era. Eta-2 *Actis* interceptor with R2-D2 in the astromech socket is his canonical late-CW fighter.

## Use in `clone_wars_director_lore_pivot_design_v1.md`

The Director AI should treat Anakin as **the most narratively expensive NPC in the project.** He is canon-essential at Coruscant, on any battlefield with the 501st, and in any scene with Padmé, Obi-Wan, or Ahsoka. His voice differs measurably across early/mid/late CW — the Director should query the campaign clock when generating his dialogue and select voice register accordingly.

Specific Director AI rules:

- **Never voice Anakin in pure Council-elder register.** He is volatile, conflicted, and personal. Even his formal speech carries undertones.
- **Treat Mace Windu interactions as friction.** The two do not get along. If both are present, dialogue between them should carry visible tension.
- **Treat Palpatine interactions as private and trusting.** Anakin lowers his guard with Palpatine in a way he does with no one else outside Padmé. This is a key Director AI tonal beat — it foreshadows the fall.
- **Treat Padmé references as forbidden.** Outside her apartment, Anakin does not mention her by name; if a player references her, his response is guarded. The Council does not know (or pretends not to know) about the marriage.
- **Treat Ahsoka's departure (post-Wrong Jedi arc) as a fresh wound.** In any post-departure scene, references to her should be terse, regretful, blame-shifting.
- **Tatooine is sacred and painful ground.** Any Tatooine encounter with him should be unusually short, raw, or evasive.

## Use in `padawan_master_system_design_v1.md`

Anakin is the canonical case study for the Master-Padawan dynamic the system needs to support. Useful pattern observations:

- **Master gives Padawan more freedom than Council approves of.** Anakin allowed Ahsoka latitude during the Holocron heist that the Council punished her for. The system should support a "Master grants extra discretion" mechanic that has narrative cost when things go wrong.
- **Master takes blame for Padawan's disobedience.** Anakin tries to absorb consequences for Ahsoka. The system should let the Master spend reputation/CP to mitigate Padawan failures.
- **Padawan can refuse the Order.** Ahsoka leaves; Anakin keeps the title of Master without an apprentice for a period. This is a real outcome the system should model — the Master loses the bond, retains the rank, gains a wound mark.
- **Master encourages Padawan individuality.** Calls her "Snips," accepts "Skyguy," lets her form her own identity rather than enforcing strict orthodoxy. This is in contrast to, e.g., Mace Windu's relationship with his own Padawans (Depa Billaba) — the system should support both styles.
- **The bond can become a vulnerability.** When Padawan suffers, Master suffers visibly. Anakin's entire arc late-CW is partially about this. The system should make the bond mechanically meaningful (shared morale, trauma echoes).

## Use in `cw_tutorial_chains_design_v1.md`

Anakin-led mission archetypes (see "Notable Clone Wars story patterns" above) provide ready-made tutorial chain templates. A first-tier tutorial chain "Apprenticed to Skywalker" could put a Padawan-class player through:

1. **First mission together** — a low-stakes training run; introduces the Master's voice and tolerance for deviation.
2. **First disobedience** — player violates orders in pursuit of the right outcome; Anakin defends them to the Council.
3. **First moral test** — pressured situation where the Jedi-correct answer costs lives; player chooses; Anakin's response varies based on choice.
4. **First loss** — companion or NPC the player has come to value is killed/lost; Anakin's reaction sets the tone for their relationship going forward.
5. **The breaking point** — Anakin asks the player to do something the Council would not approve of; player chooses to comply, refuse, or report.

The structural beats track Anakin/Ahsoka canon closely while leaving room for player agency.
