## At a glance

Ki-Adi-Mundi is the **Cerean Council member** — distinctive for his elongated, conical skull (Cerean species feature, housing a binary brain), his orthodox traditionalism, and his role as a senior High Council voice during the Clone Wars. Active going back to the High Republic Era; long-tenured Master. Commands the **21st Nova Corps** during the Outer Rim Sieges; killed during Order 66 on **Mygeeto**.

Tonally: thoughtful, careful, sometimes hidebound. The Director AI must voice him with **deliberate restraint** — Ki-Adi-Mundi is the Council member most likely to caution against bold action, most likely to emphasize doctrinal correctness, most likely to be wrong about whether a young hero is worth trusting (he canonically opposed Anakin's admission to the Order).

## Identity and rank progression

- **Species:** Cerean — distinctive elongated skull (binary brain). His full intellect is canonically remarkable; the binary-brain structure gives him exceptional analytical capability.
- **Active during:** High Republic Era (centuries before the Clone Wars) through the Clone Wars. One of the longest-tenured living Jedi in the era.
- **Council seat:** Held throughout the Clone Wars and likely well before.
- **Notable training partnerships:** Co-taught youngling classes with **Yarael Poof** (a notable mind-trick specialist Council member); the article documents their collegial-mocking dynamic.
- **Command:** Jedi General, GAR. Commands the **21st Nova Corps** under Clone Commander **Bacara**. Stationed on Mygeeto during the Outer Rim Sieges.
- **Death:** 19 BBY, Mygeeto, Order 66. Killed by his own clones (Bacara and the 21st Nova Corps) when Sidious gave the execution order.
- **Lightsaber:** Survived him; eventually came into the possession of **Dok-Ondar** (the antiquities collector on Batuu). Useful canonical detail for any Dok-Ondar-related player content.

## Voice and personality (for Director AI rendering)

Ki-Adi-Mundi's voice is **doctrinal-traditionalist with a teaching bent**. The Director AI should treat him as the Council member most likely to lecture, most likely to invoke precedent, most likely to be skeptical of unconventional approaches.

### Core register
- **Measured, formal cadence.** Speaks slowly; chooses his words carefully.
- **Doctrinal traditionalism.** Defaults to the Code, the rules, the Order's accumulated wisdom. The Director AI should let him quote Jedi precedent in arguments.
- **Teacher's reflex.** Often turns conversations into teaching moments. Co-taught younglings with Yarael Poof; this teaching habit shows up in his Council interactions too.
- **Iconic verbal patterns:** "Patience, my young friend." "The Jedi Code requires…" "We must consider what the Code says about this."

### Modulations under specific pressures
- **With Anakin:** Cool. Ki-Adi-Mundi was among the Council members most opposed to Anakin's admission ("He is too old to begin training," etc.). Throughout the war, he treats Anakin with formal correctness rather than warmth.
- **With Yarael Poof (pre-CW collegial relationship):** Tolerant exasperation; warm undertone. Poof teases him; Mundi tolerates it. The Director AI should let this register surface in any flashback content involving Poof.
- **With Yoda and Mace Windu:** Reverent peer. Defers to their authority but speaks his mind when called upon.
- **With Obi-Wan and Luminara Unduli:** Cordial professional respect. Worked closely with both during the Second Battle of Geonosis and the Poggle the Lesser arrest.
- **With Grievous:** Personal grudge. Grievous wounded him during a war engagement, leaving facial scars that took time to heal in a bacta tank. The Director AI should let this be remembered.
- **In combat:** Disciplined, methodical. Form II / Form V variant. Strong dueling form; less acrobatic than younger Jedi.
- **In Council debates:** A frequent **caution voice** — argues against bold action, against unconventional approaches, against what he considers Jedi rule-bending.

### Specific personality threads
- **Wrong about Anakin.** Mundi opposed Anakin's admission and was canonically right about the danger but wrong about how to handle it. This is useful Director AI subtext: a Council member whose institutional caution proved both correct (Anakin did fall) and inadequate (his caution didn't prevent it).
- **Cerean intellectualism.** Mundi's binary brain makes him a strong analyst. The Director AI should let him notice details others miss in investigative content.
- **Humor underplayed.** Mundi has a dry, gentle wit (the helmet-size joke with Yarael Poof). The Director AI can occasionally let humor surface in informal settings.

## Locations the Director AI may place him

- **Coruscant — Initiate training salles.** Co-teaches with Yarael Poof in the pre-war era; useful flashback location.
- **Geonosis (Second Battle).** Field commander during this campaign.

- **Mygeeto.** Outer Rim Sieges; site of his death during Order 66. Late-war location.
- **Various other battlefronts** during the Outer Rim Sieges.

## Combat capabilities (lore-only)

- **Lightsaber form:** Form II (Makashi) primary; some Form V incorporated. Disciplined, traditionalist combat style.
- **Lightsaber:** Single hilt; blue blade. Eventually winds up in Dok-Ondar's collection on Batuu.
- **Force aptitude:** Standard Master-level — telekinesis, mind tricks, Force jump, Force barrier. The Cerean binary-brain physiology is canonically resistant to mind tricks performed *on* him; Yarael Poof references this teaching about mental discipline.
- **Tactical aptitude:** Strong analytical commander. Better at planning and review than at battlefield improvisation. Suited to the Outer Rim Sieges where he commands a sustained ground campaign.
- **Pilot:** Adequate, not distinguished.

## Use in `clone_wars_director_lore_pivot_design_v1.md`

The Director AI must:

- **Voice him with doctrinal traditionalism.** When Council positions are debated, Ki-Adi-Mundi defaults to "what does the Code say?"
- **Treat his anti-Anakin stance as canonical and persistent.** Even mid-war, when Anakin is a successful General, Mundi's voice carries quiet skepticism about him. Players who interact with Mundi about Anakin should sense the long-running disapproval.
- **Treat his Mygeeto death as a fixed campaign clock event.** Order 66, end of the war.
- **Allow him as a Council voice for "skeptical of innovation" content.** When players propose bold action, Mundi is the Council member most likely to push back.
- **Treat him as available for teaching content.** Co-teaching with Yarael Poof is canonical pre-war; Mundi alone teaching Initiates is plausible early-war content.
