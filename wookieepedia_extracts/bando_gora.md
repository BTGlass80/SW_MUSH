## At a glance

The **Bando Gora** is a **Force-using dark-side cult** active in the galaxy across multiple eras. By the late Republic, it had degenerated from religious practice into **assassination and criminal syndicate operations**. Distinguished from other dark-side groups by its **cultic structure** (mass indoctrination, ritual practice, leader-worship) rather than apprenticeship traditions. Featured in the *Clone Wars Campaign Guide* (per `cwcg_extraction_v1.md` §4) as one of the era's catalog of non-Jedi Force-using traditions.

For SW_MUSH purposes, the Bando Gora is the **canonical "Force-using dark-side cult" reference** — useful for any player content involving cult-style Force traditions, indoctrination plots, or organized dark-side criminal operations distinct from the Sith Order's individualistic apprenticeship model.

## What's canonically known

- **A Force cult active in the galaxy.** Not Sith — the Bando Gora operate independently of the Sith Order's Rule of Two structure. They are a separate dark-side tradition with their own ideology and recruitment patterns.
- **Degenerated into assassination work.** By the late Republic, the cult had shifted from primarily religious practice to operating as a **criminal syndicate specializing in assassination and contract violence**. This degeneration is canonical lore.
- **Iconic mask.** Bando Gora members canonically wore distinctive masks — described in canon as repurposable rebreathers. The mask is the cult's visual signature; even after the cult's decline, **old Bando Gora masks turn up across the galaxy as relics**, sometimes worn by non-cultists for entirely unrelated purposes (the canonical Olega clothing-dyer Luntobetha repurposed an old Bando Gora rebreather mask as toxic-fume protection).
- **First mentioned in canon** in *Rise of the Separatists* (Fantasy Flight Games CW supplement); deeper material from Legends-era *Star Wars: Bounty Hunter* (2002 LucasArts game).

## Bando Gora in the era's broader context

Per `cwcg_extraction_v1.md` §4 (Force-Using Traditions):

- **The Bando Gora occupy a specific niche** in the Clone Wars-era catalog of non-Jedi Force-using groups: they are the **dark-side cult** archetype, complementing:
  - **The Korunnai / Pelekotan** (jungle-pragmatist neutral; Mace Windu's heritage; see `wookieepedia_extracts/korunnai.md` and `pelekotan.md`).
  - **The Believers** (a different non-Jedi Force-using sect; Legends-era reference).
  - **The Nightsisters of Dathomir** (matriarchal coven with magick tradition; see `wookieepedia_extracts/maul.md` cross-references).

The Director AI may use this catalog as a structural reference when introducing Force-tradition diversity to player content. Each tradition has a distinctive philosophical orientation; the Bando Gora's is **dark-side cultic indoctrination**.

## Voice and culture (for Director AI rendering)

The Bando Gora's canonical detail is sparse, so the Director AI has latitude to construct content while respecting the framework:

- **Cultic register.** Members speak in indoctrination cadence — references to a leader figure, ritual phrases, collective identity language ("we" rather than "I"), an apocalyptic or transformative worldview.
- **Mask-mediated identity.** The mask abstracts individual identity in favor of the cult's collective face. The Director AI should let mask-wearing be a meaningful behavioral signal — a cult member with their mask on speaks with cult-collective voice; without it, more individually.
- **Dark-side menace.** The Bando Gora are dark-side practitioners. Their Force use is canonically antagonistic. Player content involving them should treat them as genuine antagonists, not as morally ambiguous third-party figures.
- **Criminal-syndicate operational mode.** By the late Republic, the cult operates as a contract assassination organization. Player content involving them should reflect both the cult's religious framework and its mercenary day-to-day operations.

## Use in `force_resonant_landmarks_design_v1.md`

The Bando Gora provides:

- **A canonical "dark-side cult" reference** distinct from the Sith Order. Useful for player content involving alternate dark-side traditions.
- **Indoctrination plot content.** Cults canonically recruit through manipulation; player Force-sensitives might be approached for recruitment, or might be tasked with extracting a recruited NPC.
- **Iconic relic content.** The Bando Gora mask is widely scattered. Players might encounter old masks in markets, personal collections, or being worn by non-cultists. The masks themselves carry residual Force-resonance and may be sensed by Force-sensitive characters.

## Use in `clone_wars_director_lore_pivot_design_v1.md`

The Director AI must:

- **Voice Bando Gora encounters with cultic-dark-side menace.** Don't generic-criminal them; their Force-using cult identity is what distinguishes them.
- **Treat the cult as active in the galaxy during the war.** They operate beneath both Republic and CIS attention as a criminal-syndicate-with-religious-framework.
- **Allow Bando Gora content as side-arc material.** The cult is not a major war faction; it operates on the war's edges. Player content involving them should feel parallel to, rather than central to, the era's main events.
- **Treat any Bando Gora target / recruit as a meaningful character** even if a cult member. The cult uses indoctrination; cult members may have lives and identities beyond their cult role.
