## At a glance

**Onderon** is a **temperate jungle world in the Inner Rim** — pre-CW Republic member; mid-war seceded to the CIS via a coup that installed puppet-king Sanjay Rash; site of the **canonical CW Republic-backed insurgency** that liberated the world. The Onderon arc is one of the era's most concrete "small-scale planetary liberation via insurgent training" stories — Anakin, Obi-Wan, Ahsoka, and Captain Rex go to Onderon as **insurgent advisors**, not as battlefield commanders, training local fighters (Saw Gerrera, Steela Gerrera, Lux Bonteri) to overthrow the Rash regime.

For SW_MUSH purposes, Onderon templates the **insurgent advisor** content archetype — Republic Jedi or Republic operatives teaching local resistance fighters rather than commanding clone armies. This is structurally distinct from standard battlefield content and exceptionally useful for player-character agency.

## Geography and physical character

- **Location:** Inner Rim Territories. Japrael system. The Slice region.
- **Climate:** Temperate jungle. Type I atmosphere (standard breathable).
- **Capital:** **Iziz** — walled jungle city. Royal palace; markets; the public-execution arena where Rash attempts to kill Dendup.
- **Wilderness:** Jungle expanses outside the cities. The rebel base is a hidden jungle camp accessible only to those who know the path.
- **Wildlife:** Dangerous jungle predators canonically present though not specified in detail.

## Pre-Clone Wars status

- **Pre-war Republic membership.** Onderon was a Galactic Republic member world for centuries.
- **King Ramsis Dendup.** Pre-war ruler. Neutral, peace-seeking, popular among Onderonians. Refused early CIS overtures.

## Clone Wars events

### The Coup (~21 BBY)
- **Sanjay Rash** seizes the throne with CIS backing. Deposes Dendup; imprisons him. Allies Onderon formally with the Confederacy.
- **Public response.** Many Onderonians refuse to recognize Rash's legitimacy. Underground resistance forms.

### The Insurgency (~21–20 BBY)
- **Resistance leadership:**
  - **Steela Gerrera** — overall rebel commander; sister of Saw; tactical mind; killed in the final battle. Canonically a romantic interest of Lux Bonteri.
  - **Saw Gerrera** — Steela's brother; aggressive direct-action fighter. Survives to become a major Imperial-Era resistance figure. Foundational character for the broader Rebellion's "extremist faction" lineage.
  - **Lux Bonteri** — son of Mina Bonteri (deceased Separatist Senator killed by Dooku). Defected from the CIS in protest of his mother's murder; joins the Onderon resistance as a CIS-system insider with intelligence value. Canonical romantic interest of Ahsoka Tano (unrequited from her side).
- **Republic insurgent advisor mission.** Anakin, Obi-Wan, Ahsoka, and Captain Rex deploy covertly to train the rebels. **Anakin teaches battlefield tactics. Obi-Wan teaches restraint. Ahsoka serves as on-site advisor. Rex teaches small-unit infantry skills.** They do not command Republic forces in open battle; they train locals.
- **Rebel operations:** Covert sabotage in Iziz; droid patrol elimination; checkpoint destruction; eventually hijacking a droid battle tank to disable the city power grid.

### CIS escalation
- **Dooku displeased with Rash.** Sends **General Kalani** (super tactical droid) to take command. Kalani's analytical-droid approach proves more effective than Rash's incompetence — but still cannot defeat the rebels.
- **The Tandin defection.** **General Akenathen Tandin** (Onderon Royal Guard) — initially Rash-loyal — interrogates a captured Saw Gerrera, hears Saw's case for Onderonian self-determination, and ultimately refuses to comply with Kalani's torture orders. Tandin defects to the rebels with the Royal Guard.

### The Liberation
- **Public execution attempt.** Rash orders Dendup's public execution in Iziz. The rebels intervene; Tandin's defected Royal Guard intervenes; Ahsoka jumps out of the crowd to disable droids with the Force; Steela rescues Dendup. Rash deposed. Onderon rejoins the Republic.
- **Steela's death.** Steela Gerrera killed during the final battle — falls from a cliff during the rebel rescue operation. Saw and Lux are devastated; Ahsoka is at her side as she dies.

## Voice and culture (for Director AI rendering)

### Onderonian register
- **Jungle frontier cadence.** Onderonians speak with a slightly clipped, practical accent. Long memory for grievances; deep loyalty to King Dendup as the legitimate ruler.
- **Democratic instinct.** Onderonians distinguish between legitimate rule (Dendup, who consulted with his people) and imposed rule (Rash, the CIS puppet). The Director AI should let this distinction surface in dialogue.

### Saw Gerrera register
- **Aggressive, conviction-driven.** Saw speaks with the absolutism of a man who has seen too much. The Director AI should let his voice carry the weight of someone who will become harder over time. Pre-Imperial Saw is fierce but not yet the extremist he becomes; the seeds are visible.

### Steela Gerrera register
- **Tactical leader cadence.** Calmer than Saw; a commander rather than a fighter. The Director AI should let her be the rebel voice players might genuinely follow into combat.

### Lux Bonteri register
- **Educated political defector.** Speaks with a Senatorial polish (his mother trained him); carries grief and conviction in equal measure. The Director AI should let his voice be the rebel content most accessible to Republic-side players.

## Use in `clone_wars_director_lore_pivot_design_v1.md`

The Director AI must:

- **Treat Onderon as available for the entire mid-war period.** Pre-coup Republic Onderon, post-coup CIS-occupied Onderon, post-liberation reintegrated Onderon — all distinct atmospheric eras.
- **Voice the Gerrera siblings distinctly.** Steela's tactical command differs from Saw's aggressive conviction.
- **Treat Lux Bonteri as the era's "redeemed Separatist" archetype.** Players who want a CIS-defector NPC have Lux as the canonical model.
- **Treat Steela's death as a fixed campaign clock event.** During the liberation, Steela falls. Players present at the moment witness it.

## Use in `cw_tutorial_chains_design_v1.md`

The Onderon insurgent-advisor arc is **one of the era's cleanest tutorial chain templates**. Five canonical beats:

1. **Arrival and assessment.** Republic advisors land covertly; assess the resistance's capability; meet the leadership.
2. **Initial training.** Teach combat basics, sabotage tactics, force-multiplier doctrine. Player Padawan or operative content fits here.
3. **First operation.** Small-scale sabotage proves the rebels can hurt the occupiers. Confidence-building.
4. **Escalation and CIS response.** Kalani arrives; the war intensifies; new tactics required.
5. **Liberation and loss.** Final battle; victory at cost; Steela dies; Dendup restored.

This template is reusable for other player content — any "small-world insurgency under occupier" arc can borrow this structure.
