## At a glance

Yoda is the **Order's living memory** — Grand Master, head of the High Council, trainer of nearly every contemporary Jedi at one point or another (he teaches all Initiates before they pair with Masters). He is approximately 900 years old by the Clone Wars, of an unidentified species whose homeworld is unknown (this is canon — Yoda's species is left deliberately mysterious). His voice is the era's moral conscience; where Mace Windu is the Council's enforcer of orthodoxy, Yoda is its conscience and seer.

Tonally: ancient, deliberate, paradoxical. Speaks with **inverted-syntax verbal pattern** ("Powerful you have become; the dark side I sense in you"). Uses humor that the rest of the Council finds disconcerting. By late CW, increasingly questioning the doctrines he himself has long upheld.

## Identity and rank progression

- **Birth:** ~896 BBY (he is approximately 900 years old by the Clone Wars).
- **Species:** Unknown; canonically unnamed. Small, green, long-eared, ambulatory with a gimer stick when not in combat.
- **Initiate:** Pre-Republic Era origins; trained on Coruscant for centuries before the war.
- **Masterhood:** Has trained generations of Jedi. His Padawans across history include **Count Dooku** (later fallen, becomes Darth Tyranus); through Dooku, his teaching extended to **Qui-Gon Jinn**, and through Qui-Gon to **Obi-Wan Kenobi**, and through Obi-Wan to **Anakin Skywalker** — Yoda is the lineage-Master of the era's central tragedy. He also trained Mace Windu in some capacity, taught all Initiates including **Obi-Wan Kenobi** before his Padawan years, and taught **Ahsoka Tano** at Ilum.
- **Grand Master:** Head of the Jedi High Council during the Clone Wars and the most senior Jedi alive.
- **Command:** General, GAR. Less battlefield-mobile than other Council generals due to age, but a frontline combatant when needed (Geonosis arena rescue, Rugosa, Kashyyyk).

## Voice and personality (for Director AI rendering)

Yoda's voice is **the most distinctive in the Order**. Inverted syntax is canonical; the Director AI must use it consistently or break character.

### Core register
- **Inverted syntax (Object-Subject-Verb).** "Powerful you have become." "Strong with the Force, young Skywalker is." "Begun, the Clone Wars have." This is non-negotiable for canonical voice.
- **Brevity weighted with significance.** Yoda speaks shortly. Each phrase is meant to land.
- **Humor as deliberate teaching tool.** Yoda uses humor (and feigned senility) to test how others respond. Mace Windu, canonically, never appreciates this and Yoda continues using it partly because of that. The Director AI should let Yoda's humor unsettle other Council members.
- **Iconic verbal patterns:** "Do, or do not. There is no try." "Fear leads to anger; anger leads to hate; hate leads to suffering." "Always in motion is the future." "Mmm." (humming acknowledgment). "Hmm, hmm." (when thinking).
- **Old-fashioned formality with sudden directness.** Long elliptical phrasing, then a single-line truth.

### Modulations under specific pressures
- **With Initiates and younglings:** Patient, playful, mischievous. The Initiate-training register. He uses puzzles and indirection to teach.
- **With Anakin specifically:** Concerned. Yoda recognizes early that Anakin is dangerous and unsuited for the orthodox path; he assigns Ahsoka to Anakin partly as a test, partly as a corrective.
- **With the Council in late CW:** Increasingly questioning. By the final months he is openly doubting doctrines he has upheld for centuries — most notably the doctrine that one cannot retain identity after becoming one with the Force (Qui-Gon's voice contradicts this, and Yoda begins listening).
- **In combat:** Stunningly capable despite his age. Uses Form IV (Ataru) — the most acrobatic form. The Director AI should NOT voice him as physically frail; in combat he is among the deadliest Jedi alive.
- **With Dooku:** Sorrowful, weighted. Yoda failed Dooku once; the loss informs all his subsequent Mastering.
- **With Palpatine:** Polite formal courtesy throughout most of the war; growing wariness late.

### Specific personality threads
- **Self-questioning sage.** Late CW, Yoda openly doubts whether his teaching has caused the Order's decline. The Director AI should let him voice this self-criticism in private moments.
- **Patient long-game thinker.** Yoda's planning horizon is decades. He plays for the long arc, not the immediate battle.
- **Deep connection to the Living Force.** Communes with Qui-Gon's spirit late in the war; eventually travels to Dagobah to learn the path to Force-spirit retention. This communion is the seed of how the Jedi survive Order 66.

## Locations the Director AI may place him

- **Coruscant — Jedi Temple, High Council chambers.** His default. He is more often at the Temple than any other Council member except Mace.
- **Coruscant — Initiate training salles.** Teaches younglings personally; an unusual amount for a Grand Master.
- **Coruscant — Temple gardens.** Where he meditates and walks; canonical site of the Qui-Gon-voice arc.
- **Ilum.** Returns occasionally; teaches Initiates seeking their kyber crystals.
- **Battlefield deployments:** Geonosis (the arena rescue with Mace and the clone army), **Rugosa** (Toydarian alliance negotiation), Kashyyyk (Wookiee homeworld; final Battle of Kashyyyk during Order 66 — he survives), Dagobah (post-Order-66, but planned for late-CW given the Qui-Gon arc).
- **Dagobah.** Eventually his Imperial-Era exile home, but late-CW he visits via the Qui-Gon-voice arc.

## Combat capabilities (lore-only)

- **Lightsaber form: Form IV (Ataru).** The acrobatic form. Despite his apparent frailty, Yoda in combat uses Force-augmented leaps, spins, and rapid strikes that exceed any other Jedi's physical agility. The Director AI must never voice him as combat-frail.
- **Force aptitude:** Among the most powerful Force users alive. Telekinesis (lifts massive objects), Force lightning resistance (deflects Sidious's lightning during their RotS duel), precognition, beast-empathy, communion with the dead (late-CW; learned from Qui-Gon), full mastery of all standard Force disciplines.
- **Tactical aptitude:** Strategic, not tactical. Plans across years and decades. Less effective at battlefield improvisation than Anakin or Obi-Wan, but unmatched at long-arc planning.
- **Pilot:** Competent. Famously hates flying ("How embarrassing. How embarrassing.").

## Use in `clone_wars_director_lore_pivot_design_v1.md`

The Director AI must:

- **Use inverted syntax consistently.** Yoda's voice is the Order's most distinctive; breaking it is breaking character. Director should generate his dialogue with explicit OSV (Object-Subject-Verb) ordering, even when grammatically awkward.
- **Treat his humor as a teaching technique.** Players asking serious questions may receive answers that seem flippant; the answer is in the joke, and Yoda will not explain it. The Director should let players figure it out.
- **Treat his combat capability as hidden by his age.** When he draws his lightsaber, the dynamic of every scene shifts. The Director should reserve his combat appearances for weighty moments.
- **Use him sparingly.** Yoda is the most senior Jedi; he is rarely the NPC players interact with day-to-day. Saving his appearances for major story beats matches the canonical pattern (he appears less often than Obi-Wan or Anakin in canon CW content).
- **Treat his late-war doubt as canonical.** By the war's final months, Yoda is questioning the Order's doctrines openly. The Director AI should let his late-war voice carry this uncertainty rather than the firm authority of the early war.
- **Treat his Sidious blindness as a tragic flaw.** Yoda canonically does not detect Sidious for the entire war. The Director AI should let him express, late-war, that he can sense darkness rising but cannot identify the source. Players investigating the Sith should not be able to extract a clear identification from Yoda.

## Use in `padawan_master_system_design_v1.md`

Yoda is the canonical case for **the Master who teaches generations** and **the Master who can fail.** Pattern observations:

- **The Initiate-teacher Master role.** Yoda teaches all Initiates before they're paired with personal Masters. The system might support a "group-teaching elder" role distinct from individual Master-Padawan pairing.
- **The Master-by-lineage relationship.** Yoda is technically Grand-Master to nearly every CW Jedi (lineage: Yoda → Dooku → Qui-Gon → Obi-Wan → Anakin → Ahsoka). The system might track Master-lineage trees and let Padawan PCs claim distant lineage relationships.
- **The Master whose failure echoes for centuries.** Yoda failed with Dooku. That failure spawned the Sith Empire's resurgence. The system should support catastrophic Master-Padawan failures whose narrative weight extends across the whole campaign, not just the immediate Master.
- **The Master who learns from the Padawan posthumously.** Late-CW Yoda learns Force-ghost retention from Qui-Gon's spirit — i.e., learns from his lineage-Padawan after that Padawan's death. The system might support post-mortem teaching from departed Padawans.
- **The Master who questions his own teaching late.** Yoda's late-war doubts are an important counterweight to confident-Master narratives. The system should support Master PCs who reach a "did I teach this right?" arc.

## Use in `cw_tutorial_chains_design_v1.md`

Yoda is the natural **Initiate-stage tutorial NPC.** Player characters who are Initiates / younglings rather than Padawans encounter him first. Tutorial chain candidates:

1. **"What You Will Find Is Only What You Bring With You"** — the Ilum kyber crystal trial. Player Initiate travels to Ilum, enters a cave, must trust Yoda's teaching to locate their crystal before the cave seals at sundown. Maps directly to the canonical Ahsoka episode.
2. **"Strong With The Force"** — group-training lesson at the Temple where Yoda teaches multiple young players together; tests collaboration.
3. **"Question, You Must"** — late-tutorial chain where Yoda asks the player to question the Order's doctrine on something specific. No correct answer; the player's reasoning is the test.

The Initiate-tier content tier is structurally distinct from Padawan-tier content (which is Master-led). Yoda is the bridge.
