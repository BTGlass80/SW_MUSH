## At a glance

**Utapau** is the **Outer Rim sinkhole world** that hosts the Clone Wars's penultimate canonical battle — General Grievous's last stand and Obi-Wan Kenobi's final CW victory before Order 66 commences. Located in the Utapau system in the Western Reaches; rocky desert planet with a surface rendered nearly uninhabitable by storms and erosion. Most Utapauns live in the planet's massive **sinkholes** — vast vertical chasms that connect to the planetary subsurface and provide shelter from the violent surface conditions.

For SW_MUSH purposes, Utapau is **a fixed campaign clock anchor** — the location where the Clone Wars effectively ends. Pre-Battle-of-Utapau Utapau is a CIS-occupied background world with mining operations; post-Battle Utapau is the world Sidious chose as the war's terminus.

## Geography and physical character

- **Location:** Outer Rim Territories, Western Reaches, Utapau system, grid square N-19. Type I atmosphere (standard breathable). Rocky desert planet.
- **Surface:** Inhospitable. Violent storms; tidal-driven erosion from moons; constant chasm collapses. Almost no permanent surface settlement.
- **Sinkholes:** The planet's defining feature. Massive vertical chasms (kilometers deep) cratered into the rocky terrain. Each major sinkhole hosts a city built into the chasm walls — homes, markets, palaces, government buildings cut directly into the rock.
- **World-Ocean:** Beneath the surface, a massive subterranean body of water connected by tidal currents from Utapau's moons. The ocean's currents drive the surface erosion and sinkhole formation.
- **Pau City** — the capital, located in the largest sinkhole, ruled by Port Administrator **Tion Medon**.

## Inhabitants

- **Pau'ans** — tall, hairless, gaunt humanoids; long-lived; wise; the planet's administrative class. Tion Medon is the canonical Pau'an leader.
- **Utai** — shorter, large-eyed humanoids; the planet's working class. Care for **varactyls** (large reptilian mounts) and other domesticated creatures.
- **Varactyls** — large reptilian mounts, capable of climbing sheer chasm walls. Obi-Wan's iconic mount **Boga** is a varactyl.
- **Population in sinkholes.** Most Utapauns live in the largest, most stabilized continent's sinkhole cities.

## Pre-Clone Wars status

- **Generally peaceful.** Utapau was a peripheral Outer Rim world without strong galactic profile. Member of the broader Outer Rim economy.
- **Techno Union mining operations.** Wat Tambor's Techno Union held significant mining contracts on the planet pre-CW; this connection drew the planet into CIS occupation.

## Clone Wars events on Utapau

### CIS occupation (mid-to-late CW)
- **Techno Union dominance.** With the war's outbreak, Utapau is occupied by Separatist forces. Tion Medon and the Pau'an government formally cooperate under duress; covertly resent the occupation.
- **Grievous's relocation (~19 BBY, just before the Battle of Coruscant).** As the war winds toward its end, Sidious orchestrates Grievous's withdrawal to Utapau. The CIS presence concentrates here.

### The Battle of Utapau (19 BBY) — the era's final canonical battle
- **Republic intelligence.** **Sidious betrays Grievous's location** to the Republic — Sidious wants Grievous killed to clear the path for the Empire's declaration. The Jedi Council dispatches Obi-Wan Kenobi.
- **Obi-Wan's deployment.** **General Kenobi and the 212th Attack Battalion** under **Marshal Commander Cody** deploy to Pau City. Tion Medon covertly informs Kenobi that Grievous is in the lower sinkhole levels.
- **Single-person infiltration.** Kenobi enters alone, riding **Boga** the varactyl. Confronts Grievous in his command center. Their duel runs across the chasm walls.
- **Kenobi kills Grievous.** Iconic line — "So uncivilized." — as Kenobi shoots Grievous's exposed organic heart-pouch with a blaster pistol after kicking it open during their duel.
- **Order 66 at Utapau.** Within hours of Grievous's death, **Sidious issues Order 66 to all GAR forces.** Commander Cody receives the order from his communicator; commands his AT-TE crew to shoot down Kenobi and Boga. Boga is killed in the fall; Kenobi survives by falling into the deep grotto and finds an underwater escape route.
- **Kenobi escapes.** Obi-Wan eventually exfiltrates to a Republic vessel; reunites with Yoda; the two of them go to Coruscant to investigate the Jedi Temple.

## Voice and culture (for Director AI rendering)

### Pau'an register (administrative class)
- **Slow, formal, ceremonial cadence.** Pau'ans are long-lived; they speak with the patience of elders. Tion Medon's voice is canonically grave and measured.
- **Diplomatic indirection under occupation.** The Pau'an leadership has practice speaking around Separatist guards. The Director AI should let Pau'an dialogue carry quiet subtext during occupation content.

### Utai register (working class)
- **Practical, working-class.** Utai handle the labor — varactyl care, mining work, transport. Less educated than Pau'an administrators; warmer in disposition.

### CIS occupation register
- **Standard CIS / battle droid presence.** Tactical droids (TX-series), B1 battle droids, occasional droideka security. Wat Tambor visits periodically.

## Use in `clone_wars_director_lore_pivot_design_v1.md`

The Director AI must:

- **Treat Utapau as available CIS-occupation content during the late war.** Players can encounter the sinkhole cities under occupation; covert resistance contacts (Tion Medon-style); Pau'an civilians under duress.
- **Treat the Battle of Utapau (19 BBY) as a fixed campaign clock event.** Grievous dies here. Obi-Wan kills him. Order 66 commences within hours.
- **Voice the Pau'an / Utai register distinctly.** Don't generic-Outer-Rim them; the species's distinctive cadence is canonical.
- **Allow varactyl mounts as a memorable detail.** Players who visit Utapau should encounter or ride varactyls if relevant. The wall-climbing mobility is unique.
- **Treat the sinkhole architecture as atmospheric backbone.** Utapau scenes should canonically feel vertically scaled — chasm walls, climbing platforms, vertigo distance to the chasm floor.
