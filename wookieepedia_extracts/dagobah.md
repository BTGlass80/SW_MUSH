## At a glance

**Dagobah** is the **swamp planet of the Western Reaches** — Outer Rim, water-saturated, vegetation-choked, steeped in Force resonance. Canonically described as "a world strong in the Force." Most famously associated with Yoda's Imperial-Era exile (out of CW project scope), but **the Force-resonance and the Cave of Evil both predate the CW** and are accessible during the Clone Wars era for player content.

For SW_MUSH purposes, Dagobah is **the canonical "deep wilderness Force-resonant world" reference** — useful for any player content involving Force visions, isolation training, or confrontation with personal darkness.

## Geography and physical character

- **Location:** Outer Rim Territories, Western Reaches, Dagobah system, grid square M-19. Off standard hyperspace lanes.
- **Composition:** Swamp planet — extensive bogs, dense vegetation, tropical climate, near-constant rain (especially during the wet season). Highlands provide drier terrain that becomes important during seasonal flooding.
- **Atmosphere:** Standard breathable, but humid and mist-heavy. Visibility frequently limited.
- **Wildlife:** Dragonsnakes, snake-like serpents, swamp slugs (Dagobah's species canonically vary; specifics emerge in player content as needed).
- **No sentient native population.** Dagobah has no permanent civilized settlement during the era; visitors are rare.
- **Hyperspace isolation.** Dagobah's location is canonically difficult to reach; not on commercial routes; reaching it requires either explicit knowledge of the coordinates or Force-guided navigation.

## Force-resonant features

### The Cave of Evil
- **Canonical Force-resonant landmark.** A cave on Dagobah that radiates dark-side energy; canonically described as "what's in there is what you take with you" — i.e., it shows visitors visions of their own personal darkness.
- **Predates the Clone Wars era.** Yoda canonically positions his eventual exile shelter "near to the dark side cave but out of range of its influence" — meaning the cave existed before his arrival.
- **Active during the Clone Wars.** Players who reach Dagobah during the war could canonically encounter the cave and its visions.
- **Vision content.** Force-sensitive visitors experience personal-darkness visions; the cave does not show generic horrors but tailored confrontations with the visitor's own fears, attachments, regrets, and shadow-self.

### Force-resonance more broadly
- Per High Republic-era academic papers, Dagobah is canonically "a world strong in the Force." The whole planet, not just the cave, has Force resonance.
- This makes Dagobah a useful setting for Force-vision content beyond the cave itself.

## Pre-Imperial-Era status

- **Pre-CW.** Dagobah is unsettled, undisturbed, occasionally visited by Force-sensitive seekers. The High Republic-era academic record about Dagobah's Force-strength comes from pre-CW Jedi Order documentation.
- **CW-era status.** Yoda's late-CW Force-vision arc (per `wookieepedia_extracts/yoda.md`) involves a planned visit to Dagobah following Qui-Gon's spirit's instruction. This canonically connects Dagobah to the era's late events.
- **Notable visitor — Madelin Sun.** A former Sava (high-rank Jedi Master who had retired or stepped back from the Order) traveled to Dagobah seeking Yoda. She was attacked by a swamp creature; saved by Yoda; then guided into the Cave of Evil where her own visions dissuaded her from continuing the search. She left the planet without finding Yoda. (This canonical example is useful as a model for player content: Force-sensitive seekers may find more about themselves on Dagobah than they find about whoever they sought.)

## Voice and culture (for Director AI rendering)

There is no native Dagobah society to voice. The Director AI's relevant register is **environmental and Force-vision**.

- **Environmental atmosphere.** Mist, dripping vegetation, distant creature calls, the squelch of swamp underfoot. Player content here should canonically convey these sensory elements.
- **Force-vision register.** When players enter the Cave of Evil or experience Force-visions on the planet, the Director AI should voice the visions in a register distinct from normal NPC speech — uncanny, second-person, identifying personal truths the player may not have voiced. The visions canonically appear to know more about the player than the player has revealed.
- **Yoda (when present, late CW).** Standard Yoda inverted-syntax register (per `wookieepedia_extracts/yoda.md`). Late-CW Yoda visits Dagobah following Qui-Gon's communion arc.

## Use in `force_resonant_landmarks_design_v1.md`

Dagobah is the **canonical wilderness Force-resonant landmark** for the design — distinct from Ilum (sacred Jedi ritual space), the Lothal Jedi Temple (Order-built sacred site), or Mortis (mythic Force-vergence). Dagobah is **wild Force-resonance** — the planet itself is alive in the Force, not just specific structures or sites.

Pattern observation:
- **The Cave of Evil templates the "Force-trial confrontation"** scene type. Players enter a Force-resonant location alone, experience visions of their personal darkness, and emerge with self-knowledge. Reusable structure for Force-sensitive player content beyond Dagobah specifically.

## Use in `clone_wars_director_lore_pivot_design_v1.md`
