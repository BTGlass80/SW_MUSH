## At a glance

**Coruscant** is the **galactic capital** during the Clone Wars era — the Core Worlds ecumenopolis (city-planet), seat of the Galactic Republic, location of the Galactic Senate, the Jedi Temple, and the Chancellor's Office. The world's coordinates are designated **0, 0, 0** by galactic mapping convention; it is the *referential* center of the galaxy even though it is not the geographic one. Trillions of permanent residents inhabit a planet-spanning, multi-thousand-level city.

For SW_MUSH purposes, Coruscant is **the central location of the era's pivot**. Most player content during the Clone Wars era will pass through Coruscant. The city has detailed landmark resources already extracted in `wookieepedia_extracts/coruscant_underworld.md`; this article complements that with **upper-level Coruscant content**.

## Geography and physical character

- **Type:** Ecumenopolis. The entire surface is built up — no natural land remains. Vertical city development has produced thousands of habitable levels, with the lowest fifty already buried over 100,000 years before the era.
- **Location:** Core Worlds. Major hyperspace lane junction.
- **Climate (artificial):** The upper levels have engineered weather that approximates temperate variability. The lower levels develop their own microclimates from thermal venting, industrial exhaust, and stagnant air. Sunlight does not reach below approximately level 1500.
- **Notable surface feature:** **Umate** — the only natural mountain peak left exposed. Surrounded by greenery during the High Republic era as an attempted recreation of pre-development Coruscant. By the CW era this is a tourist site.

## Political/administrative structure (CW era)

- **Senate Building** — galactic Senate seat. Domed structure with floating senatorial pods. (Senate District.)
- **Chancellor's Office** — Palpatine's official quarters and political seat. (Senate District / 500 Republica building region.)
- **Jedi Temple** — Jedi headquarters. Massive five-spired structure on a Force-resonant location (Sith shrine buried beneath, unknown to the Jedi but known to Sidious).
- **Coruscant Security Force (CSF)** — civilian police; coordinates with Coruscant Guard for capital security.
- **Coruscant Guard** — clone-trooper garrison force; distinctive red markings; commanded by Commander **Fox** during the CW.
- **Federal District** — administrative center.
- **Senate District** — government and elite housing.
- **CoCo Town** — middle-class commercial district. **Dex's Diner** is here.
- **Uscru District** — entertainment district. (See `wookieepedia_extracts/coruscant_underworld.md` for detail.)

## The Works (notable industrial-zone landmark)

A large sector and industrial area; once a great manufacturing district producing spacecraft parts, construction droids, and building materials. Centuries before the Clone Wars, increasingly high costs on Coruscant prompted most manufacturers to shift to off-planet facilities. **By the CW era, the Works mainly consists of long-abandoned buildings and empty warehouses,** many of which have been appropriated by criminals, with large areas rendered uninhabitable by toxic waste.

Notable canonical events at The Works:

- **Sidious-Tyranus clandestine meetings.** A derelict hangar in The Works was used by Darth Sidious and Darth Tyranus for face-to-face Sith Lord meetings during the Clone Wars. Useful Director AI content for player investigation arcs.
- **The Dooku-Yaddle duel.** Dooku's fall to the dark side included a duel in The Works in which he killed Jedi Master Yaddle. This event canonically completed his transition to Darth Tyranus.

The Works is therefore a **Force-resonant landmark candidate** despite not being on Wookieepedia's normal Force-resonant list — Sith activity has left traces.

## Coruscant population and culture

- **Trillions of permanent residents.** Cosmopolitan; humans dominant but multi-species community throughout. Galactic Basic Standard primary language.
- **Class stratification by altitude.** Wealth correlates with height. Senators and elites live in the spires; middle-class in the surface districts; working class below; the underworld extends down into poverty, refugees, and criminal communities.
- **Roulette-table animals.** A canonical detail: the small reptiles used as cues in a Coruscanti roulette game evolved through mutations from the planet's gutters — possibly from a union of lizards and rats. Useful flavor for casino/cantina content.
- **Tourist economy (pre-war).** Pre-Clone Wars Coruscant was a tourist destination; cultural shows, opera, dining, sightseeing. Wartime decreases tourism but does not eliminate it.

## Voice and culture (for Director AI rendering)

The Director AI must select Coruscant register based on **which level of the city** content is set in.

### Upper-level register (Senate District, Federal District, Jedi Temple, 500 Republica)
- **Cosmopolitan formality.** Politicians, bureaucrats, Jedi, military officers. Standard Galactic Basic with educated cadence.
- **Wartime weariness.** By mid-war, even the upper levels feel the conflict — anti-war protests outside the Jedi Temple, Senate-floor speeches, increased security.
- **Class confidence.** Upper-level Coruscanti speak with the assumption that they are at the center of the galaxy and matter accordingly.

### Middle-level register (CoCo Town, working districts)
- **Practical, friendly, multi-species.** Dex's Diner is the canonical example — diverse, comfortable, business-as-usual even during the war.
- **Working-class accents** vary by species and district.

### Lower-level register (entertainment districts, industrial zones)
- **Edge-of-respectability.** Uscru's upper-tier sites (Galaxies Opera House) are still respectable; Uscru's lower-tier sites (Outlander Club, The POTU) are seedier.
- **Industrial decay.** The Works and similar zones are nearly empty, echoing, dangerous.

## Use in `clone_wars_director_lore_pivot_design_v1.md`

The Director AI must:

- **Treat Coruscant as the era's most-default location.** Most major Jedi NPCs default to the Temple here. Most political content happens in the Senate. Most Anakin content involves Coruscant scenes.
- **Layer the city's altitudes.** Players should experience clear differences between upper, middle, and lower content. Movement vertically through the city is part of the era's atmospheric texture.
- **Treat the Sith shrine beneath the Jedi Temple as deep lore.** Most Jedi don't know it exists. Sidious does. Players who somehow uncover it gain rare and dangerous knowledge.
- **Treat the Empire transition (19 BBY, immediately after Order 66) as a fixed pivot.** The Senate becomes the Imperial Senate; statues are erected; aesthetic shifts toward severe dark architecture. Out of CW project scope, but the transition itself is part of the era's end.

## Use in `from_dust_to_stars_design_v2_clone_wars.md`

Coruscant is the **canonical Core Worlds reference** for the design. As the galaxy's referential center, it shapes how all other worlds are described relative to it:

- **Outer Rim worlds** are described by their distance/difference from Coruscant.
- **Mid Rim worlds** balance Coruscant-influence with regional independence.
- **Core Worlds** orbit politically and economically around Coruscant.
