## At a glance

The Republic's clone special-forces program produced **two distinct elite branches**: the **Advanced Recon Commandos (ARCs)** and the **Republic Commandos** (also called Clone Commandos). Both are elite clone units; both received intensified training; both operated outside standard line-trooper deployment. They are **not the same unit**: ARCs are individual operatives often attached to standard units for specialty operations; Republic Commandos work in **four-man squads** for surgical infiltration. The distinction is canonically affirmed.

For SW_MUSH purposes, these are the **canonical references for elite clone player content** — alongside Clone Force 99 (the Bad Batch), they provide the established models for non-standard clone party composition.

## Advanced Recon Commandos (ARCs)

### Composition
- **Individual operatives.** Selected from standard clone troopers based on aptitude.
- **Designation:** "ARC Trooper" or "ARC Commander" (rank variants).
- **Loaned to standard units.** ARCs are typically attached to specific legions (the 501st has multiple ARCs at various points; the Wolfpack and other units also host ARCs).
- **Distinct armor.** Phase I ARC armor and later Phase II ARC armor; both feature distinctive kama (waist-cloth), pauldron, and additional gear; visually marked as elite.

### Training
- Intensified beyond standard clone training. ARCs receive additional combat instruction, often beyond what Kaminoan management formally sanctions.
- Some ARCs (the Null-class and Alpha-class in deeper canon) trained personally by **Jango Fett** before his death — these are the original prototypes for the program.
- ARC Commanders **Blitz, Colt, and Havoc** lead the **Rancor Battalion**.

### Notable named ARCs
- **Fives (CT-5555)** — 501st ARC trooper. Discovers the inhibitor chip conspiracy on Kamino. Killed on Coruscant before he can warn the Jedi. The era's most consequential individual clone tragedy.
- **Echo (CT-1409)** — 501st ARC trooper. Captured at the Citadel; presumed dead; recovered as a Techno Union cyborg interface during the Anaxes campaign. Joins Clone Force 99.
- **Jesse (CT-5597)** — 501st clone lieutenant who is promoted to ARC trooper after Fives's death. Killed in the Order 66 *Venator* crash with Ahsoka.
- **Captain Ordo, Captain Maze** — Null-class ARCs in deeper Legends-canon (mentioned as part of the broader ARC program).

### Equipment
- **Reciprocating quad blaster cannons.**
- **Z-6 rotary blaster cannons.**
- **JT-12 jetpacks** for aerial deployment.
- **AT-FRC Force Reconnaissance Walker** — Kuat Drive Yards-built scout walker designed for ARC operations late in the war.

### Voice and culture
- **Confidence backed by capability.** ARCs know they're elite and act accordingly. Less subordinate to drill discipline than standard troopers; more individually-minded.
- **Strong personal voice.** Named ARCs (Fives, Echo) develop distinctive personalities. The Director AI should let ARCs have stronger individuality than standard line clones.

## Republic Commandos (Clone Commandos)

### Composition
- **Four-man squads.** Each squad designated by a Greek letter — **Delta Squad** (the most famous), **Omega Squad**, others.
- **Designations:** "RC-####" (e.g., RC-1138 Boss, RC-1140 Sev, RC-1207 Scorch, RC-1262 Fixer for Delta Squad).
- **Specialized roles within squad.** Each commando trains for a primary role: leader/commander, sniper/marksman, demolitions/ordnance, technician/slicer. Squads operate as integrated units where each role complements the others.

### Training
- **Trained by Mandalorian instructors.** Per Legends canon (Karen Traviss novels), some Mandalorian instructors (Kal Skirata being the most notable) trained the Commando program. This canonically influences Commando culture — they speak Mando'a, hold to Mandalorian honor traditions, develop closer bonds with their trainers than typical clones do.
- **Distinct training tracks** from standard troopers and ARCs. The training is meant to produce **integrated four-man teams** rather than individual operatives.

### Notable squads
- **Delta Squad:** Boss (RC-1138), Sev (RC-1140), Scorch (RC-1262), Fixer (RC-1207). The flagship Republic Commando squad. Multiple deployments across the war (Geonosis, Kashyyyk).
- **Omega Squad:** Karen Traviss's novel-series squad. Highly developed in Legends canon.

### Equipment
- **DC-17m interchangeable weapons system** — modular weapon that can configure as blaster rifle, sniper rifle, or anti-armor.
- **Specialized armor** with integrated comms, environmental seals, and specialty kit.

### Voice and culture
- **Mando-influenced cadence.** Republic Commandos speak with a Mandalorian-inflected vocabulary (*aliit*, *vode*, *cyar'ika*) and treat their squad as family-equivalent.
- **Tight squad bond.** Commandos canonically die together more often than they survive without their squad. The four-man unit is the operational and emotional center.

## Key distinction (canonical clarification)

**ARC troopers and Clone Commandos are separate classifications.** Per *Star Wars: The Secrets of the Clone Troopers* and *Star Wars: Battlefront II* canonical assertion:

- **ARC = individual operative**, often attached to a standard unit for specialty work.
- **Clone Commando = four-man integrated squad**, deployed as a unit for surgical operations.

The Director AI should respect this distinction in player content. A player party of "elite clones" might be either an ARC strike team (individual elites pulled together for one mission) or a Republic Commando squad (a permanent four-man unit). These have different operational rhythms.

## Use in `weight_of_war_design_v1.md`

These are **two distinct canonical models** for elite clone player content:

- **ARC trooper model.** Individual specialist; high autonomy; attached to standard unit for missions. Player ARCs would have rich personal arcs and might rotate through different units.
- **Republic Commando squad model.** Four-man permanent team; tight cohesion; specialty role distribution. Player parties of four would map directly onto a Commando squad.
- **Both alongside Clone Force 99.** The Bad Batch (5+ members, mutation-enhanced specialists) is a third elite model. Players can choose any of the three as their party-composition reference.

## Use in `clone_wars_director_lore_pivot_design_v1.md`

The Director AI must:

- **Distinguish ARC from Commando in voice and operations.** Don't conflate them.
- **Voice ARCs with strong individual character.** Fives, Echo, and Jesse demonstrate the canonical pattern.
- **Voice Commandos with Mando-cadence.** Mando'a vocabulary, squad-as-family register.
- **Use Fives's death as canonical late-war tragedy.** Players in late-CW Coruscant content may encounter Fives's last warning attempt; his death without delivery is a fixed campaign clock event.
- **Use the four-man squad as a Commando default.** Even if players see only one Commando, the others are usually nearby; squads rarely split.
