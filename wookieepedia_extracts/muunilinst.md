## At a glance

**Muunilinst** is the **Muun homeworld** and the **headquarters of the InterGalactic Banking Clan (IGBC)** — the financial powerhouse that funds the CIS and serves as the central reference for galactic banking during the Clone Wars era. Located in the Outer Rim Territories, New Territories region. Strategically and economically pivotal for any CIS-side or Republic-financial player content.

For SW_MUSH purposes, Muunilinst is the **canonical "banking world" reference** — the place where the Confederacy's money comes from, the world Republic-side investigators must engage to uncover the war's financial architecture.

## Geography and physical character

- **Location:** Outer Rim Territories, New Territories region, Muunilinst system, grid square K-4.
- **Composition:** Standard Type I atmosphere planet. Heavily developed; the planetary surface canonically dominated by **banking institutions, financial computing facilities, and corporate towers**. The architecture is austere, monumental, and serious — designed to project financial gravity rather than warmth.
- **Climate:** Temperate. Comfortable for Muun and human alike.

## Inhabitants

- **Muuns** — tall, slender, gaunt, long-faced humanoids. Pale-skinned. Long-lived (Muun lifespans canonically extend well past human norms). Known for **mathematical and financial aptitude** that exceeds most other species's baseline. Cold-disposed; transactional in social interaction; intensely loyal to family and clan financial interests.
- **Notable Muuns (CW era):**
  - **San Hill** — Chairman of the InterGalactic Banking Clan. Member of the Separatist Council. Calculating banker; treats the Clone Wars as a balance-sheet event. (See `wookieepedia_extracts/confederacy_of_independent_systems.md`.)
  - **Hego Damask** — Per Legends, Darth Plagueis (Sidious's master) was a Muun. The Muun species's connection to Sith political-economic operations runs deep.

## The InterGalactic Banking Clan

- **Headquartered on Muunilinst.** The IGBC is the era's most powerful financial institution.
- **Funds the CIS war effort.** The Banking Clan provides credit and capital to the Confederacy throughout the war. This is canonical — the war is partially a banker's war from the CIS side.
- **Member of the Separatist Council.** San Hill represents the Clan on Dooku's executive council.
- **Subjected to Chancellor's oversight (mid-war).** The Republic Senate, prompted by Palpatine, places the IGBC under the Chancellor's direct oversight as a war measure. This canonically gives Palpatine personal control of major sectors of galactic finance — accelerating his consolidation of power.
- **Central to the financial-deregulation arc.** The Republic Financial Reform Bill (mid-war) deregulates banking to allow new credit lines for the Republic war effort. The IGBC benefits even while supporting the CIS — a canonical example of war-profiteering across faction lines.

## Voice and culture (for Director AI rendering)

### Muun register
- **Calculating, polite, transactional.** Muun speech is canonically measured and refined. They use precise vocabulary, particularly around financial concepts. Banking-clan dialogue should read like a financial-meeting professional.
- **Long-view temporal frame.** Muuns are long-lived; they speak with the patience of beings who can wait decades for an investment to mature. The Director AI should let this inflect Muun dialogue — quick decisions and rapid shifts feel undignified to them.
- **Family and clan loyalty.** Beneath the transactional surface, Muuns canonically maintain deep familial bonds. Players who establish trust with one Muun may earn cautious openness with their family.

## Use in `gcw_counterparts_and_lore_expansion_design_v1.md`

Muunilinst is **the era's canonical banking world** for the design. Concrete uses:

- **The Republic financial-investigation arc.** Players investigating the war's funding may trace credit lines back to Muunilinst — and find the discomforting truth that Republic and CIS share a banker.
- **CIS-side player content.** Banking-clan Muun PCs or NPCs provide the financial side of CIS operations — a different operational mode than military or political service.
- **Negotiation and contract content.** Banking-clan deals require extended negotiation; the Director AI may use this for player content involving financial brokering, war-loan negotiations, or refinancing operations.

## Use in `clone_wars_director_lore_pivot_design_v1.md`

The Director AI must:

- **Voice Muuns with calculating refinement.** Don't generic-corporate them; Muun speech has a distinctive cadence.
- **Treat the IGBC's dual role (CIS funder + Republic war-profiteer) as canonical.** This is one of the era's most cynical realities. Players who uncover it find it hard to "side" with anyone purely.
- **Allow the Republic's mid-war Chancellor-oversight of the IGBC as a canonical event.** Players in financial-investigation arcs may find that the Chancellor's office (i.e., Sidious) has unusual knowledge of CIS finances.
- **Treat Muun longevity as a canonical character feature.** Muun NPCs may remember events from decades or centuries before the war.
