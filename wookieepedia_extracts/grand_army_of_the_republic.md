## At a glance

The Grand Army of the Republic (GAR) is the **Republic's military arm during the Clone Wars** — a clone-trooper army bred on Kamino, formally instituted by Senate mandate after the First Battle of Geonosis (22 BBY), and led by Jedi Generals throughout the war. It is the institutional context in which all Republic-side military content takes place. Order 66 transforms it into the early Imperial Military.

For SW_MUSH purposes, the GAR is the **canonical military structure for Republic-side player content** — the table of organization (legions, battalions, companies, platoons, squads), the rank structure (Jedi General → clone Commander → Captain → Sergeant → trooper), and the named units that appear in the canon.

## Origin

- **Conceived:** ~33 BBY by Jedi Master **Sifo-Dyas**, who saw Force visions of a coming galactic war and contracted the Kaminoans to begin clone production. The Jedi Council rejected his proposal; Sifo-Dyas pursued it secretly.
- **Subverted by Sidious / Tyranus:** Dooku (as Tyranus) intercepted the Sifo-Dyas commission, murdered Sifo-Dyas via the Pyke Syndicate, and assumed Sifo-Dyas's identity in dealings with the Kaminoans. Tyranus retooled the **inhibitor chips** that Sidious would later use to execute Order 66. The Kaminoans believed throughout that they were producing an army for the Jedi.
- **Genetic template:** **Jango Fett** (Mandalorian bounty hunter; killed at Geonosis by Mace Windu in 22 BBY). All clones share Jango's DNA. **Boba Fett** is canonically Jango's unaltered clone son.
- **Formal institution:** Established by Senate mandate after the First Battle of Geonosis (22 BBY), at the prompting of **Jar Jar Binks's emergency motion** that granted Palpatine emergency powers. The clones — already produced — were "discovered" by the Republic and formally accepted as the Republic's army.
- **End:** Order 66 transitions the GAR into the Imperial Military. Clone troopers become stormtroopers. Clones' accelerated aging eventually leads the Empire to prefer human conscripts; clone production ends.

## Structure

GAR table of organization (canonical, simplified):

| Echelon | Approximate size | Commander |
|---|---|---|
| **Legion** | ~9,000+ troopers; multiple battalions | Jedi General + senior Clone Commander (e.g., 501st under Anakin/Rex) |
| **Corps** | Several legions | Jedi General + senior Commander (e.g., 7th Sky Corps under Obi-Wan/Cody) |
| **Battalion** | ~500–1,000 troopers | Jedi General or Clone Commander (e.g., 212th Attack Battalion, 187th Battalion, 21st Nova Corps) |
| **Regiment** | Subordinate to battalion | Clone Commander |
| **Company** | ~100 troopers | Clone Captain (e.g., Torrent Company under Rex) |
| **Platoon** | ~30–40 troopers | Clone Lieutenant or Sergeant (e.g., 41st Ranger Platoon, Waxer's platoon) |
| **Squad** | ~5–10 troopers | Clone Sergeant or Captain (e.g., Domino Squad, Wolfpack, Rostu Squad, Bravo Squad) |

Specialty branches:
- **Special Operations Brigade** — clone special forces wing; includes ARC troopers and Republic Commando squads.
- **Tank brigades, engineering battalions, scout regiments, signal corps, medical corps, intelligence.**
- **Coruscant Guard** — Coruscant garrison force; ceremonial and security functions in the capital. Distinctive red markings.
- **Republic Commandos** — elite four-trooper special forces squads (Delta Squad, Omega Squad).
- **Advanced Recon Commandos (ARCs)** — special operations clones, often "loaned" to standard units (Fives, Echo, Colt).

Canonical named legions / battalions worth memorizing for content:
- **501st Legion** (Anakin / Rex) — "Vader's Fist"; blue-and-white markings.
- **212th Attack Battalion** (Obi-Wan / Cody) — orange markings; part of the 7th Sky Corps.
- **104th Battalion** (Plo Koon / Wolffe) — Wolfpack squad embedded; gray markings.
- **187th Battalion** (Mace Windu) — purple markings (matches Mace's lightsaber).
- **41st Elite Corps / 41st Scout Battalion** (Yoda) — green markings; jungle-operations specialty (Kashyyyk).
- **21st Nova Corps** (Ki-Adi-Mundi / Bacara) — Mygeeto deployment; site of Ki-Adi-Mundi's Order 66 death.
- **442nd Siege Battalion** (Plo Koon, Cato Neimoidia) — late-war.
- **Carnivore Battalion** (within 501st), **Execute Battalion** (within 501st), **Rancor Battalion** (Clone ARC Commanders Blitz, Colt, Havoc).
- **Torrent Company** (501st) — Rex's company within the legion.
- **Outer Rim Garrison** (Ima-Gun Di / Captain Keeli) — defended Ryloth.
- **Lightning Squadron** (Commander Ponds) — air/aerospace.
- **Bravo Squadron** (Anakin Skywalker) — air/aerospace.

## Composition (predominantly clones, but not exclusively)

- **Clones:** The vast majority. Bred on Kamino; Jango Fett DNA; conditioned for obedience and aging at accelerated rate (~2× normal). Inhibitor chip embedded for Order 66 compliance.
- **Non-clone soldiers (rare canon):** A full company of **Roonian** soldiers fought in the 212th Attack Battalion at the Battle of Abrion Bridge. **Skelly**, an explosives expert, was the only Republic soldier to survive the Battle of Slag's Pit. **Sefin Garoby** worked with the GAR contingent that crashed on Toshara. These cases are rare but canonical — non-clone humans (and non-humans) did serve. The Director AI may use them as precedent for non-clone player content within GAR-themed campaigns.

## Leisure and morale

- **GAR broadcast:** A hologram-based feed available across the army, playing music throughout deployments. The "VJ" announces specific units engaged in combat to inspire them and dedicates songs to those troops. Useful flavor for Director AI atmospheric content — clone troopers had radio.

## Voice and culture

- **Professional discipline.** The GAR is a real army, not a horde. Officers, NCOs, ranks, salutes, regulations, paperwork.
- **Personal pride in unit.** Each legion has its own culture (501st loyalty to Anakin, 212th respect for Obi-Wan, Wolfpack closeness with Plo Koon). The Director AI should let troopers from different units have visibly different unit cultures.
- **Clone identity emerging.** Despite genetic uniformity, individual clones develop names, nicknames, distinct armor markings, personal voices, even hairstyles. The Director AI should treat clone individuality as canonically permitted and celebrated by some Jedi (Anakin, Plo Koon) and discouraged by others (the Kaminoan management).
- **Jedi-General relationship varies.** The "Jedi General + Clone Commander" command pair is canonical; the warmth of that pairing varies by Jedi. (Anakin/Rex: brotherly. Obi-Wan/Cody: professional with mutual respect. Plo Koon/Wolffe: personal warmth. Mace Windu/Ponds: formal. Ki-Adi-Mundi/Bacara: distant.)

## Use in `weight_of_war_design_v1.md`

The GAR is the **central reference for Republic-side mass-combat content** in the design. Concrete uses:

- **Mass-combat unit scaling.** The CWCG §2 mass-combat ruleset (additive) treats individual battalions as Colossal-scale units. The 501st as a *legion* would be modeled as several stacked units in mass combat. The 21st Nova Corps as a corps would be even larger.
- **Player-character GAR roles.** Players might play:
  - A clone trooper at any rank from Private to Commander.
  - A non-clone GAR soldier (Roonian, human conscript, specialist).
  - A Jedi Knight or Padawan assigned as General/Commander.
  - A non-combat GAR specialist (medic, mechanic, intelligence, signal, supply).
- **Squad-scale player parties.** A four-to-six-trooper squad mirrors canonical units like Delta Squad (Republic Commando), Domino Squad (training cohort), Bad Batch (special forces).
- **Order 66 inflection.** All GAR units except those with chip-removed troopers will execute Order 66 against their Jedi commanders. This is structural to any Republic-side content that crosses the 19 BBY pivot.

## Use in `clone_wars_director_lore_pivot_design_v1.md`

The Director AI must:

- **Voice GAR troopers with unit-appropriate culture.** Don't generic-clone them. A 501st trooper sounds different from a 212th trooper.
- **Treat the inhibitor chip as canon and active.** Players in close contact with troopers may notice subtle behavioral signs of chip influence late in the war.
- **Treat the Sifo-Dyas / Tyranus commission origin as deep lore.** Players who investigate the GAR's true origin will find the dark truth: the army was a Sith trojan horse from the start. Most clones never know. Most Jedi never know. The Director should treat this knowledge as carefully gated.
- **Treat Order 66 as the canonical end of the GAR.** The Republic Era ends when the chips fire. Imperial-Era stormtrooper content is out of project's CW pivot scope.
