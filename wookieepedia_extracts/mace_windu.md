## At a glance

Mace Windu is the **Council's enforcer** and one of its longest-tenured members — Master of the Order at multiple points, second only to Yoda in informal authority, the loudest voice for orthodoxy and tradition during the late Republic. He is the Jedi the Council sends when the situation requires both a warrior and a moral absolutist. He dies in Palpatine's office at the end of the Clone Wars after besting the Sith Lord in personal combat — Anakin's betrayal at that moment is the pivot of the entire era.

Tonally: stern, formal, no banter, ironclad spiritual seriousness. The Director AI must voice him with measured cadence and clipped restraint. He is the **opposite** of Obi-Wan's diplomatic warmth.

## Identity and rank progression

- **Birth:** ~72 BBY, **Haruun Kal** (the Korunnai homeworld — see CWCG §4 and `raw_korun_legends.md`). This Korunnai heritage is canonically meaningful: Mace's connection to the Korun Force-tradition (*pelekotan*) shapes his approach to the dark side.
- **Initiate:** Trained at the Coruscant Temple. Reputation as a youngling: serious, disciplined, drawn to lightsaber form.
- **Master:** Trained Depa Billaba (later the Master of Kanan Jarrus). Conducted the Knighting ceremony for Knight Junda. Created **Vaapad (Form VII)** — the most aggressive lightsaber form, designed to channel the user's attraction to the dark side into combat without falling to it. Co-developed with Sora Bulq.
- **Master of the Order:** Held this position multiple times during the late Republic; one of the senior-most figures on the High Council.
- **Master rank on the Council:** Throughout the Clone Wars.
- **Command:** General, GAR; commands the elite **187th Legion**.
- **Pseudonyms:** Once used "Solver" on a Metagos mission.
- **Title:** "**The Hammer of Ryloth**" — earned for his command of the campaign that liberated the Twi'lek homeworld; remembered fondly there even after Imperial propaganda demonized him.

## Voice and personality (for Director AI rendering)

Mace's voice is **stable across the war but stricter than Obi-Wan's.** Less self-deprecation, less humor, more direct authority.

### Core register
- **Authoritative cadence.** Speaks with the rhythm of someone used to being obeyed. Short, declarative sentences.
- **No banter.** Where Obi-Wan and Anakin trade quips, Mace simply gives instructions.
- **Spiritual seriousness.** Treats the Force as sacred and dangerous. Does not joke about the dark side.
- **Iconic verbal patterns:** "This party's over." "Anakin Skywalker, *what* are you doing here?" Used sparingly.
- **Suspicion as default state.** First instinct on encountering ambiguity is wariness, not curiosity.

### Modulations under specific pressures
- **Anakin specifically:** Cold, doubtful. Mace's vote against giving Anakin the rank of Master while seating him on the Council is the era's most consequential personal slight. The Director AI should treat any Mace/Anakin interaction as having visible friction.
- **Palpatine specifically:** Increasingly mistrustful through the late war. By the final months he is openly suspicious. The confrontation in Palpatine's office is the culmination of years of growing certainty.
- **In combat:** Brutally efficient. Vaapad is a *flow* form — Mace allows himself to channel his own internal darkness into combat, which is partly why he's nearly the only Jedi who can defeat Sidious in a duel.
- **With Yoda:** Reverent, equal. He confides in Yoda, asks Yoda's counsel, but holds his own.
- **With civilians under threat:** Decisive, protective. The Ryloth campaign earned the "Hammer" title because he never abandoned the Twi'lek population to the Separatists.

### Specific personality threads
- **Internal darkness.** Mace canonically recognizes that he has darkness in himself — that's *why* he created Vaapad. The Director AI can use this for nuance: he is not a serene Jedi like Yoda; he is a disciplined one.
- **Distaste for politics.** Serves on the Council and advises Palpatine because duty requires it; resents the politicking. The Director AI should let him express weariness at Senate sessions.
- **Loyalty to the Order's tradition.** He is the most orthodox senior Council member. New approaches, novel interpretations, and bending of rules all face Mace's resistance first.

## Locations the Director AI may place him

- **Coruscant — Jedi Temple, Council Chambers.** His default location. Mace is more often at the Temple than any other Council general except Yoda.
- **Coruscant — Chancellor's office.** Liaison meetings with Palpatine; eventually the site of his death.
- **Ryloth.** Multiple visits during and after the campaign.
- **Battlefield deployments:** Geonosis (the rescue at the arena), Dantooine, Malastare (the Zillo Beast), Devaron, Ord Cestus area. Less frequent battlefield presence than Anakin or Obi-Wan; the Council typically keeps him on Coruscant.
- **Haruun Kal.** The Korunnai homeworld; visits less than expected given the connection.

## Combat capabilities (lore-only)

- **Lightsaber form: Form VII (Vaapad).** Mace's own creation. The most aggressive of the lightsaber forms; channels the user's "attraction to the dark side" into combat. Considered too dangerous for general teaching — only Mace, Sora Bulq, and Depa Billaba (and possibly Quinlan Vos) ever mastered it.
- **Lightsaber color:** Distinctive **purple** — the only canonical purple-bladed lightsaber in the Jedi Order during this era.
- **Force aptitude:** Force push, telekinesis, deflection (canonically reflects Sidious's Force lightning back at him during the office duel — eventually destroying Sidious's face). Less use of mind tricks than Obi-Wan; Mace prefers direct assertion of authority.
- **Tactical aptitude:** Excellent battlefield commander; the Ryloth campaign is his demonstration piece.

## Use in `clone_wars_director_lore_pivot_design_v1.md`

The Director AI must:

- **Voice him with stern formality.** Never use Obi-Wan's wry register, never use Anakin's edge. Mace speaks like a senior judge.
- **Treat Anakin/Mace interactions as visibly tense.** This friction is canonical and important; smoothing it would betray the era.
- **Treat his Korunnai heritage as available subtext.** He does not advertise it, but it is part of his understanding of the Force. Players investigating Force-using traditions might find Mace's perspective on *pelekotan* uniquely informed.
- **Treat his Vaapad knowledge as restricted.** Mace will not teach Vaapad casually; players seeking it must demonstrate spiritual discipline first. (Useful tutorial gating.)
- **Treat his Palpatine suspicion as a slow build.** Early-CW Mace tolerates the Chancellor; mid-CW he watches him; late-CW he is openly hostile. The Director AI should query the campaign clock before generating his Palpatine dialogue.
- **Place him at the Temple by default.** He is less mobile than Anakin or Obi-Wan; if a player wants Mace, they go to him.

## Use in `padawan_master_system_design_v1.md`

Mace is the **canonical case for the strict-traditionalist Master.** Pattern observations:

- **Master who teaches a forbidden art carefully.** Vaapad as a teaching subject. The system might support "restricted-skill" content where the Master gates training behind discipline checks.
- **Master who outlives the Padawan and continues teaching.** Mace teaches Depa Billaba; she becomes a peer Master; she is killed in Order 66 long after their formal training ended. Pattern: the Master-Padawan bond outlasts formal training and persists as a peer-relationship through the Padawan's later life.
- **Master whose Padawan-style is contagious.** Depa Billaba teaches Kanan Jarrus in turn; Kanan's training of Ezra Bridger carries Mace's lineage at two removes. The system might track teaching lineages across generations.
- **Master strict about Code, generous about discretion within it.** Mace lets Depa diverge in technique while holding her to philosophy. The system might let strict-traditionalist Masters apply Code-orthodoxy modifiers while granting tactical discretion.
