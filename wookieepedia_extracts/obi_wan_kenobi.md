## At a glance

Obi-Wan Kenobi is the **Jedi Council's most trusted general** — Knight, then Master, then High Council member, designated commander of the 212th Attack Battalion, designated handler of the Anakin problem, designated diplomatic envoy when negotiation is supposed to outweigh combat. He is the canonical "**Negotiator**" — the Jedi the Republic dispatches when they want a problem closed without a battlefield.

Tonally, he is the steady hand. The Director AI must voice him with a restrained, dry wit; classically formal patterns of speech; iconic minor verbal tics ("Hello there," "I have a bad feeling about this"); and a near-bottomless patience that snaps only under specific pressures (Anakin's recklessness, threats to Satine Kryze, the appearance of Maul). He is the Jedi who wears the war the most visibly — by 19 BBY he looks fifteen years older than he should.

## Identity and rank progression

- **Birth:** ~57 BBY, Stewjon. Found Force-sensitive young, taken to the Jedi Temple on Coruscant.
- **Initiate:** Trained under Yoda. Reputation as a youngling: rule-breaker, rebellious. (This is canonically reversed by his Padawan years — by Knighthood, he is the rules-respecter.)
- **Padawan:** Apprenticed to **Qui-Gon Jinn** at age 13. Master and apprentice struggled to develop a working rapport; Kenobi's growing respect for Council orthodoxy clashed with Jinn's "trust the living Force in the moment" approach. Mace Windu and Depa Billaba consulted on the friction; nothing resolved.
- **Pre-Knight assignments:** Year-long protection mission on **Mandalore** (~41–39 BBY) protecting Duchess Satine Kryze from insurgents. They lived as fugitives. Forged a deep mutual attachment; she called him "Ben." This is **Obi-Wan's analogue of Anakin's secret marriage** — a forbidden Jedi attachment he never fully released.
- **Knighthood:** Conferred shortly after Qui-Gon's death on Naboo (32 BBY). Took Anakin Skywalker as Padawan over the Council's reservations to honor Qui-Gon's dying wish.
- **Master:** Conferred when Anakin was Knighted (~22 BBY).
- **Council seat:** Holds it through the Clone Wars. Trusted as a senior tactical voice on the High Council despite being one of the younger members.
- **Command:** General, Grand Army of the Republic. Commands the **212th Attack Battalion** (Commander Cody) and his flagship Star Destroyer the *Negotiator*.

## Voice and personality (for Director AI rendering)

Obi-Wan's voice is the **most stable** of the major CW figures. He does not shift register dramatically across the war the way Anakin or Ahsoka do. The Director AI can use a single core voice with situational modulations.

### Core register
- **Diplomatically patient.** First instinct in any conflict is to negotiate, defuse, redirect. The "Negotiator" nickname is earned and accurate.
- **Dry wit.** Humor is restrained, often understated, frequently at his own expense. Banter with Anakin is constant but never cruel.
- **Classically formal speech.** Slightly archaic constructions, rounded vowels, full sentences. The Director AI should never make him use clipped speech except under direct combat pressure.
- **Self-deprecating about his lot.** "Why do I get the feeling that you're going to be the death of me?" "Why do I always end up doing this?" — pattern: he points out the absurdity of his situation rather than complaining.
- **Iconic verbal tics:** "Hello there." "I have a bad feeling about this." "Oh, hello." Used sparingly as flavor, not constantly.

### Modulations under specific pressures
- **Anakin's recklessness:** wry exasperation. ("Anakin, may I remind you...") Rarely outright anger.
- **Threats to Satine:** drops the diplomatic register; the only canonical situation where he becomes openly emotional and willing to defy Council orders.
- **Maul:** cold, focused. Maul killed Qui-Gon; Obi-Wan does not banter with him.
- **Council politics he disagrees with:** principled dissent in private, conformity in public. The Wrong Jedi arc — he tries to convince the Council to side with Ahsoka, fails, then participates in stripping her rank.
- **Combat:** restrained. Uses Form III (Soresu — the most defensive lightsaber form) and lets opponents tire themselves out. Comments on the fight as it unfolds.
- **With Padmé:** professional, warm. He knows about Anakin's marriage but never says so explicitly.

## Notable Clone Wars story patterns (for `cw_tutorial_chains_design`)

Obi-Wan-led arcs and roles that template content:

1. **Negotiation under fire.** The recurring archetype — diplomatic mission goes wrong; Jedi must fight their way out without abandoning the diplomatic objective. Pattern: high-stakes social-mechanic content where combat is the failure state.
2. **Capture-and-escape.** Kenobi is captured more than any other CW Jedi (Florrum by Hondo, Caliban by Grievous, *Negotiator* hangar duel, Geonosis arena). Pattern: short solo arcs where Jedi must exfiltrate without resources.
3. **Investigation through underworld contacts.** The Dex's Diner pattern — Jedi turns to a non-Jedi friend for information the Order can't provide. Pattern: low-stakes social investigation that depends on relationships, not authority.
4. **Mentor-by-mediation.** Obi-Wan repeatedly mediates between Anakin and the Council, between Anakin and Ahsoka, between Yoda and Anakin. Pattern: triangular content where the player is mediated *for*.
5. **Hunt-the-uncatchable-villain.** Grievous and Maul both run from him for years. Pattern: long-arc antagonist content where defeat is deferred and recurrence is the point.
6. **Defensive duel.** Soresu form V, "let him exhaust himself" tactics. Pattern: combat encounter where parrying and reading the opponent matters more than offense.

## Combat capabilities (lore-only)

For atmospheric description only. Generate D6 mechanics from WEG sources for stat blocks.

- **Lightsaber form:** **Form III (Soresu)** — the supreme defensive form. Recognized as the Order's foremost Soresu practitioner of the era. Briefly demonstrates **Form IV (Ataru)** — his Master's signature form — when fighting Maul and Savage Opress on Florrum, but reverts to Soresu afterward.
- **Force aptitude:** Mind tricks (used regularly, sometimes openly), Force jump, telekinesis, Force healing (limited), strong precognition. Notable for *not* being a flashy practitioner — his Force use is workmanlike.
- **Tactical aptitude:** Best strategic mind on the Council during the CW. Often consulted on fleet-level planning. Less interested in personal command than in the campaign.
- **Pilot:** Famously dislikes flying. ("I hate flying.") Does it competently when forced to.
- **Diplomat:** The "Negotiator" title is mechanical, not just rhetorical — he closes more conflicts via talk than combat across the war.

## Use in `clone_wars_director_lore_pivot_design_v1.md`

The Director AI must:

- **Default him to the Council and Coruscant.** Unlike Anakin (battlefield) or Ahsoka (mobile), Obi-Wan's home base is the Temple. Place him there unless the campaign clock has him on a known deployment.
- **Use his voice as the era's anchor.** He is the most consistent character. Other figures shift dramatically; he holds steady. The Director AI can use Obi-Wan dialogue as a tonal calibration point for the rest of the era.
- **Treat his diplomatic instinct as a default.** When the player has options, Obi-Wan's recommendation should bend toward negotiation, then toward defensive combat, then toward offense. He is the Jedi most likely to suggest "Wait — let's try talking first."
- **Treat Satine references as a fault line.** In any scene where Satine is mentioned by another character (not by him), the Director should let his guard slip briefly. He never volunteers her name.
- **Treat the Anakin relationship as the era's central friendship.** Obi-Wan is the only character whose voice with Anakin should always carry warmth, even during disagreements. By contrast, Mace Windu/Anakin should always carry friction.
- **Allow him at Dex's Diner.** It's the only CoCo Town location where he's canonically seen relaxed.

## Use in `padawan_master_system_design_v1.md`

Obi-Wan is the **canonical case for the Master who outlives the Order**. Unlike Anakin (who falls) or Yoda (who exiles himself), Obi-Wan keeps the Master role intact — he loses Anakin but trains Anakin's son in turn, twenty years later. The system should support:

- **The "Master who took a difficult Padawan over Council objections" pattern.** Obi-Wan accepted Anakin against Council reservations; carried responsibility for the outcome; was vindicated by Anakin's powers and ultimately failed by Anakin's fall. The system might let players take Padawans the Council disapproves of, with associated narrative cost and reward.
- **The "Master mediates between Padawan and the Order" pattern.** Obi-Wan is constantly explaining Anakin to the Council and the Council to Anakin. The system should support Master PCs spending narrative resources on this kind of brokering.
- **The "Master who took a Padawan to honor a vow" pattern.** Obi-Wan's bond with Qui-Gon obligated him to train Anakin. The system should support inherited training obligations as a story hook.
- **The "Master whose Padawan becomes a Knight, then a peer" transition.** Obi-Wan's relationship with Anakin shifts from mentor to brother-in-arms. The system should mechanize this transition rather than treating Knighthood as the end of the bond.

## Use in `cw_tutorial_chains_design_v1.md`

Obi-Wan is an excellent **opening tutor** for any Jedi-class player character. His patient, classical instruction style suits introductory content far better than Anakin's or Mace Windu's. Tutorial chain candidates:

- **"The Negotiator's Apprentice"** — short chain where the player accompanies Obi-Wan on a diplomatic mission that goes wrong; player learns negotiation mechanics, defensive combat, and the importance of restraint.
- **"To Honor a Master"** — chain where Obi-Wan asks the player to take on a difficult Padawan or apprentice (echoes the Anakin obligation); introduces the Padawan-Master system mechanics.
- **"Dex Knows Someone"** — short chain set at Dex's Diner; introduces underworld contact mechanics and CoCo Town locations.
